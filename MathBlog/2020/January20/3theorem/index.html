Attempt to prove





Let $\L$ be a functional on $C^\infty (M)$ such that for every $g$ there exists a map $y^a=y^a(g)$ such that for every $h=h(y)$ $$ \L(g+\vare h)-\L(g)=\vare h(y^a_g(x))\,. \tag{1} $$ Consider a function $$ S(x^i,q_a)=\L(y^aq_a) \tag{2} $$ The hypothesis is that $$ \L=\Phi_S^* \tag{2a} $$

Note first of all that the relation (2a) evidently holds for linear functions: Indeed if $g=A_ay^a$ then $$ \Phi_S^*(g)=A_ay^a+S(x,q)-q_ay^a\,,\hbox{with}\,\, q_a={\p g\over \p y^a}=A_a\,, $$ hence relation (2a) for linear functions $g=A_ay^a$ $$ \hbox{if $g=A_ay^a$ then}\quad \Phi_S^*(g)=S(x,A_a)=\L(g)\,. \tag{2b} $$

Now check the relation (2a) in the infinitesimal vicinity of linear functions

Let $h=h(y)$ be an arbitrary function, and $g=A_ay^a$ be a linear function, then there exists a function $y^i_g(x)$ such that $$ \L(g+\vare h)-L(g)=\vare h(y^a_g(x)) \tag{3} $$ This holds for an arbitrary function $h$. Consider a special case if $h$ is the linear function also. Suppose that $h=b_aY^a$ then it follows from `(2b) that $$ \L(g+\vare h)-\L(g)= \L(A_ay^a+\vare b_ay^a)-\L(A_ay^a)= $$ $$ S(x,A_a+\vare b_a)-S(x,A_a)= {\p S(x,q)\over \p q_a}\big\vert_{q=A_a} = \vare h(y^a_g(x))\,. \tag{3b} $$ Comparing relations (3) and (3b) we come to conclusion that $$ y^a_g(x)= {\p S(x,q)\over \p q_a}\big\vert_{q=A_a}\,, $$ i.e. $$ \L(g+\vare h)=\Phi_S^*(g+\vare h) $$ in the case if $g$ is linear and $h$ is an arbitrary.
