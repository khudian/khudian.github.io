Let $\L(g)=\Phi_S^*(g)$ be a functional corresponding to a thick morphism. Then this functional defines a map $y^a_g(x)$: $M\to N$ depending on the function $g$: $$ y^a_g(x)\colon y^a_i(x)={\p S(x,q)\over \p q_a} \big\vert_{q_a={\p g(y)\over \p y^a}} \tag{1} $$ such that this map defines the differentail of the pull-back defined by the thick morphism: $$ \hbox {for arbitrary functions $g(y)$ and $h(y)$}\,,\quad \L(g+\vare h)-\L(g)= \Phi^*_S(g+h) -\Phi^*_S(g) \vare h\left(y^a_g(x)\right) \tag{2} $$ The inverse implication is also true:

Propsition Let $\L=\L(g)$ be a functional whcih transforms functions on $N$ to functions on $M$.

Let $y^a=y^a(x)_g$ be a usual map $M\to N$ depending on a function $g$ such that the differential of the map $\Phi_S^*$ at the 'point' $g$ is defined by this function: $$ \L(g+\vare h)-\L(g)=\vare h(y_g^a(x))\,. \tag{3} $$ then a map $y^a=y^a(x)_g$ depend on derivatives of function $g$, i.e. for arbotrary constant $C$, $$ y^a(x)_g= y^a(x)_{g+C}\,. \tag{4} $$

Proof. Let $$ \L(g)=L_0(x)+\sum_n\int L(x,y_1\dots, y_n) g(y_1) \dots g(y_n) dy_1\dots dy_n \tag{5} $$ and $$ y^a_g(x)=K_0^a(x)+ \sum_n\int K^a(x,y_1,\dots y_n)g(y_1)\dots g(y_n) dy_1\dots dy_n \tag{6} $$,

On the right hand side all $L_n=L_n(x,z_1,z_2,\dots z_n)$ are symmetrical over $z_1,z_2,\dots z_n$ $$ L_n(x,y,z_1,\dots, z_n)= L_n(x, z_1,\dots,z_j,y,z_{j+1},\dots, z_{n-1})= \dots= L_n(x,z_1,\dots,z_{n-1},y) $$ (being consisdered as generalised functions) whence on the left had side the dependence on $h$ has to be the same as dependance on $g$. However we see that left had side depends on derivatives of $h$.
