Let $\L(g)$ be a functional which transforms a space of functions on $N=\R^n$ to a space of functions on $M=\R^m$: $$ \L(g)= \sum_{r\geq 0}\int L_r(x,y_1,\dots,y_r) g(y_1)\dots)g(y_r)dy_1\dots dy_r= $$ $$ L_0(x)+ \int L_1(x,y)g(y)dy+ \int L_2(x,y_1,y_2)g(y_1)g(y_2)dy_1dy_2+ $$ $$ \int L_3(x,y_1,y_2,y_3)g(y_1)g(y_2)g(y_3)dy_1dy_2dy_3+\dots \tag{1} $$ $y^a$ are coordinates on $N$ and $x^i$ are coordinates on $M$. (All kernels $L(x,y_1,\dots,y_k)$ are generalised functions.)






Two words about functionals.

We say that a functional $\L(g): C^\infty(N)\to C^\infty(M)$ is a local functional, if there exists

a map $\varphi\colon M\to N$ such that $$ \L(g)=\varphi^* g=g\left(\varphi\left(x\right)\right) \tag{2} $$



An arbitrary functional $\L(g)$ defines a differential $d\L$ such that at every 'point' a function $g$, and for every function $h(y)$ $$ \vare d\L(h)=\L(g+\vare h)-\L(g)\,. \tag{3} $$

Consider functionals such that theur differential is a local functional, i.e. for every function $g=g(y)$ there exists a map $y^a_g(x):M\to N$ such that $$ \L(g+\vare h)-\L(g)= \vare h(y)\big\vert_{y^a=y^a_g(x)} \tag{4} $$ for arbitrary function $h(y)$

Every thick morphisms defined by an "action" $S(x,q)$ defines a functional $$ \L(g)=\Phi_S(g)\,. \tag{5} $$ THEOREM A differential of functional is local if and only if is defined by a thick morphism. The fact that a differentail of 'pull-back' induced by thick morphism was proved. Here we will try to prove that if a differential of functional $\L(g)$ is local, then there exists a function $S$ such that $$ \L(g)=\Phi_S(g) \tag{6} $$
