Parallel transport

Let $M$ be $k$-dimenisonal manifold in Euclidean space $\E^n$. Let $\gamma$ be a curve on $M$. Then there are three ways to define the parallel transport of a vector along this curve:







a) take metric $G$ induced on $M$ by Euclidean metric, then take parallel transport with this Levi-Civita connection induced on $M$

b) take the connection induced on $M$ by standard connection in Euclidean space: $$ \nabla^{\rm induced}_\X\Y= \nabla^{\rm Euclidean}_\X\Y\,. $$

c) We say that $\X(t)$ is parallel transport if the vector field $d \X(t)\over dt$ is orthogonal to the surface.

Theorem. These three ways coincide.

The induced connection preserves metric. Hence the uniqueness implies that $b)=a)$

Show that c)=a) by straightforward calculations.

Let $x^i=x^i(\xi^\a)$; consider vectors $$ \r_\a={\p x^i(\xi)\over \p \xi^\a}{\p \over \p x^i}= x^i_\a\e_i\,. $$ Consider an arbitrary curve on $M$ $$ \gamma\colon \r(t)=\r\left(\xi(t)\right)\,. $$

Consider the parallel transport $\Y(t)=u^\a(t)\r_\a(\xi(t))$ of vector field along this curve.

We have that for arbitrary $t$, $$ \nabla_{\X(t)}\ր ւt)=0\,, $$

where $\Y(t)$ is the vector tangent to the curve $\gamma$: $$ \Y(t)=x^\a(t)\r_\a(t)\,,\quad {\rm with}\,\, x^\a(t)={d\xi^\a(t)\over dt}\,. $$
