Example of Fadeev-Popov functional

In non-invariant verrsion of Fadeev-Popov trick essential role is played by the functional $$ W_F(x)=\left[\int \delta\left(F(x^g)\right)dg\right]^{-1} \,, $$ where compact group $G$ acts on manifold $E$, and surface $F=0$ is transversal to the 'action' of the group.

Remark One can see that this functional as well as the functional $W_F(x)$ are not geometricsal.



One can see that the functional $W_F(x)$ is $G$-invariant; If $f$ is $G$-invariant function on $E$, and $d\mu$ is also $G$-invariant measure on $E$: $$ W_F(x^g)=W_F(x)\,,\quad f(x^g)=f(x)\,,\quad \int_{\triangle} d\mu= \int_{\triangle^g} d\mu\,, $$ then for every 'gauge condition' defined by $F=0$, we have that $$ \int f(x)d\mu=\int f(x) \left( \underbrace{W_F(x)\int \delta\left(F(x^g)\right)dg}_ {\rm unity} \right)d\mu= \int f(y) W_F(y) \delta\left(F(y)\right)dg d\mu $$
