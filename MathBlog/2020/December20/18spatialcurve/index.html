Spatial curve ---recalling.



Let $I\mapsto \E^n$ be a curve in $\E^n$, Choose $s$--natural parameter. Then $\v=\dot\r(s)$ is a unit vector and curvature is equal to $k(s)=\left|{d^2\r(s)\over ds^2}\right|$.



Exercise In arbitrary parameterisation for curve in $\E^2$ $$ k={x_{tt}y_t-x_ty_{tt}\over \left(x_t^2+y_t^2\right)^{3\over 2}}\,. $$

For a curve in $\E^2$ one can consider also $k_{\rm algebraic}$, i.e. curvature with a sign: $$ k_{\rm alg}=\cases { k\,\, \hbox {if}\left\{\v(s), {d\v(s)\over ds}\right\} \hbox {form left-oriented basis}\cr -k\,\, \hbox {if}\left\{\v(s), {d\v(s)\over ds}\right\} \hbox {form right-oriented basis}\cr }\,. $$

For the curve $\r(s)$, where $s$ is a natural parameter, one can consider, the normal $$ \n(s)={1\over k(s)}{d\over ds}\v(s) $$ (we suppose that $k\not=0$) One can see that for the plane curve $$ {d\over ds} \pmatrix {\v\cr \n\cr}= \pmatrix {0 &k\cr -k & 0} \pmatrix {\v\cr \n\cr} \qquad:\hbox {(Frenet formula)} $$





${\S} 2$ Spatial curves





Now return to curve in $\E^n$ for arbitrary $n$. If curve is not 'spriamliaemaya' then the normal $\n(s)$ is defined: $\n(s)={1\over k(s)}{d\over ds}\v(s) $.

Consider the special case of $n=3$.

Consider vector $$ {\bf b}=\v\times \n \,,\quad (\hbox{vector of binormal}) $$ One can see that $$ {d\over ds} \pmatrix {\v\cr \n\cr {\bf b}}= \pmatrix { 0 & k &0\cr -k & 0 &\kappa\cr 0 & -\kappa &0\cr } \pmatrix {\v\cr \n\cr {\bf b}}\,,\qquad (\hbox {Frenet formula for spatial curves}) $$

$\kappa$ is called the torsion of the curve
