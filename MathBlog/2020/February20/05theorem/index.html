Statement

Let $\L=\L(x,g)$ be a functional $$ \L(g)=L_0(x)+L_1(x,g)+ L_2(x,g,g)+ L_3(x,g,g,g)+ \dots= $$ $$ L_0(x)+ \int L_1(x,y)g(y)dy+ $$ $$ \int L_2(x,y,z)g(y)g(z)dydz+ \int L_3(x,y,z,w)g(y)g(z)g(w)dydzdw+\dots \tag{1a} $$ such that its differential is local: $$ \L(g+\vare h)-\L(g)=\vare \left[L_1(h)+2L_2(h,g)+3L_3(h,g,g)+\dots \right]= $$ $$ \vare \left[ \int L_1(x,y)h(y)dy+ 2\int L_2(x,y,z)h(y)g(z)dydz+ 3\int L_3(x,y,z,w)h(y)g(z)g(w)dydzdw+ \dots \right] $$ $$ =\vare h\left[ K_g^a(x) \right]\,, $$ where $$ K_g^a(x)= K_0^a(x)+K_1^a(x,g)+K_2^a(x,g,g,)+\dots = $$ $$ K_0^a(x)+\int K_1^a(x,y)g(y)dy+ \int K_2^a(x,y,z)g(y)g(z)dydz+\dots \tag{1b} $$



Theorem

Let $S=S(x,q)$ be a function such that $$ S(x,q)=S_0(x)+S^a(x)q_a+S^{ab}(x)q_aq_b\,, $$

where $$ S_0(x)=\L(x,g)\big\vert_{g=0}=L_0(x)\,, \tag{2a} $$ $$ S^a(x)=\L(x, g)\big\vert_{g=y^a}- \L(x,g')\big\vert_{g'=0}= L_1(x,g)\big\vert_{g=y^a}\,, \tag{2b} $$ and $S^{ab}(x)=S^{ba}(x)$ is symmetrical tensor such that $$ S^{ab}(x)= L_2(x,g=y^a,g=y^b)= $$ $$ \int L_2(x,y,z)t_ay^at_bz^bdydz\,. \tag{2c} $$

Then the thick morphism generated by $S(x,q)$ induces a map which differs on $\L(g)$ in terms higher than 2: $$ \L(g)- \Phi_{S}^*(g)=O(g\cdot g\cdot g)\,. \tag{3} $$

$$ $$

PROOF

It follows from (1) that $L_1(x,h)= h\left(K^a(x)\right)$. Locality condition (1) holds for arbitrary smooth functions $h$ and $g$, hence for arbitrary functions $g$ $$ L_1(x,g)=\int L_1(x,y)g(y)dy= g\left(K^a(x)\right)\,\quad,i.e. L_1(x,y)={\p \delta \left( y^a- K^a\left( x \right) \right) \over \p y^a } \tag{3} $$ The same arguments lead to the fact that $$ 2L_2(x,h,g)= 2\int L_2(x,y,z)h(y)g(z)dydz= h \left( K^a_0(x)+ K^a_1\left( x,g \right) +\dots \right) - h \left( K^a_0(x) \right) = $$ $$ {\p h(y)\over\p y^a} \big\vert_{y^a=K_0^a(x)} K^a_1\left( x,g \right) = {\p h(y)\over\p y^a} \big\vert_{y^a=K_0^a(x)} K^a_1\left( x,y \right) g(y)dy\,. \tag{4} $$ Notice that for the case if $h=y^a$ then this equation implies that $$ L_2(x,h,g)\big\vert_{h=y^a}={1\over 2}K^a_1(x,g)\,. \tag{5} $$ Notice also very important fact that this is symmetrical: $$ L_2(x,h,g)=L_2(x,g,h)\,, \tag{6} $$ Thus apply identity (6) for the case if $h(y)=y^a$ ($g=g(y)$ remains an arbitrary smooth function). Using (6) and using definition (2c) of $S^{ab}(x)$ we come to $$ L_2(x,y^a,g)= {1\over 2}K_1^a(x,g)= L_2(x,g,y^a)= {1\over 2}{\p g(y)\over \p y^c}\big\vert_{y^c=K^c_0(x)} K_1^c(x,y^a)= $$ $$ l_c(x)\cdot L_2(x,y^c,y^a)= l_c(x)S^{ca}(x)\,, \hbox {where}\,\, l_c(x)= {\p g(y)\over \p y^c} \big\vert_{y^c=K^c_0(x)}\,. \tag{7} $$ We almost finished. It follows from this equation that $$ K_1^a(x,g)= 2l_c(x)S^{ca}(x)\,, \quad\left( l_c(x)= {\p g(y)\over \p y^c} \big\vert_{y^c=K^c_0(x)} \right)\,. \tag{8a} $$ Hence $$ L_2(x,h,g)= {1\over 2}{\p h(y)\over \p y^a} \big\vert_{y^a=K^a_0(x)} K_1^a(x,g)= {\p h(y)\over \p y^a} \big\vert_{y^a=K^a_0(x)} S^{ac}(x)l_c(x)\,, \tag{8b} $$

Using this equation and (3) we have that for our functional $\L(x,g)$ $$ \L(x,g)=L_0(x)+L_1(x,g)+L_2(x,g,g) +O(g\cdot g\cdot g)= $$ $$ L_0(x)+g\left(K_0^a(x)\right)+ l_a(x) S^{ac}(x)l_c(x) +O(g\cdot g\cdot g)= $$ $$ L_0(x)+g\left(K_0^a(x)+S^{ab}l_b(x)\right)+ O(g\cdot g\cdot g)\,. $$ We see that this map coincides up to the third order with the pull-back of thick morphism generated by the 'action' $$ S(x,q)=L_0(x)+K_0^a(x)+S^{ab}(x)q_bq_a $$ with $$ K_0^a(x)=L_1(x,y^a)\,,\quad S^{ab}(x)=L_2()x,y^a,y^ba\,. $$
