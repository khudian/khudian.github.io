The proof of the Theorem?



Denote by $A$ the class of functionals which

a) assign to every function $g(y)\in C^\infty(\R^N)$ a function $f(x)\in C^\infty(\R^M)$

b) have a lcoal differential, i,e. for every function $g$ $$ d\L_g(h)= {\L(g+\vare h)-\L(g)\over \vare }= h\left(y^a_g(x)\right),\qquad (\vare^2=0\,.) \tag{1} $$ We suppose that all objects have an expansion: $$ \L(g)=l_0(x)+L_1(x,g)+L_2(x,g,g)+L_3(x,g,g,g)+\dots= $$ $$ L_0(x)+ \int L_1(x,y)g(y)dy \int L_2(x,y,z)g(y)g(z)dydz+ \int L_3(x,y,z,w)g(y)g(z)g(w)dydzdw+\dots\,, $$ where $$ L_1(x,g)=\int L_1(x,y)g(y)dy=g(S_1^a(x))\,, $$ $$ L_2(x,g,g)=\int L_2(x,y,z)g(y)g(z)dydz= S^{ab}(x)g^\dagger_a(x)g^\dagger_b(x)\,, \tag{2} $$ and so on.... and in the same way $$ y^a_g(x)= K^a_0(x)+ K^a_1(x,g)+ K^a_2(x,g)+\dots= K^a_0(x)+ \int K_1(x,y)g(y)dy+ \int K_2(x,y,z)g(y)g(z)dydz+\dots \tag{3} $$

we denote by $A_k$ the subspace of functionals in $A$ which have weight $k$ $$ A_k= \{\L\colon \,\, L_0\equiv L_1\equiv \dots\equiv L_{k-1}=0\} \tag{4} $$ Very important lemma

Lemma Let $\L$ and $\tilde \L$ be two functionals such that their difference has weight $k\geq 1$ :$\tilde \L-\L\in A_k$. Then $$ \tilde \L(g)-\L(g)-S_k(\p g)\in A_{k+1}\,, \tag{5} $$ where $$ S_k(\p g)= S_k^{a_1\dots a_k}(x) g_{a_1}^\dagger(x) \dots g_{a_k}^\dagger(x)\,,\quad g_a^\dagger(x)={\p g(y)\over \p y^a} \big\vert_{y^a=K_0^a(x)} \tag{6} $$ and $$ S_k^{a_1\dots a_k}= \tilde L\left(x,y^{a_1},\dots, y^{a_1}\right) - L\left(x,y^{a_1},\dots, y^{a_1}\right)\,. \tag{7} $$ and $K_0^a(x)$ is a function on $x$.

Remark If $k=1$ then $S_1(x)=K_0(x)$.

Now we show that all elements in $A$ are defined by thick morphisms.

Let $\L\in A$. We define "action function" $$ S_0(x)=L_0(x)\,, S_1^a(x)=L_1(x,y^a)\,, S_2^{ab}(x)=L_2(x,y^a,y^b)\,, S_3^{abc}(x)=L_3(x,y^a,y^b,y^c)\,, \tag{8} $$ and so on and consider thick mporphisms $\Phi_k$ generated by functions $S_{\leq k}=S_0(x)+S_1(x,q)+S_2(x,q)+\dots+S_k(x,q)$, The inductive statement is

$$ P_k\colon \L-\Phi_k^*\in A_{k+1} \tag{9} $$ ($\Phi_0(g)=S_0(x)$, $\Phi_1(g)=g(S_1(x))$) Prove that $P_k$ implies $P_{k+1}$: $$ P_k\colon \L-\Phi_k^*\in A_{k+1}\Rightarrow P_{k+1}\colon \L-\Phi_{k+1}^*\in A_{k+2}\,. \tag{10} $$ It follows from (9) and the lemma that there exist $S_{k+1}(\p g)$ such that $$ \L(g)-\Phi_k^*(g)-S_{k+1}(\p g)\in A_{k+2} $$ Notice that $$ \Phi_{k+1}(g)=\Phi_k(g)+S_{k+1}(g) \hbox{up to terms in $A_{k+2}$} $$ Thus we proved (10).




Now we prove the lemma Proof

Suppose $k>1$. Then differentiate the condition $\tilde \L(g)-\L(g)\in A_k$. $$ \tilde \L(g)-\L(g)= B_k\left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k$ times}} \right)+\hbox{terms in $A_{k+1}$} \tag{L1} $$ Differentiating we come to $$ h\left( \tilde K_0^a\left(x\right)+\dots+ \tilde K_{k-2}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-2$ times}} \right)+ \tilde K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}} \right)+\dots \right) - $$ $$ -h\left( K_0^a\left(x\right)+\dots+ K_{k-2}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-2$ times}} \right)+ K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}} \right)\dots \right)= $$ $$ =kB_k\left( h, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}} \right) + \hbox { terms of order $\geq k-1$ on $g$ } \tag{9} $$ It follows from this equation that all $\tilde K_{r}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$r$ times}} \right)$ are equal to $K_{r}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$r$ times}} \right)$ for $r\leq k-2$ and the difference betweeen $\tilde K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$r$ times}} \right)$ and $K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$r$ times}} \right)$ defines $B$: $$ \tilde K_0^a\left(x\right) = K_0^a\left(x\right)\,, \tilde K_0^a\left(x,g\right) = K_0^a\left(x,g\right)\,,\dots \tilde K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-2$ times}} \right) = K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-2$ times}} \right) \tag{10} $$ $$ kB_k\left(h, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}} \right) = {\p h(y)\over \p y^a}\big\vert_{y^a=K^a_0(x)} \delta K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}} \right) \tag{11} $$ where $$ \delta K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}} \right)= \tilde K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}} \right) - K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}} \right)\, \tag{12}. $$ The statement of lemma follows from symmetricity condition on form $B_k\finish$

Remark it was also proved that $$ \delta K_{k-1}^a \left(x, \underbrace{g,\dots,g}_{\hbox {$k-1$ times}}\right)= kB^{a_1 a_2\dots a_k}g_{a_2}\dots g_{a_k} $$
