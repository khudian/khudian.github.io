Stationary phase method



I am thinking to understand the mystery of the hard part of atationary phase method (see Blog on 16 June) Again and again I return to the fact that the contibution to the integral $$ \int e^{{i\over \hbar} S(x)}dx=\hbox {contribution of positive powers} \tag{1} $$ (see Blog on 16 June)

I decided to do calculations again avoiding Fedoruc identity.

Calculate the integral $$ \int e^{i{c\over 2}A^{ab}k_ak_b+ik_ax^a}d^nk. $$ Notice that $$ {c\over 2}A^{ab}(k_a+l_a)(k_b+l_b)+(k_a+l_a)x^a= {c\over 2}A^{ab}k_ak_b+cA^{ab}k_al_b+ {c\over 2}A^{ab}l_al_b+k_ax^a+l_ax^a\,. $$ Hence if we choose $l_a=-{1\over c}A_{ab}x^b$, then $$ {c\over 2}A^{ab}k'_ak'_b+k'_ax^a= {c\over 2}A^{ab}k_ak_b+{c\over 2}A^{ab}l_al_b+l_ax^a= cA^{ab}k_ak_b-{1\over 2c}A_{ab}x^ax^b\,, $$ where $k'_a=k_a+l_a$,

hence $$ \int e^{icA^{ab}k_ak_b+ik_ax^a}d^nk= e^{-{1\over 2c}A_{ab}x^ax^b} \int e^{cA^{ab}k_ak_b}d^nk= K_M\sqrt {\pi \det A\over c^n} e^{-{1\over 2c}A_{ab}x^ax^b}, $$ where $\det A$ is determinan of the matrix with lower indices, $K_M$ is complex number with modulus $1$ (related with signature....) To apply thia formula to calculate (1), first prove lemma. Let $\varphi(x)$ be an arbitrary function, then $$ e^{ -iA_{ab}x^ax^b\over 2c}\varphi (x)dx= {1\over K_M} \sqrt {c^n\over \pi \det A} \int e^{-i{c\over 2}A^{ab}k_ak_b+ik_ax^a} \varphi(x)dk^n\,, $$ and $$ \int e^{ -iA_{ab}x^ax^b\over 2c}\varphi (x)dx= {1\over K_M} \sqrt {c^n\over \pi \det A} \int e^{-i{c\over 2}A^{ab}k_ak_b+ik_ax^a} \varphi(x)dk^n= $$ $$ {1\over K_M} \sqrt {c^n\over \pi \det A} \int e^{-i{c\over 2}A^{ab}k_ak_b} \underbrace { e^{ik_ax^a} \varphi(x)dx^n dk^n }_ {\tilde \varphi(k) }\,. $$ Since $$ \int f(k)\tilde \varphi (k)dk= \left[\int f(k)e^{ikx}\tilde \varphi (k)dk\right] \big\vert_{x=0}= \int \left[ f\left( {1\over i} {d\over dx} \right) e^{ikx} \tilde \varphi(k) \right]_{x=0}dk $$ Thus we come to

Lemma $$ \int e^{ -iA_{ab}x^ax^b\over 2c}\varphi (x)dx= {1\over K_M} \sqrt {c^n\over \pi \det A} e^{ -i{c\over 2} \left( A^{ik} {\p\over \p x^i} {\p\over \p x^k} \right)} \varphi(x) \big\vert_{x=0} $$

Apply this lemma to (1). Suppose expansion of $S(x)$ in stationary point is $$ S(x)=A_{ab}x^ax^b+S_+(x) $$ (we suppose that it is at $x=0$ where stationary point is). Then due to lemma $$ \int e^{{i\over \hbar} S(x)}dx= K_M {(\pi \hbar)^{n}\over \sqrt {\det A} e^\Delta S_+(x)\,. $$
