Completely integrability

using book of Eisenhart







Consider dif.equation in formal power series $$ {\p \theta^\a\over \p x^i}=\psi^\a_i(x,\theta)\,, \tag{1} $$ where $i=1,\dots,n$, $\a=1,\dots,m$.

It can be rewritten as $$ d\theta ^\a=\psi^\a_idx^i\,. \tag{2} $$

Calculating second derivative, which has to be symmetric we come to equation $$ {\p \psi^\a_i\over \p x^j} + {\p \psi^\a_i\over \p \theta^\beta} \psi^\beta_j= {\p \psi^\a_j\over \p x^i} + {\p \psi^\a_j\over \p \theta^\beta} \psi^\beta_i\,. \tag{3} $$

We say that (1) is completely integrable if equations (3) are identities. In this case we wirte down foral power series $$ \theta^\a= \theta^\a\big\vert_0 + {\p \theta^\a\over \p x^i}\big\vert_0 x^i + {1\over 2}{\p^2 \theta^\a\over \p x^i\p x^j}\big\vert_0 x^ix^j+\dots $$ where $$ \theta^\a\big\vert_0=c^\a\,, {\p \theta^\a\over \p x^i}\big\vert_0 =\psi^\a_i(x,\theta) \big\vert_{x=0\,,\theta^\a=c^\a}\, $$ $$ {\p^2 \theta^\a\over \p x^i\p x^j}\big\vert_{x=0} = \left[ {d\over d x^j}\left(\psi^\a_i\left(x,\theta\right) \right) \right]\big\vert_{x=0,\theta^\a=c^\a}= $$ $$ \left[{\p \psi^\a_i\over \p x^j} + {\p \psi^\a_i\over \p \theta^\beta} \psi^\beta_j \right]_{x=0,\theta^\a=c^\a}\,, $$ and so on.

Solution depends on parameters $n$ parameters $c^\a$. We look solutions in a vicinity of $0$.
