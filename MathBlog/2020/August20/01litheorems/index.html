Let $G$ be $r$-dimensional group $G$. Suppose that it acts on $n$-dimensional manifold $M$: $$ y^i=f^i(a^\a,x^i)\,, \tag{0.1} $$ where $\{a^\a\}$ ($\a=1,\dots,r$) are parameters of $G$, $x^i,y^i$ ($i=1,\dots,N$) coordinates of $M$.

We naturaly suppose that all group parameters $a^\a$ are essential. What is it? (See blogs on 2-nd August, 10 August) It seems naturally to think that parameters are essential in the case if $$ {\rm rank} \left({\p f^i(a^\a,x^i)\over \p a^\beta}\right)=r\,. \tag{0.2} $$ In fact the story is more subtle, (see blogs on 2-nd, 10 and 11.. august) and we take another definition:

The $r$ parameters $a^1,\dots,a^r$ {]it are not} essenti–∞l if $$ {\rm rank} \left({\p f^i(a^\a,x^i)\over \p a^\beta}\right)\leq rr\,. \tag{0.2} $$ (see the blogs 02,05,10,11... on August)

Composition of two transofmration is another transformation: $$ z^i=z^i\left(b,y^j\right) =z^i\left(b,y^j\left(a,x^i\right)\right)= f^i\left(b,f^j\left(a,x^i\right)\right)= f^i\left(\varphi\left(b,a\right),x^i\right)\,. \tag{0.3} $$ Show that parameters $a$ and $b$ in equation (0.3) are also essential: $$ \det {\p \varphi^\a(a,b)\over \p a^\beta}\not=0\,, \tag{0.4a} $$ and $$ \det {\p\varphi^\a(a,b)\over \p b^\beta}\not=0\,, \tag{0.4b} $$ Prove first (0.4b). Suppose condition (4b) fails, then equation (0.2) fails also. Indeed using composition law (0.3) we have that $$ {\p z^i(b,y)\over \p b^\beta}= {\p z^i\left(b,y\left(a,x\right)\right)\over \p b^\beta} = {\p z^i\left(\varphi(b,a),x\right)\over \p b^\beta}= {\p z^i\left(\varphi(b,a),x\right)\over \p \varphi^\a} {\p \varphi^\a(b,a)\over \p b^\beta}\, $$ hence $$ A^\beta_{(b)}(a,b){\p\varphi^\a(a,b)\over \p b^\beta}=0\Rightarrow A^\beta_{(b)}(a,b){\p z^i(b,y)\over \p b^\beta}= A^\beta_{(b)}(a,b) {\p z^i\left(\varphi(b,a),x\right)\over \p \varphi^\a} {\p \varphi^\a(b,a)\over \p b^\beta}=0\,, $$ (LHS of this equation means that condition (4b) fails, and RHS means that equation (0.2) fails.)

Analogously to prove (0.4a) we note that using (0.3) one can consider $z^i(b,y)$ as $$ z^i(b,y)=z^i(b,y(a,x))\Rightarrow {\p z^i(b,y)\over \p a^\beta}= {\p z^i(b,y(a,x))\over \p y^j} {\p y^j(a,x)\over\p a^\beta}\,. $$ Hence if condition (0.4a) fails, then condition (0.2) fails also: $$ A^\beta_{(a)}(a,b){\p\varphi^\a(a,b)\over \p a^\beta} =0\Rightarrow A^\beta_{(a)}(a,b){\p z^i(b,y(a,x))\over \p a^\beta}= A^\beta_{(b)}(a,b) {\p z^i(b,y(a,x))\over \p y^j} {\p y^j(a,x)\over\p a^\beta} =0\,. $$ (LHS of this equation means that condition (4a) fails, and RHS means that equation (0.2) fails.)

Begin with the simple case of one-parametric group: Let one-dimensional group $G$ acts on $\R^n$: $$ y^i=f^i(g,x^i) $$ suppose (WLOG) that coordinate $g=0$ corresponds to unity. Composition of two transofmration is another transformation: $$ z^i=z^i\left(h,y^j\right) =z^i\left(h,y^j\left(g,x^i\right)\right)= f^i\left(h,f^j\left(g,x^i\right)\right)= f^i\left(\varphi\left(h,g\right)x^i\right)\,. $$
