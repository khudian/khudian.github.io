Essential parameters I

using book of Eisenhart



Let $f^i=f^i(a^\a)$, where $i=1,\dots,n$, $\a=1,\dots,r$.

In the blog on 1-st August I wrote that parameters $a^\a$ are essential if: $$ {\rm rank} \left({\p f^i(a^\a,x^i)\over \p a^\beta}\right)=r\,. \tag{0.1} $$ Is it true? Yes, and no. We will explain that condition that rank of the matrix is equal to $r$ implies that parameters are essential, however, if rank of the matrix is less than $r$ this does not mean that parameters are not essential. No, e.g. it will be surprising if somebody will tell that for function $$ f=x+a_1+a_2x^2 \tag{0.2} $$ parameters are not essential....







Definition



Let ${\cal C}=\{f^i(x,a)\}$ be collection of functions on $x$ and $r$ parameters $a^\a$ ($\a=1,\dots,n$).

These parameters may be essential or not essential.

We say that parameters $a^\a$ are not essential for the collection $\cal C$ if there exist $r-1$ functions $$ A_1(a^1,\dots,a^r)\,,\ldots A_{r-1}(a^1,\dots,a^r)\,, \tag{1.1} $$ such that any function $f^i(x,a)$ in this collection, $f\in \cal C$, can be expressedd as a function depending on parameters $A_1,\dots,A_{r-1}$: $$ f^i(x,a_1,\dots,a_r)\equiv F^i(x,A_1,\dots,A_{r-1})\,. \tag{2} $$ What is relation of this statement with (01)?





Proposition All $r$ parameters $a^\a$ are not essential for collection $\cal C$ of functions if and only if there exists a non-vanishing vector field $\R$ on space of parameters such that every function in this collection is not changing along $\R$: $$ \R=\varphi^\s(a){\p\over \p a^\s}\not=0\colon\, \hbox {for every function $f\in \cal C$}\,,\, df(\R)=\varphi^\s(a){\p f(x,a)\over \p a^\s}\equiv 0\,, \tag{3} $$

Remark Condition (3) implies that rank of Jacobian ${\p f\over \p a}$ is less than equal to $r$. There is temptation to say that

$$ \hbox{.... parameters are not essential if ${\rm rank}\, {\p f\over \p a}$ however} \tag{3a} $$ a) these conditions ARE NOT Ðµquivalent ((3) implies (3a), however (3a) does not imply (3).

b) Counterexample : for two parametric family of maps $$ f(x,a,b)=x+a+bx^2 $$ parameters $a,b$ are essential, in spite of the fact that condition (3a) is fullfilled.



Proof of Proposition.

Suppose that all parameters $a^\a$ are not essential, i.e. equation (2) is obeyed: you can find $r-1$ functions (2a) such that equation (2b) holds. Consider $(r-1)\times r$ matrix $$ \pmatrix { &{\p A_1(a)\over \p a^1}\dots &{\p A_1(a)\over \p a^r}\cr &\dots \dots &\dots \cr &{\p A_{r-1}(a)\over \p a^1}\dots &{\p A_{r-1}(a)\over \p a^r}\cr } $$ Rank of this matrix is $\leq r-1$, hence rows of this matrix are linearly dependent. This means that there exists non-vanishing vector field $\R=\varphi^\s(a_1,\dots,a_r){\p \over \p a^\s}$,($\s=1,\dots r$, (at least one of functions $\varphi^\s$ is not vanished), such that for all functions $A_I(x,a)$, $(I=1,\dots,r-1)$ $dA_I(\R)=0$: $$ d A_I(\R)=\varphi^\a(a){\p A_I(x,a)\over \p a^\a}\equiv 0\,. \tag{4} $$ Using equation (2) one can see immediately that this equation implies equation (3): $f\in {\cal C}$, $df(\R)=dF(\R)=0$: $$ \varphi^\a(a){\p f^i\over \p a^\a}= \varphi^\a(a){\p F^i\over \p a^\a}= \varphi^\a(a){\p A_I(x,a)\over \p a^\a} {\p F^i(x,A_1,\dots, A_{r-1})\over \p A_I}=0\,. \tag{4} $$ Now prove inverse implication. Suppose that there exists non-vanishing vector field $\R$ on space of parameters $a^1,\dots,a^r$, such that for arbitrary functions $f^i(x,a)\in \cal C$ $$ df^i(\R)=0\,. \tag{5} $$ Consider local coordinates $(t,\xi_1,\dots,\xi_{r-1})$ which make straight the vecot field $\R$, i.e. coordinates such that $\R={\p\over \p t}$. It is evident that in these coordinates $$ f=f(\xi_1,\dots,\xi_{r-1}) \tag{6} $$ (see blog on 05 August for details.)

Thus we see that choosing functions $$ A_s(a_1,\dots,a_r)=\xi_s(a_1,\dots,a_s) (s=2,\dots,r) $$ we come to $r-1$ functions such that every function $f\in\cal C$ depends on these functions.

Proposition is proved.
