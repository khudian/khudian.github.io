${\S}0$ one technical lemma



I was learning it during all my life from 1978... On other hand I never red Sophus Lie, however I red Hisenhart.... Let me try to reconstruct it...

Let $G$ be $r$-dimensional group $G$. Suppose that it acts on $n$-dimensional manifold $M$: $$ y^i=f^i(a^\a,x^i)\,, \tag{0.1} $$ where $\{a^\a\}$ ($\a=1,\dots,r$) are parameters of $G$, $x^i$ ($i=1,\dots,N$) are coordinates of $M$.

We suppose that all $f^i$ are idependent for any values of parameters, i.e. $$ \det {\p f^i(x,a)\over \p x^j}\not=0 \tag{0.2a} $$ and all group parameters $a^\a$ are essential, i.e. for an arbitrary non-vanishing vector field $\R = R^\s(a){\p \over \p a^s}$ its image is not vanished: $df_*\R\not=0$ if $\R\not=0$: $$ R^\s(a){\p f(x,a)\over \p a^s}=0 \Rightarrow R^s\equiv 0\,, \tag{0.2b} $$ (see blogs on 2-nd 10,11 ) Composition of two transformation is another transformation: $$ f^i(y,b)= f^i(f^j(x,a),b)= f^i(x,\varphi (a,b))\,, \tag{0.3} $$ where $a\colon\, y=f^i(x,a)$ and function $\varphi(a,b)$ defines multiplication of parameters.

Lemma $$ \det {\p\varphi^\a(a,b)\over \p b^\beta}\not=0\,, \tag{0.4a} $$ and $$ \det {\p \varphi^\a(a,b)\over \p a^\beta}\not=0\,, \tag{0.4b} $$





We prove this lemma in this paragraph.

Consider on $G\times G$ vector fields ' left' vector field ${\bf L}=N^\s(a,b){\p\over \p a^\s}$ and ' right' vector field $\R=R^\s(a,b){\p\over \p b^\s}$. Consider images of left and right vector fields under the map $f$. It follows from equation (0.3) that for right vector field $$ f_*\R=R^\s(a,b){\p\over \p b^\s} f\left(f\left(x,a\right),b\right)= R^\s(a,b){\p f(x.\varphi)\over \p \varphi^\a} {\p\varphi^\a(a,b)\over \p b^\s}\,, \tag{5a} $$ and for left vector field $$ f_*{\bf L}=L^\s(a,b){\p\over \p a^\s} f\left(f\left(x,a\right),b\right)= L^\s(a,b){\p f^i(a,b)\over \p \varphi^\a} {\p \varphi^\a\over \p a^\s} \tag{5b} $$ We are ready to prove relations (4a) and (4b). Proof is by contradiction.

Failing of relation (4a) means that there exists non-vanishing right vector field such that $$ d\varphi (\R)= R^\s(a,b){\p \varphi ^\a\over\p b^\s}=0\,. $$ Respectively failing of relation (4b) means that there exists non-vanishing left vector field such that $$ d\varphi ({\bf L})= L^\s(a,b){\p \varphi ^\a\over\p a^\s}=0\,. $$

We will show that condition $d\varphi(\R)=0$ for non-vanishing vector field $\R$ implies that $f_*\R=0$, and respectively we will show that condition $d\varphi({\bf L})=0$ for non-vanishing vector field $\bf L$ implies that $f_*{\bf L}=0$. Thus degeneracy of multiplication implies that parameters are not essential.





Suppose relation (4a) is failed. We see that this means that there exists non-vanishing right vector field $\R$ such that $ d\varphi (\R)=0$. It follows from relation (5a) that $f_*\R=0$. This is true for all values $a$. This implies that for function $f(y,b)$ parameters are not essential. Contradiction.





Now prove equation (0.4b). We see that there exists non-vanishing left vector field $\bf L$ such that $d\varphi ({\bf L})=0$. It follows from relation (0.5b) that $f_*{\bf L}=0$. Again using (0.5b) we see that $$ 0=f_*{\bf L}= L^\s(a,b){\p \over \p a^\s} f\left( f \left(x,a \right), b \right)= L^\s(a,b) {\p f^i\over \p y^j}\big\vert_{y=y(x,a)} {\p y^j\over \p a^\s} $$ Since equation (0.2) $df$ is invertible, hence this relation implies that for non-vanishing vector field $\bf L$ $f_*{\bf L}=0$. This contradicts to the fact that parameters are essential. $$ L^s(a,b){\p y^i(x,a)\over \p x^s}=0 $$ Thus we proved very small but essential equation (0.4)
