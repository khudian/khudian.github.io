Essential remark on essential parameters

using book of Eisenhart



In blogs on 2-nd August and 10 August we discussed this conception. We see that number of essential parameters is very close to rank of Jacobian, however this is not the same.

I would like again to emphasize the key points.

Let $\cal C$ be $r$-parametric family of maps $$ y^\mu =f^\mu(x^i,a^\s)\colon\,\, M\times P {\buildrel f\over \rightarrow }N \tag{1} $$ from $M\times A$ to $N$, $M$ is $m$-dimensional manifold, $P$ is the $r$-dimensional space (manifold) of parameters, and $N$ is $N$-dimensional 'target' manifold, ($x^i$ are coordinates on $M$, $y^\mu$ are coordinates on $N$, and $a^\s$--parameters).

We say that parameters $a^\s$ are essential if it is not true that they are not essential. We say that parameters are not essential if one can reduce their amount, i.e. there exists another $r-1$-dimensional space (manifold) of parameters $P'$ such that $$ y^\mu =f^\mu(x^i,a^\s)\colon\,\, M\times P {\buildrel f\over \rightarrow }N= M\times \left(P{\buildrel A\over \rightarrow } P'\right) {\buildrel F\over \rightarrow} N\,, \tag{2a} $$ i.e. $$ y^\mu =f^\mu(x^i,a^\s)= F^\mu\left(x^i, A_1\left(a^1,\dots,a^r\right),\dots A_{r-1}\left(a^1,\dots,a^r\right) \right)\,. \tag{2b} $$ In this case consider non-vanishing vector field $\R$ such that $A_*\R=0$: $$ \R=\varphi^\s{a}{\p\over \p a^\s}\colon\,\, \R\not=0, {\rm but\,} A_*R=0, {\rm i.e.} \varphi^\s {\p A_I(a)\over \p a^\s}=0 (I=1,\dots,r-1)\,. \tag{3} $$ (this field exists since dimension of $P$ is greater than dimension of $P'$.). One can see from equation (2) that image of this vector field under the map $f$ vanishes. We see that if parameters are not essential then there exists non-vanishing vector field $\R$ in the space of parameters such that vector field $f_*\R$ in $N$ vanishes: $$ f_*\R= \varphi^\s(a){\p f^\mu(x,a)\over \p a^\s}{\p\over \p y^\mu}= \varphi^\s(a) {\p F^\mu\left(x, A_I(a^\a) \right)\over \p a^\s}{\p\over \p y^\mu}= $$ $$ \varphi^\s(a) {\p A_I(a)\over \p a^\s} {\p F^\mu\left(x, A_I(a^\a) \right) \over \p A_I}{\p\over \p y^\mu}=0\,. $$ One can see that this condition is also sufficient. The existence of non-vanishing vector field $\R$ in space of parameters such that $f_*\R=0$ means thaat these parameters are not essential. Indeed make 'straight' this vector field: go to coordinates $(t,\xi_1,\dots,\xi_{r-1})$ such that $\R={\p\over \p t}$ in these coordinates. It is evident that in this case parameters $a^i$ are not essential: map (2) depends on parameters $$ A_1(a)=\xi_1(a)\,,\dots A_{r-1}(a)=\xi_{r-1}(a)\,. $$ We see that parameters $a^\s$ are not essential if and only if there exists non-vanishing vector field $\R$ in space of parameters such that $f_*\R=0$. One can formulate it in the following way also:

Parameters $\{a^\s\}$ ($s=1,\dots,r$) are essential if and only if an arbitrary vector field $\R=\varphi^\s(a){\p\over \p a^\s}$ in the space of parameters which does not change any function $f\in\cal C$ has to vanish: $$ \left( \forall f\in {\cal C}\,, df(\R)=\varphi^\a{\p f^\mu(x,a) \over \p a^\a}=0 \right)\Rightarrow \varphi^\a\equiv 0\,. \tag{4} $$ Consider Jacobian, $r\times n$ matrix $M_0= \left|\left|{\p f^\mu(x,a)\over \p a^\a}\right|\right|$ In the case if $$ {\rm rank} M_0=r \tag{5} $$ it is comme il faut: all parameters are essential since $$ \varphi^\s {\p f^\mu(x,a)\over \p a^\s}=0 \Rightarrow \varphi^\s\equiv 0\,. \tag{6} $$ Sure this does not mean that if rank of $M_0$ is less than $r$, then parameters are not essential. To exclude 'non-essentiality' of parameters one has to exclude the existence of non vanishing solution for equation $$ \varphi^\s (a){\p f^\mu(x,a)\over \p a^\s}=0\,. (\hbox {for $\varphi$}) \tag{7} $$ i.e. one has to differentiate this equation on $x$. E.g. equation (7) implies that $$ \varphi^\s (a) {\p^2 f^\mu(x,a)\over\p x^i\p a^\s}=0\,,\quad\,, \varphi^\s (a) {\p^3 f^\mu(x,a)\over \p x^i\p x^j\p a^\s}=0\,,\quad $$ and one comes to the fact that parameters are esseential if for example rank of extended matrix $M_1$ is equal to $r$ or rank of matrix $M_2$ is equal to $r$. (Here $M_1= \left|\left| \matrix { {\p f^\mu(x,a)\over \p a^a}\cr {\p^2 f^\mu(x,a)\over \p x^i\p a^a}\cr } \right|\right| $ and $M_2= \left|\left| \matrix { {\p f^\mu(x,a)\over \p a^a}\cr {\p^2 f^\mu(x,a)\over \p x^i\p a^a}\cr {\p^3 f^\mu(x,a)\over \p \p x^j x^i\p a^a}\cr } \right|\right| $ ) Sure this does not work for groups, but....

Consider example:

$y=a+x+bx^2$. Rank of matrix $M_0$ is equal to $1$, however rank of all matrices $M_n$ is equal to $2$ for $n\geq 2$ $$= M_0=\pmatrix {1 x^2}\,, M_1=\pmatrix {&1 &x^2\cr &0 & 2x\cr}\,,\quad M_2=\pmatrix {&1 &x^2\cr &0 & 2x\cr &0 &2\cr}\,\dots $$ Sure this does not happen for groups...
