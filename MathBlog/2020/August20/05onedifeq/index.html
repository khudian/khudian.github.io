Solving one equation: $f\colon df(\X)=0$

I did hundred years ago, however...



How look functions $f=f(x)$ on $\R^n$ such that $$ A^i(x){\p f\over \p x^i}=0\,, (i=1,\dots,n)\,. \tag{1} $$ Consider vector field $\X=A^i(x){\p \over \p x^i}$. Equation (1) may be rewritten as $$ f\colon\, df(\X)=0\,, $$ where $\X=A^i(x)\p_i$ is vector field.

To solve this equation we have to choose coordinates such that vector field $\X$ becomes "straight" in these coordinates $$ \hbox {Local coordinates $t,\xi_1,\dots,\xi_{r-1}$ such that $\X={\p\over \p t}$} $$ Then this is obvious that solution of (1) is arbitrary function on $\xi_1,\dots\xi_{r-1}$.



Let $M$ be $n-1$ dimensional 'surface' in $\R^n$. Consider in tubular neighborhood of $M$ new local coordinates $$ t\,,\xi_1,\dots,\xi_{n-1} $$ adjusted to the surface $M$ and vector field $\X$, i.e. coordinates such that $$ \X={\p\over \p t}\,,{\rm and}\,\,t\big\vert_M=0\,. $$ one can see that for arbitrary point (which is in a vicinity of $M$) $$ t(\pt)\colon\,\, \exp (-t\X) \pt\rangle\in M $$ $\xi_i$ ($i=1,\dots,n-1$) is constant along the flux of vector field $\X$: $$ {dx^i\over dt}=A^i(x) $$ Example Let $M$ be a surface in $\R^3$ defined by equation $$ z=f(x,y)\,, $$ where $c x,y,z$ are standard cartesian coordinates. Consider vector field $$ \X= x{\p\over \p x}+ y{\p\over \p y}+ z^2{\p\over \p z}\,, $$ and describe coordinates $(t,\xi_1,\xi_2)$ such that $$ \X= {\p\over \p t}\,,\quad t\big\vert_M=0\,. $$

Solution $$ \cases { \dot x=x\cr \dot y=y\cr \dot z=z^2\cr }\Rightarrow \cases { x(t)=Ae^t\cr y(t)=Be^t\cr z(t)={1\over C-t}\cr } \,,\quad \r(t)\vert_{t=0}\in M \,\, {\rm condition \,\, means}\,\, z(t)\vert_{t=0}={1\over C}= $$ $$ f\left(x(t)_{t=0}, y(t)_{t=0}\right)= f\left(A, B\right),\,\, {\rm i.e.} \, t=t(x,y,z)\colon\,\, {\rm such \,\, that}\, \cases {x(t)=Ae^t\cr y(t)=Be^t\cr z(t)={f(A,B)\over 1-tf(A,B)}\cr } $$
