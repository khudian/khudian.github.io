Here I would like to do some standard exercise in classical mechanis using HAMILTONIAN REDUCTION.



Consider partcile in central symmetric field: $$ L={m(\dot x^2+\dot y^2+\dot z^2)\over 2} -{\a\over |\r|}\,. $$ (it will be attracting if $\a>0$). The Lagrangian can be reduced to one-dimensional by the following standard procedure. Momentum ${\bf M}=\r\times\bf \v$ is preserved, so take a plane $\a$ which is orthogonal to $\bf M$. Particle moves in this plane. In this plane in polar coordinates $$ L= { m\left( {\dot R}^2+ +R^2{\dot \varphi}^2 \right) \over 2}-{\a\over R}\,. $$ We have ${d\over dt}{\p L\over \p \dot\varphi}=$ ${d\over dt}mR^2\dot \varphi=0$, thus $\dot \varphi={M\over mR^2}$. We come to one-dimensional Lagrangian: $$ L= { m\left( {\dot R}^2+ +R^2{\dot \varphi}^2 \right) \over 2}-{\a\over R}\big\vert_{\dot \varphi={M\over mR^2}}= $$

We have that Hamiltonian $$ H={p_x^2+p_y^2+p_z^2\over 2m}+{\a\over r}\,. $$
