Two years ago reading lectures of Prasolov I learned something important about stereographic projection

Stereographic projection in a standard way $$ \cases { x={2u\over 1+u^2+v^2}\cr y={2v\over 1+u^2+v^2}\cr z={u^2+v^2-1\over 1+u^2+v^2}\cr } \Leftrightarrow \cases { u={x\over 1-z}\cr v={y\over 1-z}\cr x^2+y^2+z^2=1\cr } $$ has annuying problem that we connect three coordinates $(x,y,z)$ (which obey constraint $x^2+y^2+z^2=1$) with two "free" coordinates $u,v$. One can avoid this problems considering stereographic projection embedding in inversion. wiIf you consider the sphere $S^2$ with centre at origin,then

Inversion sphere has centre at the point $\n=(0,0,1)$ and it has radius $R=\sqrt 2$. We come to transformation $$ \r\to \n+{2(\r-\n)\over (\r-\n)^2}\, $$ i.e. in components $$ \hbox {a point}\,\,\, \pmatrix {u\cr v\cr h\cr}\to \pmatrix{0\cr 0\cr 1\cr} + {2\over u^2+v^2+(h-1)^2} \pmatrix {u\cr v\cr h-1\cr}\,, $$ i.e. $$ \cases { x={2u\over u^2+v^2+(h-1)^2}\cr y={2v\over u^2+v^2+(h-1)^2}\cr z=1+{2(h-1)\over u^2+v^2+(h-1)^2}\cr } \quad\hbox {and inverse}\quad \cases { u={2x\over x^2+y^2+(z-1)^2}\cr v={2y\over x^2+y^2+(z-1)^2}\cr h=1+{2(z-1)\over x^2+y^2+(z-1^2}\cr } $$ This is transformation of three coordinates to three coordinates. The plane $h=0$ goes to the sphere $x^2+y^2+z^2$ and vice versa.
