Gauging of $\delta$-function

We know that $\delta(x)=C\int e^{ikx}dk$. Calculate $C$.

Take function $\varphi$ such that $\varphi=\varphi_\d(x)= \cases{ 0 \quad \hbox {for $|x|\geq \delta$}\cr 1 \quad\hbox {for $|x|\leq \delta$}\cr }$. We have $$ 1=\int \delta(x)\varphi dx= \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dk \left( \int_{-\d}^\d Ce^{ikx}dx \right)= \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \left({Ce^{ikx}\over ik}\big\vert^\d_{-\delta}\right) $$ $$ = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{Ce^{ik\d}-Ce^{-ik\d}\over ik}dk= 2C\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{\sin k\d\over k}dk= 4C\int_0^{\infty}{\sin k\d\over k}dk=4C\cdot {\pi\over 2}\,. $$ Thus $C={1\over 2\pi}$ and $$ \delta(x)={1\over 2\pi}\int e^{ikx}dk\,. $$ One can see that for Fourier transfomration $$ \varphi(x)= C_1\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \tilde \varphi(k)e^{ikx}dk\Rightarrow \,\tilde \varphi(k)= C_2\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \varphi(x)e^{-ikx}dk\,, {\rm with}\,\, C_1C_2={1\over 2\pi}\,, $$ usually $$ \varphi(x)= {1\over \sqrt {2\pi}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \tilde \varphi(k)e^{ikx}dk\,,\quad \tilde \varphi(k)= {1\over\sqrt {2\pi}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \varphi(x)e^{-ikx}dk\,, {\rm with}\,\, C_1C_2={1\over 2\pi}\,, $$ Usually we put $C_1=1$ and $C_2={1\over 2\pi}$: $$ \varphi(x)=\int \tilde\varphi(p)e^{ipx}dp\,,\quad \tilde\varphi(p)={1\over 2\pi} \int \varphi(x)e^{-ipx}dp\,. $$
