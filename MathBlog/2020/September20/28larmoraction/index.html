Here we will calculate the action of a particle which moves in parallel electircal and magnetic fields.

We will present here the short version of calculations based on calculations of action in homogeneous electric field (see the Blog on 24 September 2019).

Conisder Lagrangian in homogeneous electirc and magnetic fields directed along $z$-axis: $$ L= {m(\dot x^2+\dot y^2+\dot z^2)\over 2}+ {eb\over 2c}(y\dot x-x\dot y)-mgz\,, \left(\hbox {we choose $\E=E\e_z$ such that $eEz=mgz$}\right) \tag{1a} $$ respectively $$ H= {\left(p_x-{yeb\over 2c}\right)^2\over 2m} +{\left(p_y+{xeb\over 2c}\right)^2\over 2m}+ {p_z^2\over 2m}+mgz\,,\quad (e\varphi=mgz)\, \tag{1b} $$ We have to calculate an action $W=W(T,r,\R)$. To do this we consider the trajectory $x(\tau)$ such that it starts at the point $\r$ at $0$ and ends at the point $\R$ at the moment $\tau=T$ (system is conservative): $$ W(T,\r,\R)=\int_0^T L(x(\tau),\dot x(\tau))d\tau \tag{1c} $$ Remark For not conseervative Lagrangians $W=W(t_1,\r,t_2,\R)$. To calculate the action we consider the trajectory which begins at the point $\r$ at $t_1$ and ends at the point $\R$ at the moment $t_2$. In the case if Lagrangian is conservative (it does not depend on time) then the action $W=W(t_1,\r,t_2=t_1+T,\R)$ does not depend on $t_1$.

Using some properties of action we already calculated an action if magnetic interaction is absent (see the blog on September 2019)" $$ W_0=W_{b=0} (t_1,\r;t_2,\R)={m(\R-\r)^2\over 2T}-mg{z+Z\over 2}T- {1\over 24}mg^2T^3\,,\quad (T=t_2-t_1)\,. \tag{2a} $$ The switching on of magnetic field leads to $$ W_b=W_0+{eb\over 2c}\int_C ydx-xdy\, \tag{2b}, $$ where $C$ is the trajectory of the particle. The problem is reduced to differential geometry: we have to solve it!

It is convenient to introduce $$ w=w_L={eb\over mc} \tag{3a} $$ (so calledd 'Larmor' frequency.) Partcile moves along the helix of the radius $R_L$. Denote by $\v_\perp$ the projection of the velocity on the $OXY$ plane. We have that $$ |\v_\perp|=R_Lw\,,\quad \v=\v_\perp-gt \e_z\, \tag{3b} $$ One can see (using Stokes formula) that $$ {eb\over 2c}\int_C ydx-xdy= {mw\over 2}\int_C ydx-xdy={mw\over 2}{R^2\Phi\over 2}\,, \tag{3c} $$ where $\Phi=wT$ is the angle of rotation of the particle, which moves along the helix.
