Fedoruk identity and meaning of $\delta$-function of momenta

Fedoruk identity (see the Stirling fomula Blog) claims that $$ \int f(x_0-x)u(x)dx= \sqrt {2\pi}\left[ \tilde f\left({1\over i}{d\over dx}\right)u(x) \right]_{x=x_0}\,, $$ where $\tilde f$ Fourier image of $f$.

The formal proof is the following $$ \int f(x_0-x)u(x)dx= {1\over 2\pi}\int \tilde f(k)\exp \left({ik\left(x_0-x\right)}\right) \tilde u(p)\exp \left({ipx}\right)dkdpdx= $$ $$ {1\over 2\pi}\int \tilde f(k)\exp (ikx_0) \exp \left({ix\left(p-k\right)}\right) \tilde u(p)dkdpdx= \int \tilde f(k)\exp (ikx_0) \delta (k-p) \tilde u(p)dkdp $$ $$ \int \tilde f(k)\exp (ikx_0) \tilde u(k)dk =\sqrt `[2\pi]\left[ \tilde f\left({1\over i}{d\over dx}\right)u(x) \right]_{x=x_0}\,. $$ Here we choose gauging: $$ f(x)={1\over \sqrt 2\pi}\int \tilde f(k)\exp (ikx)dk\,, \tilde f(k)={1\over \sqrt 2\pi}\int f(x)\exp (-ikx)dx\,, \left(\delta (x)={1\over 2\pi}\int \exp (ikx)dk\right)\,. $$ (this is the gauging which is different from the gauging which we used in other texts ( see Striling, Blog,... ))

I have to confess that I did not understand the Fedoruk identity

To understand it better consider the special case if $f\equiv 1$. Then $$ \tilde f(k)=\sqrt {2\pi}\delta(k)\,. $$ Fedoruk identity tells that in this case for 'every' function $u$ $$ \int u(x)dx= \sqrt{2\pi} \left[ \left(\sqrt{2\pi }\delta(k) \big\vert_{k= {1\over i}{d\over dx}}\right)u(x) \right]_{x=x_0}= {2\pi} \left[ \left(\delta(k) \big\vert_{k= {1\over i}{d\over dx}}\right)u(x) \right]_{x=x_0}\,. $$ Check it: $$ 2\pi \left[ \left(\delta(k) \big\vert_{k= {1\over i}{d\over dx}}\right)u(x) \right]_{x=x_0}= 2\pi\left( \delta (\hat k)u(x)\right)_{x=x_0}= \left[ \left(\int \exp {i\hat k}z dz\right) u(x) \right]_{x=x_0}\,. $$ Using the fact that $$ \exp {ikz}u(x)=\exp \left\{i{1\over i}{d\over dx}z\right\} u(x)=u(x+z)\,, $$ we come to the answer: $$ 2\pi\left[ \left(\delta(k) \big\vert_{k= {1\over i}{d\over dx}}\right)u(x) \right]_{x=x_0}= \int u(x_0+z)dz =\int u(z)dz\,. $$ Fedoruk Identity have the deep physical meaning.
