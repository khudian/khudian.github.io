{I liked very much the fact that stationary ohase method possesses easy and hard part. (see e.g. my Etude on Stirling). On the other hand I realised that physicists 'do not care'" the hard part can be easy swallowed. Yesterday speaking with Ruben Mkrtchyan I was convinced that this isd true.}

Let $$ e^{{i\over \hbar}S(x)} $$ with $S(x)$ which has stationary point only at $x_0=0$.

It is almost obvious that $$ \int e^{{i\over \hbar}S(x)}d^nx\approx e^{{i\over \hbar}S(x_0)} {\pi^{n\over 2} \over \sqrt {\rm S(x_0)}}\,. $$ This is that I called the easy part of stationary phase method.

The further analysis tells that all the other terms are polynomials on $\hbar$. To prove this I used so called 'Fedoruk identity'. On the other hand this may be done by rescaling $$ x={y\sqrt h}\,. $$
