Here I will formulate some of their properties, paying attention to non-conservative systems. Let $L=L(q,\dot q,t)$ be a Lagrangian, $H=H(p,q,t)$ be a Hamiltonian, and $S=S(q_1,t_1, q_2,t_2)$ be an action of the mechanical system: $$ H=H(p,q,t)=p_i\dot q^i-L(q,\dot q,t)\,,\quad\colon\,\, p_i={\p L(q,\dot q,t)\over \p \dot q^i}\,, \tag{1a} $$ $$ S(q_1,t_1;q_2,t_2)= \int^{t_1}_{t_2} L\left(x(\tau),\dot x(\tau)\right)d\tau\,, \tag{1b} $$ where $x^i(tau)$ be a path, which obeys Euler-Lagrange equations of motion, and the boundary conditions $$ x(\tau)\colon\quad {d\over dt} \left({\p L\over \p \dot q^i}\right)= {\p L\over \p q^i}\,, \cases { x(\tau)\big\vert_{\tau=t_1}=q_1\cr x(\tau)\big\vert_{\tau=t_2}=q_2\cr }\,. \tag{1c} $$ Using this formula one can show that $$ {\p S\left(t_1,q_1;t_2,q_2\right)\over \p t_2} =-H\left( p_i={\p S\left(t_1,q_1;t_2,q_2\right)\over \p q_2^i}, q_2,t_2 \right)\,, \tag{2a} $$ $$ {\p S\left(t_1,q_1;t_2,q_2\right)\over \p t_1} =H\left( p_i={\p S\left(t_1,q_1;t_2,q_2\right)\over \p q_1^i}, q_1,t_1 \right)\,. \tag{2b} $$ Show for example (2a): $$ \delta_2{\p S\over \p t_2}= S\left(t_1,q_1;t_2+\delta_2,q_2\right) -S\left(t_1,q_1;t_2,q_2/\right)= $$ $$ \int^{t_1}_{t_2+\delta_2} L\left(\tilde x(\tau),\dot {\tilde x}(\tau)\right)d\tau- \int^{t_1}_{t_2} L\left(x(\tau),\dot x(\tau)\right)d\tau= L(q_2,\dot q_2)\delta_2+ $$ $$ \int^{t_1}_{t_2} L\left(x(\tau)+\vare h(\tau),\dot {\tilde x}(\tau)\right)d\tau- \int^{t_1}_{t_2} L\left(x(\tau),\dot x(\tau)\right)d\tau= $$ $$ L(q_2,\dot q_2)\delta_2+ \int_{t_1}^{t_2} {d\over dt} \left[ {\p L(q,\dot q,t)\over \p \dot q^i} \vare h^i(\tau) \right]^{t_2}_{t_1}\,, \tag{3a} $$ where $\delta_2$,$\vare$ are nilpotents: $\delta_2^2=\vare^2=0$. Boundary conditions tell us that $$ \tilde x^i(t_2+\delta_2)= x^i(t_2+\delta_2)+\vare h(t_2+\delta_2)= x^i(t_2)+v^i(t_2)\delta_2+\vare h(t_2), $$ Since $\tilde x^i(t_2+\delta_2)=x^i(t`_2)=q_2^i$, this means that $$ \vare h^i(t_2)=-\dot x^i(t_2)\delta_2\,. \tag{3b} $$ Thus exptession in (3a) is equal to $$ L(q_2,\dot q_2)\delta_2+ \int_{t_1}^{t_2} {d\over dt} \left[ {\p L(q,\dot q,t)\over \p \dot q^i} \vare h^i(\tau) \right]^{t_2}_{t_1}= \delta_2\left(L\left(q_2,\dot q_2,t\right)- {\p L(q,\dot q,t)\over \p \dot q^i} \dot q^i \right) \tag{3d} $$ Using (1a) we prove (2a) using this relation.






${\S}2$ Extended canonical transformations



Every function $F=F(q_,Q,t)$generates extended canonical transformations (see previous blogs) $$ \pmatrix {p_i\cr q^j\cr } \mapsto \pmatrix {\rho_i(p,q,t)\cr Q^j(p,q,t)\cr }\colon \quad, p_i= {\p F(q,Q,t)\over\p q^i}\,, Q^i=- {\p (q,Q,t)\over\p q^i}\,, \tag{2.1a} $$ such that new Hamiltonia is defined by $$ h(\rho,Q,t)=-{\p F(q,Q,t)\over \p t}+H(p,q,t)\,. (2.1b) $$ These equations follow from equation: $$ pdq-Hdt=\rho dQ-hdt+dF\,. $$

${\S}3$ Action and extended canonical transformations



Take as $F$ an action, however action depends on two times, and here 'mystery' began:

An arbitrary action defines one-parametric family Notice that equation (2,1b) implies that new Hamiltonian $h$ vanishes if $$ {\p F(q,Q,t)\over \p t}+H(p,q,t)\,, {\rm where}\, , p_i={\p F(q,Q,t)\p q^i}\,, $$ but this is nothing but Hamilton Jacobi equation for action! We come to the statement.

Let $S=S\left(q_1,t_1;q_2.t_2\right)$ be an action of mechanical system with hamiltonian $H=H(p,q,t)$. Consider an arbitrary canonical transformation generated by one-parametric family of functions $$ F_\tau(q,Q,t)=S\left(q,\tau;Q,t\right)\,. $$ For an arbitrary such a tramsformation a new Hamiltonian vanishes, i.e. equations of motion are: $$ \rho(t)\equiv \rho_0\,, Q(t)\equiv Q_0\,. $$ Remark There is a temptation to write 'one parametric' family of extended canonical transformations, however in simple cases this is not true. I do not know the general statement.
