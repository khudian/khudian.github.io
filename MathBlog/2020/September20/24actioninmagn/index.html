Action in parallel magnetic and elevtirc field)



We calculate here the action of Hamiltonian $$ { H=\left({\bf p}-{e\over c}{\bf A}\right)^2 \over 2m}+e\varphi \tag{1a} $$ in the case if electric and magnetic fields are directed along $z$ axis. We know that in the case if magnetic field vanishes, then $$ S\left(t_1,q_1;t_2,q_2\right)= {m(\r_2-\r_1)^2\over 2T}-mg{z_1+z_2\over 2}T-{1\over 24}mg^2T^3 \tag{0} $$ (here $T=t_2-t_1$, and $g$ is such that $e\varphi=mgz$) (see previous Bloges)

Switch on magnetic field $\bf B$ along $z$-axis. $$ {\bf A}={1\over 2}{\bf B}\times \r\,,\quad {\rm with}\,, {\bf B}=b\e_z, \quad {\rm i.e.} {\bf A}={b\over 2}\left(y\e_x-x\e_y\right)\,, \tag{1b} $$ and $$ H= {\left(p_x-{yeb\over 2c}\right)^2\over 2m} +{\left(p_y+{xeb\over 2c}\right)^2\over 2m}+ {p_z^2\over 2m}+mgz\,,\quad (e\varphi=mgz)\, \tag{2a} $$ and Lagrangian is equal to $$ L={m(\dot x^2+\dot y^2+\dot z^2)\over 2}+ {e\over c}A_i\dot x^i-mgz= {m(\dot x^2+\dot y^2+\dot z^2)\over 2}+ {eb\over 2c}(y\dot x-x\dot y)-mgz\,. \tag{2b} $$ Euler-Lagrange equations of motion are: $$ \cases { {d\over dt}{\p L\over \p \dot x}= m{\buildrel {..}\over x}+{eb\over 2c}\dot y ={\p L\over \p x}=-{eb\over 2c}\dot y\cr {d\over dt}{\p L\over \p \dot y}= m{\buildrel {..}\over y}-{eb\over 2c}\dot x ={\p L\over \p y}={eb\over 2c}\dot x\cr {d\over dt}{\p L\over \p \dot z}= m{\buildrel {..}\over z} ={\p L\over \p z}=-mg\cr } \Leftrightarrow \cases { m{\buildrel {..}\over x}+ {eb\over c}\dot y=0\cr m{\buildrel {..}\over y}-{eb\over c}\dot x=0\cr m{\buildrel {..}\over z}=-mg\cr } \Leftrightarrow \cases { {\buildrel {..}\over x}+w\dot y=0\cr {\buildrel {..}\over y}-w\dot x=0\cr {\buildrel {..}\over z}=-g\cr } \,, \left(w={eb\over mc}\right)\,. \tag{3a} $$ Solve these equations. The last equation (${\buildrel {..}\over z}=-g$) is trivial, solve first and second: $$ \cases { {\buildrel {..}\over x}+w\dot y=0\cr {\buildrel {..}\over y}-w\dot x=0\cr } \Leftrightarrow {d^2\r(t)\over dt^2}=-w P{d\r(t)\over dt}\,,\quad P=\pmatrix {0 & 1\cr -1 & 0}\,. \tag{4a} $$ $P$ is generator of $SO(2)$, i.e. this equation (4) implies that $$ {d\r(t)\over dt}=e^{-wPt}\ac\,,\quad \left( e^{-wPt}= \pmatrix { \cos wt &-\sin wt\cr \sin wt & \cos wt\cr }\,\,,\hbox {and $\ac$ is a constant vector} \right)\,. \tag{4b} $$ This equation implies that $$ \r(t)=-{P^{-1}\over w}e^{-w Pt}\ac+\b= {P\over w}e^{-w Pt}\ac+\b\,, \hbox {since $P^2=-1$}\,. $$ here $\ac$ and $\b$ are constant vectors. Use boundary conditions to calculate these vectors: $$ \cases { \r(t)\big\vert_{t=0}=\r_0\cr {d\r(t)\over dt}\big\vert_{t=0}=\v_0\cr } \Rightarrow \cases { {1\over w}P(\ac)+\b=\r_0\cr \ac=\v_0\cr } \Rightarrow \r(t)={1\over w}e^{-wPt} P(\v_0)+\r_0- {1\over w}P(\v_0)\,, $$ i.e. $$ \r(t)= {1\over w} \pmatrix { \cos wt & -\sin wt\cr \sin wt & \cos wt\cr } \pmatrix { 0 & 1\cr -1 & 0\cr } \pmatrix { v_{0x}\cr v_{0y}\cr }+ \pmatrix { x_0\cr y_0\cr } - {1\over w} \pmatrix { 0 &1\cr -1 &0\cr } \pmatrix { v_{0x}\cr v_{0y}\cr }\,. $$ Collect equations together, we come to $$ x(t)={v_{0y}\over w}\cos wt+ {v_{0x}\over w}\sin wt+x_0 -{v_{0y}\over w}\,, \tag{5a} $$ $$ y(t)={v_{0y}\over w}\sin wt- {v_{0x}\over w}\cos wt+y_0 +{v_{0x}\over w}\,, \tag{5b} $$ and $$ z(t)=z_0+v_{0z}t-{gt^2\over 2}\,. \tag{5c} $$ These equations describe standard 'Larmor' circles in non-relativistic case. The desribe particle starting at initial point $\r_0=\pmatrix {x_0\cr y_0\cr z_0\cr}$ with initial velocity ${\bf v}_0=\pmatrix {v_{x0}\cr v_{y0}\cr v_{z0}\cr}$ rotates around the helix with Larmor frequence $w={eb\over mc}$ \footnote {$^*$} {the centre of Larmor circle is an 'integral of motion'}

We are ready to calculate the action

We already caclulated the action of free particle, and the action of particle in homogeneous field. This is why it is convenient to consider Lagrangian $L$ (see equation (2b)) as a sum of three: $$ L=L_A+L_B+L_C\,, \tag{6a} $$ where $$ \underbrace {L_A={m(\dot x^2+\dot y^2)\over 2}}\,, _{\hbox {free particle}} \tag{6b} $$ $$ \underbrace {L_B={m\dot z^2\over 2}-mgz}\,, _{\hbox {free particle}} \tag{6c} $$ and $$ L_C= {eb\over 2c}(y\dot x-x\dot y)\,. \tag{6d} $$ We have $$ W\left(\r_1,t_1;\r_2,t_2|g,b\right)= \int_{t_1}^{t_2} L\left(x(\tau),\dot x(\tau),\tau\right)d\tau $$ where $x^i(\tau)$ is a trajecctory which obeys boundary conditions Simplify calculations using the fact tht Lagrangian does not depend on time, and it is the sum of three Langrangians, such that we also know the action of Lagrangians $L_A$ and $L_B$. However we again will check the previous calculations. Hence now switch off magnetic field: In this case $v_x$ and $v_y$ are preserved and $$ W\left(\r_1,t_1;\r_2,t_2|g,b=0\right)= \int_0^T \left[{m\dot z^2\over 2}-mgz\right]d\tau+ \int_0^T \left[{m\dot x^2+m\dot y^2\over 2}\right]d\tau\,,\quad (T=t_2-t_1)\,. \tag{7a} $$ The boundary conditions (see equation (5c)) say that in this case $$ x_2=x_1+v_{x0}T\,, y_2=y_1+v_{y0}T\Rightarrow v_{x0}={x_2-x_1\over T}\,, v_{y0}={y_2-y_1\over T}\,, \tag{7b} $$ and $$ z_2=z_1+v_{0z}T-{gT^2\over 2}\Rightarrow v_{0z}={z_2-z_1\over T}+{gT\over 2}\,, \tag{7c} $$ thus in the absence of magnetic field $$ W\left(\r_1,t_1;\r_2,t_2|g,b=0\right)= \int_0^T \left[{m\dot z^2\over 2}-mgz\right]d\tau+ \int_0^T \left[{m\dot x^2+m\dot y^2\over 2}\right]d\tau= $$ $$ {m\over 2}\int_0^{t_2-t_1}\left[ {z_2-z_1\over t_2-t_1} +{g(t_2-t_1)\over 2}-g\tau \right]^2d\tau+ $$ $$ -mg\int_0^T\left[z_1+ \left( {z_2-z_1\over T} +{gT\over 2} \right)\tau-{g\tau^2\over 2} \right] d\tau+ $$ $$ {m(x_2-x_1)^2\over 2(t_2-t_1)} +{m(y_2-y_1)^2\over 2(t_2-t_1)}= $$ $$ {m\over 2}\int_0^T \left[ {(z_2-z_1)^2\over T^2}+ {g^2T^2\over 4}+ g^2\tau^2+ g(z_2-z_1)- {2(z_2-z_1)\over T}g\tau- g^2T\tau \right]d\tau- $$ $$ - mg\left[ z_1T+{\Delta z\over T}{T^2\over 2} +{gT\over 2}{T^2\over 2}- {gT^3\over 6} \right]+ $$ $$ {m(x_2-x_1)^2\over 2(t_2-t_1)} +{m(y_2-y_1)^2\over 2(t_2-t_1)}= $$ $$ {m\over 2}\left[ {(z_2-z_1)^2\over T}+{g^2T^3\over 4}+{g^2T^3\over 3} +g(z_2-z_1)T- {2(z_2-z_1)\over T}g{T^2\over 2}- {g^2T^2\over 2} \right]- $$ $$ -mg\left[{z_1+z_2\over 2}+{1\over 12}gT^3\right] +\left[ {m(x_2-x_1)^2\over 2(t_2-t_1)} +{m(y_2-y_1)^2\over 2(t_2-t_1)}= \right]= $$ $$ {m(\r_2-\r_1)^2\over (t_2-t_1)}- mg{z_1+z_2\over 2}-{1\over 24}{mg^2T^3}\,. \tag{8} $$ {\bf Remark} We are happy; the answer coincides with previous calculations (see the Blog on September 2019).

Now using equation (5d)calculate the most intriguing: the part of action responsible for magnetic field: $$ W\left(\r_1,t_1;\r_2,t_2|g,b\right)- W\left(\r_1,t_1;\r_2,t_2|g,b=0\right)= {eb\over 2c}\int_C ydx-xdy\,. \tag{9} $$ where we have to integrate over the trajectory of the particle. Here let us take a breath. $C$ is a curve $x^i(\tau) $ such that it starts at the point $\r_1$ and it ends at the point $\r_2$: $$ C\colon\,], \r(t)\,,\quad \cases { \r(t_1)=\r_1\ \r(t_2)=\r_2\ } $$ Sure one can find this curve using formulae (5), in particular using (5) one can find $\v$ such that particle with initial velocity $\v$ approaches the point $\r_2$ at the moment $T=t_2-t_1$. On the other hand the calculation of the integral (9) can be preformed in a short and beautiful way: Particle's trajectory is helix, its radius is equal to $R={v\over w}$, where $w={eb\over mc}$, hence in (9) we come to the projection of helix on the base circle" $$ {eb\over 2c}\int_C ydx-xdy= {eb\over 2c}\cdot {R^2\varphi\over 2}= {eb\over 2mc}\cdot {mv^2\over w^2}\cdot {wT\over 2}= {mv^2T\over 4} \tag{10} $$ Recall that $T$ is a time, during this time particle moving by this helix with speed $|\v|$ moves from the point $\r_1$ to the point $\r_2$.



Now we have to calcuclate $\v$ such that particle reachs the point $\r_2$ from the point $\r_1$ in a time $T=t_2-t_1$. Recall that as we shown in equations (4), (5) $$ \r(t)={1\over w}e^{-wPt} P(\v)+\r_1- {1\over w}P(\v)\,. \tag{11a} $$ This means that if particle reaches the point $\r_2$ in the time $T=t_2-t_1$, then $$ {1\over w}\left(e^{-TPw}-1\right)P(\v)=\r_2-\r_1\,, \left(\hbox {recall that}\,\, e^{-TPw}= \right)\,, \tag{11b} $$ Note that $$ e^{-TPw}-1= \pmatrix { \cos Tw -1&-\sin Tw\cr \sin Tw & \cos Tw-1\cr }= -2\sin {Tw\over 2} \pmatrix { \sin {Tw\over 2}& \cos {Tw\over 2}\cr -\cos {Tw\over 2} & \sin {Tw\over 2} \cr } $$ where the last matrix is orthogonal. Since matrix $P$ is also orthogonal, the we see that equation (11b) implies that $$ {1\over w}\left|-2\sin {Tw\over 2}\right| |\v|=|\r_2-\r_1|\,, $$ i.e. $$ \v^2={w^2(\r_2-\r_1)^2\over 4\sin^2{Tw\over 2}}\,, $$ and $$ {eb\over 2c}\int_C ydx-xdy= {mv^2T\over 4} ={mw^2(\r_2-\r_1)^2T\over 16\sin^2{Tw\over 2}} $$ Thus wee come that action is equal to $$ W\left(\r_1,t_1;\r_2,t_2|g,b\right)= W\left(\r_1,t_1;\r_2,t_2|g,b=0\right)+ {eb\over 2c}\int_C ydx-xdy= {mv^2T\over 4}= $$ $$ {m(\r_2-\r_1)^2\over 2T}+ {mw^2T(\r_2-\r_1)^2\over 16\sin^2{Tw\over 2}}- mg{\r_2+\r_1\over 2}\n T-{1\over 24}mg^2T^3\,. $$
