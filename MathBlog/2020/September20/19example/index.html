Example of canon. transformation induced by an action



We know that motion of particle can be conisdered as canonical transformation generrated by action. More precisely this means the following:

Let $H=H(p,q,t)$ be a Hamiltonian of the system, then arbitrary trajectory $\pmatrix {p(t)\cr q(t)\cr}$ is a canocial transformation. One can say it in the following more precise way:

The extended canonical transformation generated by the one-parametric family of functions $$ F_\tau(q,Q,t)=-S\left(\tau,q;t,Q\right) \tag{1} $$ where $S=S(\tau,q; t,Q)$ is an action transforms Hamiltonian $H$ to the new Hamiltonan, which vanishes.

Recall that an arbitrary function $F=F(q,Q,t)$ gemerates canonical transformation $$ \pmatrix {p\cr q\cr}\mapsto \pmatrix {\rho=\rho(p,q)\cr Q=Q(p,q)\cr}\colon\quad p_i={\p F_\tau(q,Q,t)\over \p q^i}\,, \rho_m={\p F_\tau(q,Q,t)\over \p Q^m}\,, \tag{2a} $$ and Hamiltonian $H(p,q,t)$ transforms to the new Hamiltonian $$ h(\rho,Q,t)=H(p,q,t)+{\p F(t,q,Q)\over \p t}\,. \tag{2b} $$ Formulae (2a) and (2b) follow from the equation $$ pdq^i-Hdt=\rho_mdQ^m-dh+dF\,. \tag{2c} $$

Recall that action is defined by equation $$ S(\tau,q; t,Q)=\int_\tau^t L(x(z),\dot x(z),z)dz\,, \tag{3a} $$ where $x(z)$ is trajectory of the particle, solution of dynamic equations with boundary conditions $x(\tau)=q$, $x(t)=Q$

In the case if generating function of canonical transformation (2) is defined by an action then Hamilton-Jacobi equation is obeyed: $$ H\left(\rho_m={\p S(\tau,q;t,Q)\over \p Q^m},Q\right)+ {\p S(\tau,q;t,Q)\over \p t}=0\,, \tag{4a} $$ this implies that $$ H\left(p_i=-{\p S(\tau,q;t,Q)\over \p q^i},q\right)- {\p S(\tau,q;t,Q)\over \p t}=0\,, \tag{4b} $$ and this equation means that under canonical transformation with the generating function (1) the new Hamiltonian $h$ vanishes. {\bf Remark} Pay attention diffference of signs in (7a) and (7b).

These equations lead to Hamilton-Jacobi equation (4a) and modified Hamilton-Jacobi .
