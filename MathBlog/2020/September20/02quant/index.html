For $S=S_1^a(x)q_a+S_2^{ab}(x)q_aq_b$ we calculate qunatum thick morphism and compare and compare it with classical one.

Define quantum morphism: $$ \Phi_S^{q*}(\hbar,g)\colon\quad e^{{i\over \hbar}\left( \Phi_S^{q*}(\hbar,g) \right) }= \int e^{{i\over \hbar} \left(S(x,q)-y^aq_a+g(y)\right)}dydq\,. $$

We have that $$ \int e^{{i\over \hbar} \left(S(x,q)-y^aq_a\right)}dq= \int e^{{i\over \hbar} \left( B^a(x)q_a+S^{ab}q_aq_b -y^aq_a\right)}dq\,. $$ Use substitution $q_a\to q_a+t_a$, and choose $$ t_a={y_a-B_a\over 2}\,, \left(S^{ab}y_b=y_a\right) $$ (we suppose that $S_2^{ab}$ is non-degenerate). Then only quadratic term will remain: $$ \int e^{{i\over \hbar} \left( B^a(x)q_a+S^{ab}q_aq_b -y^aq_a\right)}dq= C e^{{i\over \hbar} \left( -t_at^a \right)}= C e^{{i\over \hbar} \left( -{1\over 4}(y-B)^2 \right)} $$ where $$ C= \int e^{{i\over \hbar} S^{ab}q_aq_b}dq= \left( {\pi \hbar\over i}\right)^n\det S_2\,, $$ and for every $g(y)$ $$ e^{{i\over \hbar}\left( \Phi_S^{q*}(\hbar,g) \right) }= \int e^{{i\over \hbar} \left(S(x,q)-y^aq_a+g(y)\right)}dydq= \left(\int e^{{i\over \hbar} \left(S(x,q)-y^aq_a\right)}dq\right) e^{{i\over \hbar} g(y)}= $$ $$ C\int e^{{i\over \hbar} \left(g(y) -{1\over 4}(y-B)^2 \right)}dy $$ In accordance with classical limit stationary phase method this integral is 'concetrated' in a vicinity of the point $$ y^a=S_1^a(x)+2S_2^{ab}(x)q_b\,. $$
