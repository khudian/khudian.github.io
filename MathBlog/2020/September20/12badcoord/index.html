Action of free particle in very bad coordinates



We know that in fact action is $S=S(q_1,t_1;q_2,t_2)$, and $$ {\p S\over \p t_2}+H\left( p_i={\p S\over \p q_2^i},q^i\right)=0\,, \tag{1a} $$ where $$ H(p,q,t)=p_i\dot q^i-L(q,\dot q,t)\,,\qquad {\rm with}\,, p_i={\p L\over \p q^i} \tag{1b} $$ Formulae for action follow from definition: $$ S=S(q_1,t_1;q_2,t_2)= \int_{t_1}^{t_2} L\left(x(\tau),\dot x(\tau),\tau\right)\,, {\rm where} \,\, x(t_1)=q_1, x(t_2)=q_2. $$ however for conservative systems $L=L(q,\dot q)$, and action effectively depends on $T=t_2-t_1$.

$$ $$

Consider free particle $L={m\dot q^2\over 2}$ in 'bad' coordinate: $$ q=xt\,. $$ In new coordinates $$ L={m\over 2}(x+t\dot x)^2= {m\over 2}x^2+ {m\over 2}\dot x^2t^2\,,+ $$ and $$ H={p^2\over 2mt^2}-{p_ix^i\over t}\,. $$ For free particle $$ L={m\dot q^2\over 2}\,, S={m(q_2-q_1)^2\over 2(t_2-t_1)}, $$ and action $S$ is scalar, hence in new coordinates $$ S={m(x_2t_2-x_1t_1)^2\over 2(t_2-t_1)},. $$ Perform calculations. It is useful to introduce notations $$ S={m\Delta_i\Delta^i\over 2T}\,, \Delta^i=x_2^it_2-x_1^it_1\,, T={t_2-t_1}\,. $$ We see that $$ {\p S\over \p t_2}={m\Delta_ix_2^i\over T}- {m\Delta_i\Delta^i\over 2T^2}\,, $$ and $$ p_i={\p S\over x_2^i}={m\Delta_i t_2\over T}\,. $$ This leads to the answer: $S$ is obeyed to Hamilton Jacobi equation (1) in new 'bad' coordinates also.
