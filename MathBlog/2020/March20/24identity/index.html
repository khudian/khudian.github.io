Polarisation formula

Let $Q(x)=Q_n(x)$ be polynomail of degree $n$. For every $k$ We write an expression such that it vanishes if $n< k$:

$$ B(x,y)={1\over 2}\left(Q(x+y)-Q(x)-Q(y+Q(0)\right)\,, $$ $$ B(x,y,z)={1\over 6}\left( Q(x+y+z)-Q(x+y)-Q(y+z)-Q(x+z)+\right. $$ $$ \left. Q(x)+ Q(y)+Q(z)-Q(0) \right)\,, $$ $$ B(x,y,z,t)={1\over 24} \left( Q(x+y+z+t)-\right. $$ $$ \left.-Q(x+y+z)-Q(x+y+t)-Q(x+z+t)-Q(y+z+t)+ \right. $$ $$ \left. Q(x+y)+Q(x+z)+Q(x+t)+Q(y+z)+Q(y+t)+Q(z+t)\right. $$ $$ \left. -Q(x)-Q(y)-Q(z)+Q(0) \right)\,, $$ and so on: $$ B_n(x_1,\dots,x_n)=\sum (-1)^{n+k} Q\left( x_{i_1}+\dots+i_k \right)\, $$ where summation goes over all subsets, and for empty subset we mean that $$ Q\left(\emptyset\right)=Q(0) $$ where $0$ is null-vector.

Note that $B_n$ is polarised form of $Q(x)$ and moreover: $B_n$ vanishes if $Q$ has degree less than $n$.

One can say that for arbitrary polynomial $Q(x)$, the expression $B_n$ vanishes or has first term linear!

Namely Consider $$ \Phi_1(u_1)=u_1-u_0\,, $$ $$ \Phi_2(u_0,u_1,u_2,u_{12})= {1\over 2} \left(u_{12}-u_1-u_2+u_0\right)\,, $$ $$ \Phi_3(u_0,u_1,u_2,u_{12},u_{13},u_{23},u_{123})= {1\over 6} \left(u_{123}-u_{12}-u_{13}-u_{23}+u_1+u_2+u_3-u_0\right)\,, $$ $$ \Phi_2(u_0,u_1,u_2,u_3,u_4,u_{12},u_{13},u_{14}, u_{23},u_{24},u_{34},u_{123},u_{124},u_{134},u_{234})= $$ $$ {1\over 24} \left(u_{1234}-u_{123}-u_{124}-u_{134}-u_{234}+\right. $$ $$ \left. u_{12}+u_{13}+u_{14}+ u_{23}+u_{24}+u_{34}- u_1-u_2-u_3-u_4+u_0\right)\,, $$ and so on: $$ \Phi\left(u_\sigma\right) $$

Let $P=P(\x)$ be an arbitrary function on vecit space $V$. Then consider $$ B_1(\x)=\Phi_1\big\vert_{u_1=P(\x),u_0=P(0)}\,, $$ $$ B_2(\x_1,\x_2)= \Phi_2\big\vert_{u_0=P(0),u_1=P(\x_1),u_2= P(\x_2),u_{12}=P(\x_1+\x_2)}\,, $$ $$ B_3(\x_1,\x_2,\x_3)= $$ $$ \Phi_3\big\vert_{u_1=P(\x_1),u_2= P(\x_2),u_3=P(\x_3), u_{12}=P(\x_1+\x_2), u_{13}=P(\x_1+\x_3), u_{23}=P(\x_2+\x_3), u_{123}=P(\x_1+\x_2+\x_3), }\,, $$ and so on: $$ B_n(\x_1,\dots,\x_n)=\Phi_n(u_{i_1\dots i_k})\big\vert _{u_{j_1\dots j_m}=P(\x_{j_1}+\dots+x_{j_m})} $$

Statement Let $P=P(\x)$ be a polynomial of order $N$ and of minimum degree $n$, i.e. $$ P(\x_1,\dots,x_k)=0 \,\, {\rm if}\,\, k< n\,\, k>N $$ Then $B_k$ vanishes at $k< n$ and $k>N$ and if $k_0$ is minimal such that which produces not zero $B_{k_0}\not=0$ then $B_{k_0}$ is $k_0$-linear.
