HYPOTHESIS



Let $\Phi$ be a function on $V$ such that for arbitrary vectors $\x,\y,\z\in V$, $$ \Phi\left(\x+\y+\z\right)+ \Phi\left(\x\right)+ \Phi\left(\y\right)+ \Phi\left(\z\right)= \Phi\left(\x+\y\right)+ \Phi\left(\x+\z\right)+ \Phi\left(\y+\z\right)+ \Phi\left(0\right)\,. \tag{1} $$ It is evident that this identity hold for $$ \Phi(\x)=C+A-ix^i+Q_{ij}x^ix^j \tag{2} $$

Question Does there exist a function which obeys identity (1) and which has not appearance (2)?

PS Note that The difference of right and left sides of equation (1) defines polarisation $B_3(\x,\y,\z)$ of $\Phi(\x)$. (See Blog on 24 March 2020)
