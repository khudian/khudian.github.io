Periodic Hamiltonian systems



Guillemin, Sternberg, Chapter IV, {${\S}6 $ }





Let $H$ be a Hamiltonian on symplectic manifold $(M,\Omega)$, where in local coordinates $$ \Omega={1\over 2}\omega_{ik}(x)dx^i\wedge dx^k\,, \omega_{ik}=-\omega_{ki}\,,\quad H=H(x)\,. \tag{0.a} $$ Let $\xi=D_H$ be Hamiltonian vector field: $$ \iota_{\xi}\Omega= \Omega {\rfloor}\xi=-dH\,, \tag{0.b} $$ i.e. in local coordinates Hamiltonian vector field $D_H$ defines solutions of differential equations $$ {d x^i(t)\over dt}=\omega^{is}(x){\p H(x)\over \p x^s}\,. \tag{1a} $$ Consider one-parametric family of solutions of this differential equation, i.e. a map from $\R^2$ to $M$, $F\colon x^i=x^i(s,t)$ such that $$ {\p x^i(t,s)\over \p t}= \omega^{is}(x){\p H(x)\over \p x^s}\big\vert_{x^i=x^i(t,s)}\,, \tag{1b} $$

Lemma $$ F^*\Omega=dt\wedge d\bar H $$ where we denote by $\bar H$ the value of $H$ on $\R^2$ $$ \bar H=F^*H=H(x^i(s,t)) $$ Remark $\bar H$ evidently does not depend on $t$, however we will discuss it later (adiabatic invariant???)

Proof: $$ F^*\Omega=F^* \left( {1\over 2}\omega_{ik}(x)dx^i\wedge dx^k \right)= $$ $$ {1\over 2}\omega_{ik} \left( x\left(t,s\right) \right) \left[ {\p x^i(t,s)\over \p t}dt+ {\p x^i(t,s)\over \p s}ds \right] \wedge \left[ {\p x^k(t,s)\over \p t}dt+ {\p x^k(t,s)\over \p s}ds \right]= $$ $$ {1\over 2}\omega_{ik} \left( x\left(t,s\right) \right) \left( {\p x^i(t,s)\over \p t} {\p x^k(t,s)\over \p s} - {\p x^k(t,s)\over \p t} {\p x^i(t,s)\over \p s} \right)dt\wedge ds= $$ $$ \omega_{ik} \left( x\left(t,s\right) \right) {\p x^i(t,s)\over \p t} {\p x^k(t,s)\over \p s} dt\wedge ds= \omega_{ik} \left( x\left(t,s\right) \right) \left[ \omega^{ir}(x){\p H(x(t,s))\over \p x^r} \right] {\p x^k(t,s)\over \p s} dt\wedge ds= $$ $$ - {\p H(x(t,s))\over \p x^k} {\p x^k(t,s)\over \p s} dt\wedge ds =dH\big\vert_{x^i=x^i(t,s)} \wedge dt=d\bar H \wedge dt\,. $$ (Recall that $\bar H=F^*H$).



Now use this lemma to study some features of dynamics.

Suppose that trajectory the family of trajectores $x^i=x^i(t,s)$ (depending on $s$) is periodic: $x^i(t+T(s),s)=x^i(t,s)$.

Denote by $C_s$ the curve $$ C_s\colon x^i_s(t)=x^i(t,s) $$ The period of motion around curve $C_s$ is equal to $T(s)$: $$ x^i_s(t+T(s))=x^i_s(t)\,. $$ Consider $2$-dimensional domain $D$ in $\R^2$ $$ D=\{(t,s)\colon s_1\leq s\leq s_2\,, 0\leq t\leq Т(с)\} $$ The image of this domain under the map $F\colon\, x^i=x^i(t,s)$, $F_*D$ is the piece of cylindre between two 'circle' $C_{s_1}$ and $C_{s_2}$.

Now calculate different 'manifestations' of integral of two form $\Omega$ over domain $D$.

From now on we suppose that $\Omega=d\a$ is exact form. On one hand $$ \int_D F^*\Omega=\int_{F_* D}\Omega\,. $$ and if $\Omega=d\a$ then due to Stokes Theorem it is equal to $$ \int_{F_*D}\Omega=\int_{F_*D} d\a=\int_{\p \left(F_*D\right)}\a= \int_{C_{s_{_2}}}\a -\int_{C_{s_{_1}}}\a $$ On the other hand due to the Lemma $ F^*\Omega=d\bar H\wedge dt\,, $, hence $$ \int_{F_*D}\Omega= \int_D F^*\Omega= \int_D d\bar H \wedge dt= \int_{\p D} t d\bar H $$ The curves $C_{s_{_1}}$ and $C_{s_{_2}}$ do not contibute to these integrals. This is only vericl curve contributes: $$ \int_{F_*D}\Omega= \int_D F^*\Omega= \int_D d\bar H \wedge dt= \int_{\p D} t d\bar H= \int_0^a T(s)H'(s)ds $$ Comparing this equation with previous one we come to conclusion:
