Primer of canonical transformation



Let $H=H(p,q,t)$ be Hamiltonian, and let $F=F(p,q,t)$ be generating function of canonical transformation $$ \pmatrix {p\cr q\cr} \mapsto \pmatrix {\rho\cr Q\cr} \tag{1a} $$ such that $$ pdq-Hdt=\rho dQ-hdt\,, \tag{1b} $$ i.e. $$ p={\p F(q,Q,t)\over \p q}\,, \rho={\p F(q,Q,t)\over \p Q}\,, \tag{1c} $$ and new Hamiltonian $h(\rho,Q,t)$ $$ h(\rho,Q,t)=H(p,q,t)+{ F(q,Q,t)\over \p t}\,. \tag{1d} $$ This is extended canonical transformation: $$ dp_i\wedge dq_i-dE\wedge dt= d\rho_i\wedge dQ_i-dE'\wedge dt\,. \tag{2a} $$ Check straightforwardoy that this canonical transformation transforms solutions of equation $$ \dot p={\p H(p,q,t)\over \p p}\,\quad \dot q=-{\p H(p,q,t)\over \p q}\,. \tag{2b} $$ to the solutions of equation $$ \dot \rho={\p h(\rho,Q,t)\over \p \rho}\,, \tag{2c} $$ and $$ \dot q=-{\p h(\rho,Q,t)\over \p Q}\,. \tag{2d} $$

Check (2c). First consider example



Example $$ H={p^2\over 2m}+mgq\,, F=qQt\,, \tag{3a} $$ we have that $$ \dot q={p\over m}\,,\dot p=-mg \tag{3b} $$ and relation between new coordinates $(\rho,Q)$ and the old $(p,q)$ is the following: $$ \cases { p=Qt\cr \rho=-qt\cr }\,, {\rm i.e.}\,\, \cases { p=Qt\cr q=-{\rho\over t}\cr }\,,\,\, \cases { \rho=-qt\cr Q={p\over t}\cr }\,,\, {\rm and}\,\, h={p^2\over 2m}+mgq+qQ= {Q^2t^2\over 2m}-mg{\rho\over t}-{\rho Q\over t}\,\,, \tag{3c} $$ Equation of motion for coordinates $(\rho,Q)$ according equations (3c) is $$ \dot\rho={d\over dt} \left(-qt\right)=-\dot q t-q= -t{\p H(p,q,t)\over \p p}-q= -{pt\over m}-q=-{Qt^2\over m}+{\rho\over t}\,, $$ $$ \dot Q={d\over dt} \left(p\over t\right)={{\dot p}\over t}-{p\over t^2}= {-{\p H(p,q)\over \p q}\over t}-{Q\over t}= -{mq\over t}-{Q\over t}\,. $$ One can see that they coincide with Hamiltonian equations of motion: $$ \dot\rho=-{\p h(\rho,Q,t)\over \p Q}= -{Qt^2\over m}+{\rho \over t}\,\quad\,, \dot Q={\p h(\rho,Q,t)\over \p \rho}= -{mg\over t}-{Q\over t}\,\,, $$

Now return to general case



Check in general case that for new momenta and coordinates $(\rho,Q)$ canonical Hamiltonian equations in terms of new coordinates $(\rho,Q)$ and new Hamiltonian $h(\rho,Q,t)$ coincide with euqations in terms of coordinates $(p,q)$.

First consider coordinates $\rho$:

In coordinates $(p,q)$, $$ \dot\rho={d\over dt}\left(\rho(p,q,t)\right)= {\p \rho(p,q,t)\over \p p}\dot p + {\p \rho(p,q,t)\over \p q}\dot q + {\p \rho(p,q,t)\over \p t}= $$ $$ {\p \rho(p,q,t)\over \p p} \left(-{\p H(p,q)\over \p q}\right) + {\p \rho(p,q,t)\over \p q} \left({\p H(p,q)\over \p p}\right) + {\p \rho(p,q,t)\over \p t}\,. \tag{5a} $$ On the other hand in coordinates $(\rho,Q)$ $$ \dot\rho=-{\p h(\rho,Q,t)\over \p Q} \tag{5b} $$ It has to be proved that (5a) coincides with (5b). In the same way for $Q$:

In coordinates $(p,q)$, $$ \dot Q={d\over dt}\left(Q(p,q,t)\right)= {\p Q(p,q,t)\over \p p}\dot p + {\p Q(p,q,t)\over \p q}\dot q + {\p Q(p,q,t)\over \p t}= $$ $$ {\p Q(p,q,t)\over \p p} \left(-{\p H(p,q)\over \p q}\right) + {\p Q(p,q,t)\over \p q} \left({\p H(p,q)\over \p p}\right) + {\p Q(p,q,t)\over \p t}\,. \tag{5c} $$ And on the other hand in coordinates $(\rho,Q)$ $$ \dot Q={\p h(\rho,Q)\over \p \rho} \tag{5d} $$ and it has to be proved that (5c) coincides with (5d). Here as usual $$ h\left(\rho,Q,t\right)= H\left(p\left(\rho,Q,t\right), q\left(\rho,Q\right)\right)+ F_t\left(q\left(\rho,Q,t\right), Q,t\right)\,. $$ So, check that (5a) coinsides with (5b) and (5c) coincides with (5d).

We have for (5b) $$ \dot \rho_m= -{\p h(\rho,Q,t)\over \p Q^m} =-{\p\over \p Q^m} \left[H(p,q)+F_t(q,Q) \right]= $$ $$ -{\p\over \p Q^m} \left[H\left(p\left(\rho,Q,t\right), q\left(\rho,Q,t\right) \right)+F_t(q(\rho,Q,t),Q,t) \right]= $$ $$ -H_{p_i}{\p p_i\over \p Q^m} -H_{q^i}{\p q^i\over \p Q^m}- {\p^2 F\over \p t \p Q^m}- {\p^2 F\over \p t \p q^i}{\p q^i(\rho,Q)\over \p Q^m} \tag{5b'} $$ Compare this with (5a). Recall that in (5a) $$ \dot\rho_m= H_{p_i} {\p \rho_m(p,q,t)\over \p q^i} -H_{q^i}{\p \rho_m(p,q,t)\over \p p_i} + {\p \rho_m(p,q,t)\over \p t}\,. \tag{5a'} $$ In the same way we have for (5d) $$ \dot Q^m= {\p h(\rho,Q,t)\over \p \rho_m} ={\p\over \p \rho_m} \left[H(p,q)+F_t(q,Q) \right]= $$ $$ {\p\over \p \rho_m} \left[H\left(p\left(\rho,Q,t\right), q\left(\rho,Q,t\right) \right)+F_t(q(\rho,Q,t),Q,t) \right]= $$ $$ H_{p_i}{\p p_i\over \p \rho_m} +H_{q^i}{\p q^i\over \p \rho_m}+ {\p F\over \p q^i}{\p q^i(\rho,Q,t)\over \p \rho_m} \tag{5d'} $$ Compare this with (5c). Recall that in (5c) $$ \dot Q^m= H_{p_i} {\p Q^m(p,q,t)\over \p q^i} -H_{q^i}{\p Q^m(p,q,t)\over \p p_i} + {\p Q^m(p,q,t)\over \p t}\,. \tag{5c'} $$

${\S} $ $2$ Proof of invariance



Comparing equations (5a) and (5b), we see that $$ {\p \rho_m(p,q,t)\over \p p_i}= {\p q^i(\rho,Q,t)\over \p Q^m}\,, {\p \rho_m(p,q,t)\over \p q^i}= -{\p p_i(\rho,Q,t)\over \p Q^m}\,, $$ and $$ {\p\rho_m(p,q,t)\over \p t}= -{\p^2 F(q,Q,t)\over \p t \p Q^m} -{\p^2 F(q,Q,t)\over \p t \p q^i} {\p q^i(\rho,Q,t)\over \p Q^m} $$ In the same way Comparing equations (5c) and (5d) we see that we have to prove that $$ {\p Q^m(p,q,t)\over \p q^i}={\p p_i(\rho,Q,t)\over \p \rho_m}\,, {\p Q^m(p,q,t)\over \p p_i}= -{\p q^i(\rho,Q,t)\over \p \rho_m}\,. \tag{2.1} $$

The following matrices are engaged:

$$ A=\pmatrix { {\p \over \p p_i}\rho_m\quad {\p \over \p q^j}\rho_m \cr {\p \over \p p_i}Q^m \quad {\p \over \p q^j}Q^m \cr } =\pmatrix { {\p \rho_m(p,q,t)\over \p p_i}\quad {\p \rho_m(p,q,t)\over \p q^j} \cr {\p Q^m(p,q,t)\over \p p_i} \quad {\p Q^m(p,q,t)\over \p q^j} \cr } $$ and $$ B=\pmatrix { {\p \over \p \rho_m} p_i\quad {\p \over \p Q^n}p_i \cr {\p \over \p \rho_m} q^j \quad {\p \over \p Q^n}q^j \cr } = \pmatrix { {\p p_i(\rho,Q,t)\over \p \rho_m} \quad { \p p_i(\rho,Q,t)\over \p Q^n} \cr {\p q^i(\rho,Q)\over \p \rho_m} \quad {\p q^j(\rho,Q)\over \p Q^n} \cr } \tag{2.2} $$

We have to prove the relations (2.1) for matrices (2.2).
