Flux lagrange surface and mixed density



Consider flux of the vector field $$ \R=R^i\p_i. \tag{1} $$ through the $n-1$-dimensional surface in $\R^n$ $$ F=0 \tag{2} $$ which is already can be given by parameterised form $$ x^i=x^i(\xi^\a)\,,\quad,\a=1,\dots,n-1\, \tag{2a}. $$ Assign to this surfsce a Lagrangian surface in $E^{n|n}$ given by gauge fermion $$ \Psi=F\eta $$ It is $$ % L_\Psi\colon \cases { F=0, x^i(\xi^a)\cr x_{i*}={\p \Psi\over \p x^i}= ={\p F\over \p x^i}\eta= F_i\eta\cr }\,\quad {\rm i.e.} \cases { x^i=x^i(\xi)\cr x_{i*} ={\p F\over \p x^i}\eta= F_i(x(\xi))\eta\cr } \tag{3} $$ Later we will assign to an arbitrary semidensity $\ss$ the density on the Lagrangian surface (3), and we integrate this semidensity over Lagrangian surface in the following way. If $L$ is $(n-1|1)$-dimensional lagrangian surface given by parameterisation $x(\xi^1,\dots,\xi^{n-1},\eta)$ and $\E_\a$ is a tangent vectors corresponding to parameter $\xi^\a$, ($\a=1,\dots,n-1$), $\f$ is a tangent vector corresponding to the odd parameter $\tau$, then we come to the following formula for the integral $$ \int_{F=0}\R=\int\rho_L \left( x(\xi,\eta)\,, x_*(\xi,\eta); \E_1,\dots, \E_{n-1},\f \right) D(\xi,\eta)\,, \tag{xxx} $$ where $D(\xi,\tau)$ is coordinate volume form.

Perform calculations.





Consider tangent basis $\{\E_\a,\f\}$ to this lagrangian surface. This basis span $(n-1|1)$-dimenisonal vector space. $$ \cases { \E_\a= {\p x^i(\xi,\eta)\over \p \xi^\a} {\p\over \p x^i} +{\p x_{*i}(\xi,\eta)\over \p \xi^\a} {\p\over \p x_{*i}} \,,\cr \f= {\p x^i(\xi,\eta)\over \p \eta} {\p\over \p x^i} +{\p x_{*i}(\xi,\eta)\over \p \eta} {\p\over \p x_{*i}} =F_i(x) {\p\over \p x_{*i}} \cr } $$ and consider the self-dual basis $\{\E_\a,{\bf F},\e^\beta,\f\}$, i.e. a basis in $E^{n|n}$ such that $$ \cases { \omega(\E_\a,\E_\beta)=0\,, \omega(\E_\a,{\bf F})=0\,, \omega(\E_\a,\e^\beta)=\delta_\a^\beta\,, \omega(\E_\a,\f)=0\,, \cr \omega({\bf F},{\bf F})=0\,, \omega({\bf F},\e^\beta)=0\,, \omega({\bf F},\f)=1\,,\cr \omega(\e^\a,\e^\beta)=1\,, \omega(\e^\a ,\f)=0\,,\cr \omega(\f ,\f)=0\,.\cr } \tag{2} $$ Many of these relations are fullfilled automatically. In any rate we may choose $$ \cases { \E_\a= {\p x^i(\xi,\eta)\over \p \xi^\a} {\p\over \p x^i}+\dots \,,\cr {\bf F} ={F_i(x) {\p\over \p x^i}\over p} \cr \e^\beta= { {\p x^i(\xi,\eta)\over \p \xi^\beta} \over q} {\p\over \p x_{*i}}\,,\cr \f =F_i(x) {\p\over \p x_{*i}}\,, } $$ where we choose constants $p$ and $q$ such that $$ p={\p F(x)\over \p x^i} {\p F(x)\over \p x^j} \delta^{ij} $$ and $$ q\delta^{\a\beta}={\p x^i(\xi)\over \p \xi^\a} {\p x^j(\xi)\over \p \xi^\beta} \delta_{ij} \tag{4} $$ Of course here the second condition put the additional restriction on the surface. (E.g. it cannot be obeyed for surface with zero gaussian curvature). )We sssume later that this condition is dullfilled. . Now consider half-density corresponding to vector field (1) $$ \ss=\ss_\R=R^ix_{*i}= (Ax_*+ By_*+Cz_*)\sqrt {D(x,y,z,x_*,y_*,z_*)}\,. $$ This semidensity induces density $\rho_L$ on the lagrangian surface: $$ \rho_L( x^i(\xi,\eta), x_{*i}(\xi,\eta) \E_1,\dots,\E_{n-1},\f)= $$ $$ \ss_\R\left( x^i(\xi,\eta)\,, x_{*i}(\eta,\eta),; \E_1,\dots,\E_{n-1}, {\bf F}\,,\e^1,\dots, \e^{n-1},\f\right)= $$ $$ R^i\left(x(\xi,\eta)\right) {\p F\over \p x^i}\big\vert _{x(\xi,\eta)} \times $$ $$ \sqrt{ {\rm Ber} \pmatrix { &\E_x &\E_y &\E_{x_{*}} &\E_{y_{*}}\cr &{\bf F}_x &{\bf F}_y &{\bf F}_{x_*} &{\bf F}_{y_*}\cr &\e_x & \e_y &\e_{x^*} &\e_{y^*}\cr &\f_x & \f_y &\f_{x^*} &\f_{y^*}\cr } } \sqrt {D(x,y,x_*,y_*)} \left( {\p\over \p x}, {\p\over \p y}, {\p\over \p x_*}, {\p\over \p y_*} \right)= $$ (The rest of caclulation are done for the flux of vector field through the curve in $\R^2$) $$ R^i\left(x(\xi,\eta)\right) {\p F\over \p x^i}\eta\big\vert _{x(\xi,\eta)\eta } \sqrt{ {\rm Ber} \pmatrix { &x_\xi& y_\xi &F_{xx}x_\xi+F_{xy}y_\xi &F_{yx}x_\xi+F_{yy}y_\xi\cr &{F_x\over F_x^2+F_y^2} &{F_y\over F_x^2+F_y^2} &0 &0\cr &0 & 0 &{x_\xi\over x_\xi^2+y_\xi^2} &{y_\xi\over x_\xi^2+y_\xi^2} \cr &0 & 0 &F_x& F_y\cr } }= $$ $$ R^i\left(x(\xi,\eta)\right) {\p F\over \p x^i}\eta\big\vert _{x(\xi,\eta)\eta } \sqrt{ { \det \pmatrix { &x_\xi& y_\xi\cr &{F_x\over F_x^2+F_y^2} &{F_y\over F_x^2+F_y^2} } \over \det \pmatrix { &{x_\xi\over x_\xi^2+y_\xi^2} &{x_\xi\over x_\xi^2+y_\xi^2}\cr &F_x &F_y } }} $$ $$ R^i\left(x(\xi,\eta)\right) {\p F\over \p x^i}\eta\big\vert _{x(\xi,\eta)\eta } { \sqrt {x_\xi^2+y_\xi^2} \over \sqrt {F_x^2+F_y^2} } $$

Now calculate the integral of density $\rho_L$ over the circle $F(x,y)=0$ and compare it with the flux of $\R$.

Suppose that $F=y-f(x)$. Consider the vector field $\R=A\p_x+B\p_y$ To this vector fielf corresponds semidensity $\ss=Ax_*+By_*$.

Calculate the integral of density $\rho_L$ associated with semidensity $\ss$ over the curve $F=y-f(x)$.

$$ \int_{F=0} \rho_L=\int\rho_L\left(x \left(\xi,\eta\right)\, \E,\f\right) D(\xi,\eta)= \int R^i\left(x(\xi,\eta)\right) {\p F\over \p x^i}\eta\big\vert _{x(\xi,\eta)} { \sqrt {x_\xi^2+y_\xi^2} \over \sqrt {F_x^2+F_y^2} }D(\xi,\eta)\,. $$ Now taking in account that $F=y-f(x)$ and $\cases {x=\xi\cr y=f(\xi)\cr}$ we simplify the last expression $$ \int R^i\left(x(\xi,\eta)\right) {\p F\over \p x^i}\big\vert _{x(\xi,\eta)\eta } { \sqrt {x_\xi^2+y_\xi^2} \over \sqrt {F_x^2+F_y^2} }D\xi = \int R^i\left(x(\xi,\eta)\right) {\p F\over \p x^i}\big\vert _{x(\xi,\eta)\eta } D\xi $$ $$ =\int (AF_x+BF_y)D\xi= \int (B-Af_x)D\xi=\int Bdx-Ady\,. $$ This is equal to the flux:

$$ \int_F=0\R=\int *\R=\int *\left(A\p_x+B\p_x\right)= \int Bdx-Ady\,. $$
