Natural construction of groupoid



Let $M$ be a (super)manifold Let $A$ be a trivial groupoid of volume forms on $M$.

Let $\rho$ be a volume form, $S^{ab}\p_a\otimes\p_b$ be symmetric contravariant tensor field on $M$, and let $\Pi(\rho,\lambda)$ be a pencil of operators acting on densities of weight $\lambda$ auch that



We know that $\lambda={1\over2}$ is a singular point of this pencil.

Consider set of pairs $(\rho,\rho')$ such that for this pair operators at the singular points, i.e. on semidensities coinside at the points $\rho$ and $\rho'$: $$ \Pi\left(\rho,{1\over 2}\right)\equiv \Pi\left(\rho',{1\over 2}\right) \tag{1} $$

In the case if $$ \Pi(\rho,\lambda) s(x)|Dx|^\lambda= \rho^\lambda\Delta_\rho\left({s(x)}\over \rho^\lambda\right) $$ condition (1)reads $$ \sqrt\rho\Delta_\rho\left({s(x)}\over \sqrt \rho\right)= \sqrt{\rho'}\Delta_{\rho'}\left({s(x)}\over \sqrt {\rho'}\right)\,. \tag{2} $$

Thus we come to the groupoid $C_S$. Let a pair $(\rho,\rho')\in C_S$. i.e. $$ \sqrt\rho\Delta_\rho\left({s}\over \sqrt \rho\right) \equiv \sqrt\rho'\Delta_{\rho'}\left({s}\over \sqrt {\rho'}\right)\,. \tag{3} $$ In particular relation (3) is obeyed for semidensities $s=\sqrt {\rho'}$ and $s=\sqrt {\rho}$ . Applying relation (3) for these semidensities and using that $\Delta_\rho 1=0$ we come to the relation that for arbitrary pair $(\rho,\rho')\in C_S$. $$ \hbox {for arbitrary $(\rho,\rho')\in C_S$}\quad \Delta_\rho\left( \sqrt {\rho'\over \rho} \right)=0 \Leftrightarrow \Delta_{\rho'}\left( \sqrt {\rho\over \rho'} \right)=0 \quad \hbox{inverse} $$ In the same way consider two conjugate pairs $(\rho_1,\rho_2)\in C_S$ and $(\rho_2,\rho_3)\in C_S$.

Relation (3) reads

$$ \sqrt{\rho_1}\Delta_{\rho_1} \left({s(x)}\over \sqrt {\rho_1}\right)= \sqrt{\rho_2}\Delta_{\rho_2} \left({s(x)}\over \sqrt {\rho_2}\right)= \sqrt{\rho_3}\Delta_{\rho_3} \left({s(x)}\over \sqrt {\rho_3}\right) \tag{4} $$

Using it and in particular the fact that for semidensity $\s=\sqrt {\rho_3}$ all terms in equation (4) vanish, we come to the relation $$ \Delta_{\rho_1} \left( \sqrt {\rho_3\over \rho_1} \right)=0= \Delta_{\rho_1} \left( \sqrt {\rho_3\over \rho_2} \sqrt {\rho_2\over \rho_1} \right)\,. $$ Thus we checked that for arbitrary two conjugate pairs $(\rho_1,\rho_2)$, $(\rho_2,\rho_3)$ belonging to the groupoid $C_S$, composition law $$ \Delta_{\rho_1} \left( \sqrt {\rho_3\over \rho_1} \right)=0\,. $$ holds.
