Tchebyshof polinomials (continuation: see 22 September)



There are not many formulae which are deep and almost evident. Good example of such a formula is the famous Cauchi-Buniakovskij inequality: $$ \langle u,u\rangle \langle v,v\rangle \geq \langle u,v\rangle^2 $$

We call this type formula fundamental

To Russian mathematician Tchebyshof belong at least two fundamental formulae.

One is about famous Chebyshov polinomials $T_n(x)\colon \cases {T_n(x)=\cos n\theta \cr x=\cos\theta\cr } $

One can see that polinomial ${T_n(x)\over 2^n}=x^n+\dots$ is on the minimal distance on $[-1.1]$. i.e. for every $x\in [-1,1]$ $|T_n(x)|\leq 1$ and for an arbitrary polinomial $P(x)=x^n+\sum_{i< n} a_i x^i$ there exists a point $x_0\in [-1,1]$ such that $$ |P_n(x_0)|={1\over 2^n} $$ Another fundamental formula which belongs to Chebyshov is related with probability.

Let $X_1$, \dots, $X_n$ be independent random variables which take values $0$ or $1$ such that every $X_i$ it takes value $0$ with probability $p$ and respectively it takes value $1$ with probability $q=1-p$.

The famous Bernulli law says that $$ \lim_{n\to\infty} P\left( \left| {X_1+\dots+X_n\over n}-p \right|>\vare \right)=0\,. $$ This follows from the fact that $$ P\left( \{X_1+\dots+X_n= k\} \right)=C^k_np^k(1-p)^{n-k}\,. $$
