Consider the space $\R^{2n}$ with coordinates $(p_1,\dots,p_n;q^1,\dots,q^n)$

Consider the surface $$ \L=\L_\Psi=\{x^i,y^a, p_i,q_a\colon\quad p_i={\p\Psi(x,y)\over \p x^i}\,, \quad q_a={\p \Psi(x,y)\over \p y^a}=0\}\,. \tag{1} $$ in the space $\R^{2n}$. If $$ {\p^2\over \p x^i\p y^a}\not=0\, $$ this is $n$-dimensional surface Show that this is Lagrange surface: $$ dp_i\wedge dx^i= d\left({\p\Psi(x,y)\over \p x^i}\right)\wedge dx^i= {\p^2\Psi(x,y)\over \p x^j\p x^i}dx^j\wedge dx^i+ {\p^2\Psi(x,y)\over \p y^a\p x^i}dy^a\wedge dx^i= $$ $$ 0+{\p^2\Psi(x,y)\over \p x^i\p y^a}dy^a\wedge dx^i= 0+{\p q_a\over \p x^i}dy^a\wedge dx^i=0\,. $$ Je ne peux pas me souvenir plus de cette cas!
