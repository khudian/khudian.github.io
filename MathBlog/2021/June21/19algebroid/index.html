BV algebroid.



Recall BV groupoid of odd Poisson manifold.

The objets are non-vanishing volume forms, and arrows are $$ \rho {\buildrel J \over \rightarrow} \rho' \quad {\rm such\,\, that}\, \Delta_\rho J=0\,, {\rm where} J={\rho'\over \rho}\,. $$ (One can consider connections instead volume forms.) Construct the Lie algebroid.

For BV groupoid the Lie algebroid is the vector bundle over the (infinite-dimensional) manifold $Vol^\times (M)$ of volume forms. The fiber over volume form $\rho$ is the vector space of slutions of equation $$ \Delta_\rho F=0 \tag{1} $$ where $F\in C^\infty(M)$. The anchor is tautological: it sends a function $F$ to s the infinitesimal shift $$ \rho\to \rho+\vare\rho F $$ A section of this bundle is a functional $F=F[\rho]$ of non-vanishing volume forms with values in functions on $M$, such that for each $\rho=\rho(x)Dx$, the above equation (1???) is satisfied.

The Lie bracket is the restriction of the canoncial commutator of vector fields on $Vol^\times$ and can be expressed by the explicit formula: $$ [F,G][\rho,x]=\int_M D(y)\rho(y) \left( F\left[\rho,y\right] {\delta G\left[\rho,x\right]\over \delta\rho(y)} - G\left[\rho,y\right] {\delta F\left[\rho,x\right]\over \delta\rho(y)} \right) $$ Here we write $F[\rho,x]$ for the value of (function) $F[\rho]$ at the point $x\in M$. (There are extra signs for super...)
