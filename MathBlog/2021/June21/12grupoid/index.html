Groupoid of Batalin Vilkovisky



We know that for every second order operator of weight $\delta$ there is a pencil of operators $\{\Delta_\sigma\}$ which passes through this operator [My work with Ted of 2012]. Moreover for special values of $\sigma$ there are many operators passing through the given operator. This comes from the following selfadjoint pencil : $$ \Delta={t^{\delta}\over 2} \left[ S^{ab}\p_b\p_a+\p_bS^{ba}\p_a+ \left(2\hat \lambda+\delta-1\right)\gamma^a\p_a+ \hat\lambda\p_a\gamma^a+ \hat\lambda\left( \hat\lambda+\delta-1 \right)\theta \right]\,, \tag{1} $$ acting on algebra of densities of all weights. Here $t=Dx$, $\gamma_a$ is the connection on volume forms, ($\gamma^a=S^{ab}\gamma_b$. One can consider a special case if this connection is produced by volume form. We will come to the flat connection: $$ \gamma_a=-\p_a\log\rho_0 \tag{2} $$ for the given volume form $\rho_0$. In this casse for arbitrary volume form $\rho$ $$ \nabla_\X \rho=X^a(\p_a\rho+\gamma_a\rho)=\rho_0\p_\X \left(\rho\over\rho_0\right) $$ In (1) $$ \theta=\gamma_a\gamma^a \,, $$ and $$ \hat \lambda=t{\p \over \p t} $$ However we return to the pencil (1). Let $$ L= |Dx|^\delta \left[ E^{ab}\p_a\p_b+T^a\p_a+U(x) \right] \tag{3} $$ be an arbitrary second order operator of the weight $\delta$ acting on densities of the weight $\lambda$ (i.e. transforming them to the densities of the weight $\mu=\lambda+\delta$).

Ask a question: $$ \hbox{Does the pencil cross the operator $L$ in equation (3)?} $$ To have this it has to be obeyed: $$ \cases { E^{ab}=S^{ab}\cr T^a=(2\lambda+\delta-1)\gamma^a\cr U=\lambda\p_a\gamma^a+ \lambda(\lambda+\delta-1)\theta\cr } $$: We see that in the case if main symbol of operator $L$ coincides with the $S$ and $$ \cases{ 2\lambda+\delta-1\not =0\cr \lambda\not=0\cr \lambda+\delta-1\not =0\cr } \tag{4} $$ then the pencil $\Delta\left( t^\delta S^{ik},gamma\right)$ with suitable parameters passes through the operator $L$ in (3). What happened if equation (4) is not obeyed?

Consider the case if $(2\lambda+\delta-1)=0$, i.e. operator $\Delta$ acts on densities of the weight $\lambda={1-\delta\over 2}$ abd takes values in densities of the weight $\mu=\lambda+\delta={1+\delta\over 2}$.

Consider the following groupoids:

a) groupoid $C$--an abelian groupoid of connections on volume forms on manifold $M$. Groupoid $C_S$ is associated with principal symbol $S^{ab}(x)Dx^\delta$. b) groupoid $C^1$--an abelian groupoid of volume forms (to every volume form corresponds the connection $\gamma_a= -\p_a\log \rho$) a') groupoid $C_S$--subgroupoid of $C$ of all arrows $\gamma\arrow \gamma'$ such that $$ \Delta (S,\gamma)=\Delta (S,\gamma') $$

b') groupoid $C^1_S$--subgroupoid of $C^1$ of all arrows $\rho\arrow \rho'$ such that $$ \Delta (S,\gamma)=\Delta (S,\gamma') $$ where $\gamma=-\p_a\log\rho$, $\gamma'=-\p_a\log\rho'$.
