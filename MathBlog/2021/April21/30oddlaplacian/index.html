Invariance of odd Laplacian



We study here transformations which preserve the operator $$ \Delta f={\p^2 f\over \p x^i\p \theta_i}\,. $$ This operator is an operator which can be constructed by odd symplectic form and volume form $\rho$ We choose here $\rho$ to be a coordinate form, and coordinates $(x^1,\dots x^n,\theta_1,\dots,\theta_n)$ are Darboux coordinates of the symplectic structure

I want to prove the theorem:

Theorem Let $F$ be a transformation of superspace $E^{n|n}$ which preserves the operator $\Delta_0$. Then $F$ is a canonical transformation, and there exists a number $\lambda$ such that $F\circ F_\lambda$ is unimodular symplectic transformation.

A much weaker statement is the following:

Let $Q$ be a hamiltonian such that is generating the canonical infinitesimal transformation. This transformation preserves $\Delta_0$ iff $\Delta_0 Q=constant$.

This weaker statement follows from

Proof of the Theorem

Use the espression of bracket in terms of $\Delta$-operator: $$ \Delta_0 \{f,g\}=\{\Delta_0 f,g\}+(-1)^{p(f)}\{f,g\}+ \{ f \Delta_0 g\}+(-1)^{p(f)}\{f,g\} $$

It follows from this equation that under transformation non-degenerate symplectic structure is preserved.
