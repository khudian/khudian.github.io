The base of odd symplectic



There is the symplectic structure in $E^{n|n}$. It defines symplectoorthogonal product which is defined by the condition that coordinates $x^m$ are conjugate to $\theta_n$ \footnote{$^1)$}{the condition of closedness and not generacy of two form are covered by condition of Darboux coordinates existnesse}: $$ \Omega\left( {\p\over \p x^m}, {\p\over \p\theta_n}\right) =\delta_m^n\,. \langle {\p\over \p x^m}, {\p\over \p\theta_n}\rangle=\delta_m^n\,. $$ The 'scalar product' is superamtisymmetric $$ \langle\X, \Y\rangle(-1)= \langle\Y, \X\rangle(-1)^{p(\X)p(\Y)}\,, $$ and linear $$ \langle\X \lambda, \Y\rangle= \langle\X, \lambda\Y\rangle\,, \langle\lambda\X+\mu{\bf Z}, \Y\rangle= \lambda\langle\X, \Y\rangle + \mu\langle\Z, \Y\rangle\,, $$ To every function $f$ corresponds the vector field $D_f$ : $$ D_f= {\p f\over \p x^i}{\p\over \p\theta_i} + (-1)^{p(f)} {\p f\over \p \theta_i}{\p\over\p x^i} $$ such that for arbitrary vector field $\X$ $$ \langle\X,D_f\rangle=\X f\,. \tag{1} $$ Check it: $$ \langle\X,D_f\rangle= \left\langle X^i{\p \over \p x^i}+ X_m{\p \over \p \theta_m} \,,\, {\p f\over \p x^j}{\p\over \p\theta_j} + (-1)^{p(f)} {\p f\over \p \theta_j}{\p\over\p x^j} \right\rangle= $$ $$ \left\langle X^i{\p \over \p x^i} \,,\, {\p f\over \p x^j}{\p\over \p\theta_j} \right\rangle +\left\langle X_m{\p \over \p \theta_m} \,,\, (-1)^{p(f)} {\p f\over \p \theta_n}{\p\over\p x^n} \right\rangle $$ $$ X^i{\p f\over \p x^j}\left\langle {\p \over \p x^i} \,,\, {\p\over \p\theta_j} \right\rangle +X_m{\p f\over \p \theta_n} (-1)^{p(f)+(p(f)+1)1} \left\langle {\p \over \p \theta_m} \,,\, {\p\over\p x^n} \right\rangle $$ $$ = X^i{\p f\over \p x^j}\delta_i^j -X_m{\p f\over \p \theta_n} (-\delta^m_n)= X^i{\p f\over \p x^i}+ X_m{\p f\over \p \theta_m} =\X f\,. $$ It preserves the symplectic structure: $$ \L_{D_f}\langle\rangle=0\,. $$ This property which sometimes is called the softness of symplectic structure (or rigidness of Riemannian structure) essentially differes the symplectic structure from Riemannian.

We will recall also the Buttin bracket: $$ \{f,g\}=\langle D_f,D_g\rangle= \left\langle {\p f\over \p x^i}{\p\over \p\theta_i} + (-1)^{p(f)} {\p f\over \p \theta_i}{\p\over\p x^i} \,\,,\,\, {\p g\over \p x^m}{\p\over \p\theta_m} + (-1)^{p(g)} {\p g\over \p \theta_m}{\p\over\p x^m} \right \rangle= $$ $$ {\p f\over \p x^i}\left\langle {\p\over \p\theta_i} \,\,,\,\, (-1)^{p(g)} {\p g\over \p \theta_m}{\p\over\p x^m} \right \rangle+ \left\langle (-1)^{p(f)} {\p f\over \p \theta_i}{\p\over\p x^i} \,\,,\,\, {\p g\over \p x^m}{\p\over \p\theta_m} \right \rangle= $$ $$ {\p f\over \p x^i}{\p g\over \p \theta_m} (-1)^{p(g)+(p(g)+1)1} \left\langle {\p\over \p\theta_i} \,\,,\,\, {\p\over\p x^m} \right \rangle+ {\p f\over \p \theta_i} {\p g\over \p x^m} (-1)^{p(f)} \left\langle {\p\over\p x^i} \,\,,\,\, {\p\over \p\theta_m} \right \rangle= $$ $$ {\p f\over \p x^i}{\p g\over \p \theta_m} (-1) (-\delta^i_m) + {\p f\over \p \theta_i} {\p g\over \p x^m} (-1)^{p(f)} \delta^i_m= {\p f\over \p x^i}{\p g\over \p \theta_i} + (-1)^{p(f)} {\p f\over \p \theta_i} {\p g\over \p x^i}\,. $$
