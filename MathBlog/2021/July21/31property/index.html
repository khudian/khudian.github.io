One propety of odd symplectic vector space



Let $L^{n-k|k}$ be a lagrangian plane in $E^{n|n}$ Suppose $$ \{\E_1,\dots,\E_{n-k},\f^1.\dots,f^k\} $$ be a basis in this plane. Assign to this basis the basis $$ \{\E_1,\dots,\E_{n-k},,{\bf F}_1,\dots,{\bf F}_k; \e^1,\dots,\e^{n-k},\f^1.\dots,f^k\} $$ in the whole space $\E^{n|n}$ such that the basis is self-dual: $$ \cases { \omega (\E_i,\E_j)=0\,, \omega (\E_i, {\bf F_j})=0\,, \omega (\E_i,\e^j)=\delta_i^j\,, \omega (\E_i,\f^j)=0\,,\cr \omega({\bf F}_i,{\bf F}_j)=0\,, \omega({\bf F}_i,\e^j)=0\,, \omega({\bf F}_i,\f^j)=\delta_i^j\,,\cr \omega (\e^i,\e^j)= \omega (\e^i,\f^j)=0\cr \omega (\f^i,\f^j)=0\cr } $$

some of these relations are automatically fulfilled, e.g. $\omega (\E_i,\f^j)=0$ (since $L$ is a lagrangian plane.)



Do it first for the case $k=1,n=2$. Let $\{\E,\f\}$ be a basis in $L$ and let $\{\E,{\bf F},\e,\f\}$ be a basis in $E^{2|2}$ such that the basis $\{\E,{\bf F},\e,\f\}$ is self-dual $$ \cases { \omega (\E,\E)=0\,, \omega (\E, {\bf F})=0\,, \omega (\E,\e)=1\,, \omega (\E,\f)=0\,,\cr \omega({\bf F},{\bf F})=0\,, \omega({\bf F},\e)=0\,, \omega({\bf F},\f)=1\,,\cr \omega (\e,\e)= \omega (\e,\f)=0\cr \omega (\f,\f)=0\cr } $$ some of these relations are automatically fulfilled Consider new basis $\{\E',\f'\}$ in $L$ $$ \cases { \E'=a\E+\beta \f\cr \f'=\gamma\E+d\f\cr } $$

Try to define the new self-dual basis in $E^{2|2}$, $\E', {\bf F}', \e',\f'$ adjusted to this basis: We try $$ \cases { {\bf F}'=p{\bf F}+\pi \e\cr \e'=\rho {\bf F}+r\e\cr } $$

Then $$ \cases { 0=\omega (\E',\E')=\omega(a\E+\beta \f,a\E+\beta \f)=0\cr 0= \omega (\E', {\bf F}')= \omega(a\E+\beta\f,p{\bf F}+\pi \e)=a\pi-p\beta=0 \cr 1= \omega (\E',\e')=\omega(a\E+\beta\f,\rho {\bf F}+r\e) =ar-\beta\rho=1,,\cr 0= \omega (\E',\f')=\omega (a\E+\beta\f,\gamma\E+d\f)= ad+\beta\gamma=0 \,,\cr 0= \omega({\bf F}',{\bf F}')=\omega(p{\bf F}+\pi\e,p{\bf F}+\pi\e)=0\cr 0= \omega({\bf F}',\e')=0= \omega(p{\bf F}+\pi\e,\rho {\bf F}+r\e)=0\cr 1= \omega({\bf F}',\f')=\omega(p{\bf F}+\pi\e,\gamma\E+d\f)= pd-\pi\gamma\,,\cr 0= \omega (\e',\e')=0\cr 0= \omega (\e',\f')= \omega (\rho {\bf F}+r\e, \gamma \E+d\f) =\rho d-r\gamma\cr 0=\omega (\f,\f)=0\cr } $$ We come to conditions $$ \cases { 0=\omega (\E', {\bf F}')=0 \Rightarrow a\pi-p\beta=0\cr 1=\omega (\E',\e')\Rightarrow ar-\beta\rho=1\,,\cr 1= \omega({\bf F}',\f')\Rightarrow pd-\pi\gamma=1\,,\cr 0= \omega (\e',\f')\Rightarrow\rho d-r\gamma\cr } $$
