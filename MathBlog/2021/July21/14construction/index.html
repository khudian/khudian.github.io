Flux and Lagrange surface



Consider surface $$ z=f(x^i) \tag{1a} $$ Assing to this surface the gauge fermion $$ \Psi=(z-f(x^1,\dots, x^n))\eta \tag{1b} $$ and the Lagrange surface $$ L_\Psi\colon \cases { {\p \Psi\over \p \eta}\equiv =0\cr \theta_i={\p \Psi\over \p x^i}\cr \pi={\p \Psi\over \p z}\cr } $$ This will be $(n|1)$-dimensional Lagrange surface: (Even coordinates are $z,x^1,\dots, x^n$ odd coordinates are $\pi,\theta_1,\dots,\theta_n$.) $$ L_\Psi\colon \cases { x^i=\xi^i \cr z=f(\xi^i)\cr \theta_i=-f_i(\xi)\eta\cr \pi=\eta\cr } $$ where we denote $f_i={\p f\over \p \xi^i}$.

Consider $(n|1)$ tangent reper $$ \{\E_i\,,\f\}\,,\quad (i=1,\dots,n)\, $$ to this surface, where even vectors are $$ \E_i={\p\r\over \p\xi^i}{\p\over \p \r}= {\p\over \p x^i}+f_i(\xi){\p\over \p z} -f_{im}\eta{\p\over \p \theta_m} \tag{1f} $$ and odd vector is $$ \f=-f_i(\xi){\p \over \p \theta_i}+{\p\over \p\pi} $$



There is the symplectic structure in $E^{n+1|n+1}$ which defines symplectoorthogonal product which is defined by the condition that coordinates $x^m$ are conjugate to $\theta_n$, coordinate $z$ is conjugate to $\pi$, $$ \langle\X, \Y\rangle= \langle\Y, \X\rangle(-1)^{p(\X)p(\Y)}\,, $$ (see in detail e.g. blog on 15 July) We have in $E^{n+1|n+1}$ the dual reper, $$ \{\E_i,\f;\e^j,{\bf F}\}= \{\E_1\,\dots,\E_n,\f;\e^1,\dots,\e^m,{\bf F}\}\,, $$ i.e. the reper such that first $n+1$ vectors coincide with tangent reper $\{\E_i,\f;\}$= to the Lagrangian surface and the rest is 'symplectoorthonormal': $$ \langle\E_i,\e^j\rangle=\delta_i^j\,, \langle {\bf F},\f\rangle=1\,, $$ $$ \langle \E_i, \E_j,\rangle= \langle \E_i, \f\rangle= \langle\E_i,{\bf F}\rangle= \langle\f,\f\rangle= \langle\f,\e^i\rangle= \langle\e^i,\e^j\rangle= \langle\e^i,{\bf F}\rangle=0\,. \langle {\bf F},{\bf F}\rangle=0\,. $$

In details: $$ \langle\E_i,\E_j\rangle=0\,, \langle\E_i,\f\rangle=0\,, \langle\f,\f\rangle=0\,, \langle {\bf F},{\bf F}\rangle=0\,, \tag{2a} $$ $$ \langle\E_i,\e^j\rangle=\delta_i^j\,, \tag{2b} $$ $$ \langle\E_i,{\bf F}\rangle=0\,, \tag{2c} $$ $$ \langle \f,\e^i\rangle=0\,, \tag{2d} $$ $$ \langle \f,{\bf F}\rangle=1\,, \tag{2e} $$ $$ \langle \e^i,\e^j\rangle=0\,, \tag{2f} $$ $$ \langle \e^i, {\bf F}\rangle=0\,, \tag{2g} $$





(We assume that vectors $\E_1\,,\dots\,,\E_n,{\bf F}$ are even and vectors $\e_1\,,\dots\,\e_n,\f$ are odd.) Solve equation (2). Suppose that $$ \e^i=\a^{im}{\p\over \p x^m}+ \gamma^{i}{\p\over \p z}+ A^{i}_m{\p\over \p \theta_m} +B^{i}{\p\over \p \pi} $$ and $$ {\bf F}=C^m{\p\over \p x^m}+ D{\p\over \p z}+ \beta_m{\p\over \p \theta_m}+ \delta{\p\over \p \pi}\,, $$ where vectors $\e^i$ are odd, i.e. coefficients $\a^{im}$ and $\gamma^i$ are odd and coefficients $A^i_m$ and $B^i$ are even and vector $\bf F$ is even, i.e. coefficients $C^m$ and $D$ are even and coefficients $\beta_m$ and $\delta$ are odd.

Put this in equation (2).

Note that conditions (2a) are fullfilled automatically since $L$ is $(n|1)$-dimensional Lagrangian surface, and also $\langle \X,\rangle\Y$= $-\langle \X,\Y\rangle$ if one of these vectors is even. Condition (2b) means that $$ \langle\E_i,\e^j\rangle=\delta_i^j= \left \langle {\p\over \p x^i}+f_i(\xi){\p\over \p z} -f_{im}\eta{\p\over \p \theta_m}\,, \a^{jn}{\p\over \p x^n}+ \gamma^{j}{\p\over \p z}+ A^{j}_n{\p\over \p \theta_n} +B^{j}{\p\over \p \pi} \right\rangle= $$ $$ A^j_i+f_i(\xi)B^j+f_{im}(\xi)\a^{jm}\eta=\delta_i^j\,, \tag{3b} $$

Condition (2c) means that $$ \langle\E_i,{\bf F}\rangle= \left \langle {\p\over \p x^i}+f_i(\xi){\p\over \p z} -f_{im}(\xi)\eta{\p\over \p \theta_m}\,, C^m{\p\over \p x^m}+D{\p \over\p z} +\beta_m{\p\over \p \theta_m} +\delta{\p\over \p \pi} \right \rangle= $$ $$ \left \langle {\p\over \p x^i} \,, \beta_m{\p\over \p \theta_m} \right \rangle+ \left \langle f_i(\xi){\p\over \p z} \,, \delta{\p\over \p \pi} \right \rangle- \left \langle f_{im}\eta{\p\over \p \theta_m}\,, C^m{\p\over \p x^m} \right \rangle= $$ $$ \beta_i+f_i(\xi)\delta-f_{im}(\xi)C^m\eta=0\,. \tag{3c} $$ Condition (2d) means that $$ 0= \langle \f,\e^i\rangle= \left\langle -f_i(\xi){\p \over \p \theta_i}+{\p\over \p\pi}\,,\, \a^{im}{\p\over \p x^m}+ \gamma^{i}{\p\over \p z}+ A^{i}_m{\p\over \p \theta_m} +B^{i}{\p\over \p \pi} \right\rangle= $$ $$ \left\langle -f_i(\xi){\p \over \p \theta_i}\,,\, \a^{im}{\p\over \p x^m} \right\rangle+ \left\langle {\p\over \p\pi}\,,\, \gamma^{i}{\p\over \p z} \right\rangle= -f_m(\xi)\a^{im}+\gamma^i\,. \tag{3d} $$ Condition (2e) means that

$$ \langle {\bf F},\f\rangle=1= \left \langle A^m{\p\over \p x^m}+ A{\p\over \p z}+ \beta_m{\p\over \p \theta_m}+ \beta{\p\over \p \pi}\,, -f_i(\xi){\p \over \p \theta_i}+{\p\over \p\pi} \right \rangle= $$ $$ -A^m f_m(\xi)+A=1\,. \tag{3e} $$

Condition (2f) means that $$ \langle \e^i,\e^j\rangle=0= $$ $$ \left\langle \a^{im}{\p\over \p x^m}+ \a^{i}{\p\over \p z}+ A^{i}_m{\p\over \p \theta_m} +A^{i}{\p\over \p \pi} \,, \a^{jn}{\p\over \p x^n}+ \a^{j}{\p\over \p z}+ A^{j}_n{\p\over \p \theta_n} +A^{j}{\p\over \p \pi} \right\rangle= $$ $$ \a^{im}A^j_m+ \a^{jm}A^i_m+ \a^iA^j+ \a^jA^i=0 \tag{3f} $$

Condition (2g) means that $$ \langle e^i,{\bf F}\rangle=0= \left \langle A^m{\p\over \p x^m}+ A{\p\over \p z}+ \beta_m{\p\over \p \theta_m}+ \beta{\p\over \p \pi}\,, \a^{im}{\p\over \p x^m}+ \a^{i}{\p\over \p z}+ A^{i}_m{\p\over \p \theta_m} +A^{i}{\p\over \p \pi} \right \rangle $$ $$ = A^mA^i_m+AA^i+\beta_m\a^{im}+\beta\a^i=0\,, \tag{3g} $$
