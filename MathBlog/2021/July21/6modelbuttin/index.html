Linear algebra of Lagrange subspaces



Let ${\e_i}$ ($i=1.\dots,n$) be a set of linear idependent even vectors.

Let ${\f^i}$ ($i=1.\dots,n$) be a set of linear idependent odd vectors. \footnote{$^1)$} {may be here some condition of independace of vectors $\e_i$ and $f^j$?}.

Let V be span of even vectors ${\e_i}$ ($i=1.\dots,n$) and odd vectors ${\f^i}$ ($i=1.\dots,n$). $$ V=\{a^i\e_i+\a_i \f^j\}\,, $$ where coefficients $a_i$ are even elements (of an Grassman algebra) and coefficients $\a_i$ are odd elements (of an Grassman algebra).

We define in $V$ scalar product $$ G\colon G(\e_i,\f^j)=\delta_i^j\,, G(\e_i,\e_j)=0\,, G\colon G(\f^i,\f^j)=0\,, $$ This scalar product defines the odd symplectic structure.

Let $R$ be an arbitrary subspace of $V$. We say that dimension of $R$ is equal to $p|q$ ($p\leq n, q\leq n$)if there are $p$ linealy independent even vectors

Proposition $R$ is Lagrange surface iff its dimension is equal to $(n-k|k)$ ($k=0,\dots$)
