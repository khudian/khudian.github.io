Flux and Lagrange surface



Consider flux of the vector field $$ \R=A\p_x+B\p_y\,. \tag{1} $$ through the cirlce $$ x^2+y^2=R^2 $$ Assign to this circle a Lagrangian surface in $E^{2|2}$ given by gauge fermion $$ \Psi=(x^2+y^2-R^2)\eta $$ It is $$ L_\Psi\colon \cases { x^2+y^2-R^2=0\cr x^*=2x\eta\cr y^*=2y\eta\cr }\quad i.e.\quad \cases { x=R\cos t\cr y=R\sin t\cr x^*= 2R\eta\cos t\cr y^*= 2R\eta\sin t\cr } $$ Consider tangent vectors to this lagrangian surface $$ \E=-R\sin t{\p\over\p x} +R\cos t{\p\over\p y} -2R\sin t\eta{\p\over\p x_*} +2R\cos t\eta{\p\over\p y_*} \,,\quad \f=2x {\p\over\p x^*} +2y {\p\over\p y^*} $$ and consider the basis self-dual basis $\{\E,{\bf F},\e.\f\}$, i.e. a basis in $E^{2|2}$ such that $$ \cases { \omega(\E,\E)=0\,, \omega(\E,{\bf F})=0\,, \omega(\E,\e)=1\,, \omega(\E,\f)=0\,, \cr \omega({\bf F},{\bf F})=0\,, \omega({\bf F},\e)=1\,, \omega({\bf F},\f)=1\,,\cr \omega(\e,\e)=1\,, \omega(\e ,\f)=1\,,\cr \omega(\f ,\f)=0\,.\cr } \tag{2} $$ Many of these relations are fullfilled automatically. In any rate we may choose $$ {\bf F}=A{\p\over \p x} +B{\p\over \p y}\,,\quad \e=p{\p\over \p x^*} +q{\p\over \p x^*}\,, $$ such that $$ \cases { \E=-R\sin t{\p\over\p x} +R\cos t{\p\over\p y} -2R\sin t \,\eta{\p\over\p x_*} +2R\cos t\,\eta{\p\over\p y_*} \,,\cr {\bf F}={\cos t\over 2R}{\p\over \p x} +{\sin t\over 2R}{\p\over \p y}\,,\cr \e=-{\sin t\over R}{\p\over \p x^*} +{\cos t\over R}{\p\over \p y^*}\,,\cr \f=2R\cos t {\p\over\p x^*} +2 R\sin t {\p\over\p y^*}\cr } $$ and equations (1) are fullfilled Now consider half-density corresponding to vector field (1) $$ \ss=\ss_\R= (Ax^*+ By^*)\sqrt {D(x,y,x^*,y^*)}\,. $$ This semidensity induces density $\rho_L$ on the lagrangian surface: $$ \rho_L( x(t,\eta),y(t,\eta), x^*(t,\eta),y^*(t,\eta); \E,\f)= \ss_\R( x(t,\eta),y(t,\eta), x^*(t,\eta),y^*(t,\eta); \E, {\bf F}.\e,\f)\,. $$ Thus we have that $$ \rho_L( x=R\cos t\,, y=R\sin t,\quad x^*=2R\eta\cos t,\, y^*=2R\eta\sin t, ; \E,\f)= $$ $$ \ss_\R( x=R\cos t\,, y=R\sin t,\quad x^*=2R\eta\cos t,\, y^*=2R\eta\sin t, ; \E, {\bf F}.\e,\f)= $$ $$ (A(x,y)x^*+B(x,y)y^*) \sqrt {D(x,y,x^*,y^*)} \left (\E, {\bf F},\e,\f)\right) = \sqrt {D(x,y,x^*,y^*)} $$ $$ (A(x,y)x^*+B(x,y)y^*) \sqrt{ {\rm Ber} \pmatrix { &\E_x & \E_y &\E_{x^*} &\E_{y^*}\cr &{\bf F}_x &{\bf F}_x &{\bf F}_{x^*} &{\bf F}_{y^*}\cr &\e_x & \e_y &\e_{x^*} &\e_{y^*}\cr &\f_x & \f_y &\f_{x^*} &\f_{y^*}\cr } } \sqrt {D(x,y,x_*,y_*)} \left( {\p\over \p x}, {\p\over \p y}, {\p\over \p x_*}, {\p\over \p y_*} \right) $$ $$ (A(x,y)x^*+B(x,y)y^*) \sqrt{ {\rm Ber} \pmatrix { &-R\sin t & R\cos t &0 &0\cr &{\cos t\over 2R} &{\sin t\over 2R} &0 &0\cr &0 & 0 &{-{\sin t\over R}} &{\cos t\over R}\cr &0 & 0 &2R\cos t &2R\sin t\cr } }= $$ $$ (A(x,y)x^*+B(x,y)y^*) \sqrt{ { \det \pmatrix { &-R\sin t & R\cos t \cr &{\cos t\over 2R} &{\sin t\over 2R}\cr } \over \det \pmatrix { \pmatrix { &{-\sin t\over R} & {\cos t\over R} \cr & 2R\cos t &2R\sin t\cr } }}}= $$ $$ (A(x,y)x^*+B(x,y)y^* \sqrt{ { \det \pmatrix { &-R\sin t & R\cos t \cr &{\cos t\over 2R} &{\sin t\over 2R}\cr } \over \det \pmatrix { \pmatrix { &{-\sin t\over R} & {\cos t\over R} \cr & 2R\cos t &2R\sin t\cr } }}}= $$ $$ A(x,y)x^*+B(x,y)y^*\over 4 $$ Now calculate the integral of density $\rho_L$ over the circle $x^2+y^2=R^2$ and compare it with the flux of $\R$. Flux is equal to $$ \int_{x^2+y^2-R^2} \R= \int_{x^2+y^2=R^2}\iota_\R (dx\wedge dy)= \int_{x^2+y^2=R^2}(A(x,y)dy-B(x,y)dx)= $$ $$ \int_0^{2\pi} \left[A(R\cos t, R\sin t)R\cos t+ B(R\cos t, R\sin t)R\sin t \right ]dt $$

The integral of the density is equal to $$ \int_0^{2\pi}\rho_L(x(t,\eta), y(t,\eta), \E,\f)dt= $$ $$ \int_0^{2\pi}A(x(t,\tau),y(t.\tau)) x_*(t,\tau)+ B(x(t,\tau),y(t,\tau))y_*(t,\tau)D(t,\tau)= $$ $$ \int_0^{2\pi}A(R\cos t,R\sin t)2R\eta\cos t +B(R\cos t,R\sin t)2R\eta\sin t dt $$
