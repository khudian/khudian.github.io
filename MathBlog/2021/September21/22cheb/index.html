Tchebyshof polinomials



There are not many formulae which are deep and almost evident. Good example of such a formula is the famous Cauchi-Buniakovskij inequality: $$ \langle u,u\rangle \langle v,v\rangle \geq \langle u,v\rangle^2 $$ Two of them belong to Russian mathematician Tchebyshof.

Example Pick $n$ . Consider polynomial $T_n(x)$ of degree $n$.i.e $$ P_n(x)=x^n+\hbox {terms of power less than $n$} $$ such that for every polynomial $Q_n$ of degree $n$ $$ \max_{x\in A} Q_n(x) \geq \max_{x\in A} T_n(x) $$ One can see that if $A=[-1,1]$ then $T_2=x^2-{1\over 2}$, $T_3=x^3-{3\over 4}$, and for every $n$ $$ T_n(x)={1\over 2^n}\cos n\varphi\,, $$ where $x=\cos\varphi$.

$T_n(x)$ are famous Chebyshov polinomials (see the Blog... ).
