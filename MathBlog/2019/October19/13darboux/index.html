Coordinates a la Darboux?



\def\D {{\cal D}} Let $(u,v,\theta,\eta)$ be local coordinates (on $CP^{1|1}$) such that volume form has the appearance $$ \rho={\D (u,v,\theta,\eta)\over 1+u^2+v^2+k\theta\eta}\,. $$ where $k$ is a constant. This volume form gives localy not zero integral since $$ {1\over 1+u^2+v^2+k\theta\eta} = {1\over 1+u^2+v^2} \left(1-{c\theta\eta\over 1+u^2+v^2}\right)= {1\over 1+u^2+v^2}- {k\theta\eta\over (1+u^2+v^2)^2}\,. $$

Consider new coordinates $$ \cases { u=\sqrt s \cos\varphi\cr v=\sqrt s \sin\varphi\cr \theta\mapsto \theta\cr \eta\mapsto \eta\cr } $$ In these coordinates $$ \rho={1\over 2} {\D(s,\varphi,\theta,\eta)\over 1+s+k\theta\varphi} $$ Consider now new `unimodular' coordinates $$ \cases { s=s(t,\varphi,\theta,\eta)\cr \varphi\mapsto \varphi\cr \theta\mapsto \theta\cr \eta\mapsto \eta\cr } $$ such that $$ {\p s(t,\varphi,\theta,\eta)\over \p t}= 2(1+s+k\theta\varphi)\,, $$ i.e. $$ s=e^{2t}-1-k\theta\varphi\,. $$ In these coordinates volume form will be equal to $1$.
