Coordinates on $CP^n$



Let $z^\mu=z^9,z^i$, $\mu=0,1,\dots n$, $\i=1,\dots,n$ be homogeneous cordinates on $CP^N$, thus any point is $[z^0:z^1:\dots:z^n]$. Conisder non-degenerate Poisson structure with $$ \{\bar z^m, z^n\}=c, $$ on the sphere $S^{2n-1}$ defined by the energy level $H=E$ of Hamiltonian $$ H={\bar z^0 z^0+\sum_i \bar z^i z^i\over 2}\,. $$ (This is the Hamiltonian of harmonic oscillator.) amd coordinates $$ w^i={z^i\over z^0}\,,\quad \bar w^i={\bar z^i\over \bar z^0} $$ on the factor of the sphere by great circles, i.e. the projective space: $$ CP^N=S^{2n-1}\backslash S^1\,. $$ One can see that the coordinates $(w^i,\bar w^j)$ commute with the Hamiltonian: $$ \{w^i, H\}= \left\{{z^i\over z^0}, H\right\}= \{\bar w^i, H\}= \left\{{\bar z^i\over \bar z^0}, H\right\}=0\, $$ thus $(w^i,\bar w^j)$ are coordinates on $CP^n$, i.e. Poisson brackets of these coordinates is expressed in terms of these coordinates: $$ \{w^m,w^n\}= \left\{{z^m\over z^0},{z^n\over z^0}\right\}=0\,, \{\bar w^m,\bar w^n\}= \left\{{\bar z^m\over \bar z^0}, {\bar z^n\over \bar z^0}\right\}=0\,, $$ $$ \{\bar w^m,w^n\}= -\{w^n, \bar w^m\}= \left\{{\bar z^m\over \bar z^0}, {\bar z^n\over \bar z^0}\right\}= (1+\sum_r \bar w^rw^r) \left(\delta^{mn}+\bar w^mw^n\right)\,. $$






Try to find good coordinates.

Consider new coordinates $$ u^i=f(d)w^i\,, \bar u^i=f(d)\bar w^i\,, $$ where $d=\sum_r \bar w_r w_r$, $f(d)$ is a real function on real variable $d$. We see that $$ w^i=g(s)u^i\,, \bar w^i=g(s)\bar u^i\,, $$ where $s=\sum_r \bar u_r u_r$, $g(s)$ is a real function on real variable $d$ such that $$ s=f^2(d)d\,,\quad g(s)={1\over f(d)}\,. $$ One can see that coordinates $\bar w^m,w^n$ are eigenfunctions: $$ \{d,w^i\}=(1+d)^2 w^i \{d,\bar w^i\}=(1+d)^2 \bar w^i\,. $$ Using this we can easy to calculate that for new coordinates $\bar u,u$ we have that $$ \{u^m,u^n\}=0\,, \{\bar u^m,\bar u^n\}=0\,, $$ and $$ \{\bar u^m,u^n\}= \{\bar f(d)\bar w^m,f(d)w^n\}= f^2(d)(1+d)\left(\delta^{mn}+\bar w^m w^n\right)\,. $$ I think that this does not give a chance for coordinates $\bar u^i,u^j$ to be Darboux coordinates.
