Consider two odd Hamiltonians: $$ Q_M(x,p)=Q^{ab}(x)p_bp_a\,,\quad \hbox{(on $T^*M$ and } Q_N(y,q)=Q(y)+Q^i(y)q_i+Q^{ij}(y)q_jq_i\,,\quad \hbox{(on $T^*N$ )} \tag{1} $$ such that

a) they induce homological vector fields, i.e. $$ (Q_M,Q_M)_{T^*M}=0\,,\quad (Q_N,Q_N)_{T^*N}=0\, $$ where $(\,\,,\,\,)_{T^*M}$ (and respectively $(\,\,,\,\,)_{T^*N}$) is the canonical even Poisson bracket on $T^*M$ ($T^*N$).

b) they are connected by the function $$ S(x,q)=S_0(x)+\varphi^i(x)q_i\,, \tag{2} $$ i.e. $$ Q_M\left(x^a,{\p S(x,q)\over \p x^a}\right)\equiv Q_N\left({\p S(x,q)\over q_i},q_j\right) \tag{3} $$ Then according the general theory, the thick morphism generatedd by the function ((2) induces $L_\infty$ morphism of odd homotopy brackets (1), the map $$ C^\infty(M) {\buildrel \Phi_S^*\over \leftrightarrow} C^\infty(N) $$ connects the homological vector fields induced by Hamiltonians $Q_M$ and $Q_N$: for an arbitrary function $g(y)\in C^\infty(N)$ $$ \Phi_S \left( g(y)+\vare H_N(y,q)\big \vert_{q_i={\p g(y)\over \p y^i}} \right)=f(x)+ \vare H_M(x,p)\left( \vert_{p_a={\p f(x)\over \p x^a}} \right)\,, $$

in the case if $$ \Phi^*_S \left( g(y)\right)=f(x)\,. $$
