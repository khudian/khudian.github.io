Non-trivial parameterisation of cone



Let $C$ be a conic surface with apex at the point $O=(0,0,1)$ such that the circle $$ C\colon\quad \cases { x=\cos\varphi,\quad y=\sin\varphi \cr z=0}\,. $$

How looks the equation of the cone which passes through the vertex $O$ and the circle $C$?



The point $\r=(x,y,z)$ belongs to the surface of this cone if and only if $$ \det \pmatrix { x &y &z\cr 0 &0 &1\cr \cos\varphi &\sin\varphi &0\cr } = y\cos\varphi-x\sin\varphi=0\,. $$ The equation of this cone is $$ x^2+y^2=(z-1)^2\,. $$

Consider the skew cone with the vertex at the point $O=(a,0,1)$, passing through the circle $ C\colon \cases { x=\cos\varphi,\quad y=\sin\varphi \cr z=0} $. Equation of this cone is $$ S_a\colon\quad \det \pmatrix { x &y &z\cr a &0 &1\cr \cos\varphi &\sin\varphi &0\cr } = y\cos\varphi-x\sin\varphi+za\sin\varphi=0\,. \tag{1} $$ Intersect the cone $S_a$ with the plane $$ \pi\colon\quad{x\over L} + {z\over H}=0\,. $$ We come to the equation $$ \cases { y=x\tan\varphi-az\cr xH+zL=LH\cr } $$ The plane $\pi$ makes the angle $\theta$ such that $\cos\theta={H\over \sqrt {H^2+L^2}}$.
