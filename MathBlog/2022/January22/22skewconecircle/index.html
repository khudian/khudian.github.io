Second circle in the skew cone



The skew cone $$ \left[\{{\bf O}\}=(a,b,h); {\rm base}=\{R\cos\varphi,R\sin\varphi,h\}\right] $$ is passing via the circle $C\colon \{z=0,x^2+y^2=R^2\}$ and it has the apex at the point ${\bf O}=(a,b,h)$.

Its equation is $$ \left [ (x-O_x){\bf i}+(y-O_y){\bf j} +(z-O_z){\bf k} \right]\times \left [ (R\cos\varphi-O_x){\bf i}+(R\sin\varphi-O_y){\bf j}+ (0-O_z){\bf k} \right]= $$ $$ =\left [ (x-a){\bf i}+(y-b){\bf j} +(z-h){\bf k} \right]\times \left [ (R\cos\varphi-a){\bf i}+(R\sin\varphi-b){\bf j}+ (0-h){\bf k} \right]=0\,. $$ We come to the relation $$ \cases { -h(y-b)-(z-h)(R\sin\varphi-b)\cr -h(x-a)-(z-h)(R\cos\varphi-a)\cr } \Rightarrow \cases { R\varphi=b+{h(y-b)\over (h-z) }\cr R\cos\varphi=a+{h(x-a)\over (h-z) }\cr } \Rightarrow $$ (see details in the previous blog.)

Thus we see that points of this conic surface $M$ obey equations $$ \sin^2\varphi+\cos^2\varphi=1= \left({a\over R}+{(x-a)h\over (h-z)R}\right)^2+ \left({b\over R}+{(y-b)h\over (h-z)R}\right)^2=1 $$

Now consider another skew cone $$ \left[\{{\bf O}\}=(a,0,0); {\rm base}=\{\cos\varphi,\sin\varphi,1\}\right] $$ it is passing via the circle $C\colon \{z=1,x^2+y^2=1\}$ and it has the apex at the point ${\bf O}=(a,0,0)$.

Its equation is $$ \left [ (x-O_x){\bf i}+(y-O_y){\bf j} +(z-O_z){\bf k} \right]\times \left [ (R\cos\varphi-O_x){\bf i}+(R\sin\varphi-O_y){\bf j}+ (1-O_z){\bf k} \right]= $$ $$ =\left [ (x-a){\bf i}+y{\bf j} +z{\bf k} \right]\times \left [ (\cos\varphi-a){\bf i}+\sin\varphi {\bf j}+ 1{\bf k} \right]=0= $$ $$ \det \pmatrix { {\bf i} &{\bf j} &{\bf k}\cr {x-a} &{y} &{z}\cr {\cos\varphi-a} &{\sin\varphi} &{1}\cr }= {\bf i}(y-z\sin\varphi)- {\bf j}(x-a-z(\cos\varphi-a) {\bf k}((x-a)\sin\varphi-y(\cos\varphi-a))=0. $$

Thus $\sin\varphi={y\over z}$ $\cos\varphi=a+{x-a\over z}$ and $$ \left (a+{x-a\over z}\right)^2+ \left({y\over z}\right)^2=1 $$ is the equation of this skewcone.
