Parameterisation of the skew cone



Let $M$ be a conic surface with apex at the point $O=(a,0,1)$ such that it passes via the circle $$ C\colon\quad \cases { x=\cos\varphi,\quad y=\sin\varphi \cr z=0}\,. $$ How looks its equation?



The vector $\r$ belongs to this conic surface if and only if there exists a point $\r_\varphi$ at the circle $C$ such that vectors $\r-{O}$ and $\r_\varphi-{O}$ are coplanar: $$ \r-{O}\times \r_\varphi-{O}=0\,, $$ i.e. $$ \det \pmatrix { {\bf i} & {\bf j} & {\bf k} \cr x-a & y & z-1\cr \cos\varphi-a & \sin\varphi & -1\cr } =0= $$ $$ {\bf i} \pmatrix { y & z-1\cr \sin\varphi & -1\cr } -{\bf j} \pmatrix { x-a & z-1\cr \cos\varphi-a & -1\cr } + {\bf k} \pmatrix { x-a & y \cr \cos\varphi-a & \sin\varphi \cr }=0\,. $$

Thus we see that equation of the conic surface is $$ (x,y,z)\colon \cases { -y-(z-1)sin\varphi=0\cr -(x-a)-(\cos\varphi-a)(z-1)=0\cr (x-a)\sin\varphi-y(\cos\varphi-a)\cr }\,. $$ Those equation of skew cone with apex at the point $ O=(a,0,1)$ passing via the circle $C\colon \{x=\cos\varphi, y=\sin\varphi z=0 \}$ is $$ \sin^2\varphi + \cos^2\varphi= \left( {y\over 1-z} \right)^2 + \left(a+ {x-a\over 1-z} \right)^2=1\,. $$
