Symmetries of Clairaut equation

It is well-known that the Clairaut equation $$ y-xy'=f(y') \tag{1} $$ has the one-parametric family of solutions: $$ y=kx+f(k) \tag{2a} $$ and the special soluction, their envelope: $\varphi(x)$, $$ \varphi(x)=k(x)x+f(k(x))\colon\quad {\p\varphi\over k}=x+f'(k)=0\,. \tag {2b} $$ This is standard.

On the other hand the Clairaut equation (1) can be approached in the following way: Consider in $3$-dimensional space $E$ $=(p,x,y)$ ($p=y'$) the following transformation $$ (x,p,y)\to (\tilde x,\tilde p,\td y)\quad \cases {\td x=p\cr\td p=-x\cr \td y=y-px} $$ This is so called Legendre transformation of contact space $E$. Under this transformation the characteristic $1$-form $pdx-dy$ is
not changed,
the transformation (2a) preserves the distribution of planes defined by $1$-form $pdx-dy$, thus it can be applied to an arbitrary differential equation $F(x,p,y)=0$ ($p=y'$).
and the Clairaut equation transforms to the algebraic equation $$ \td y=f(\td x) $$, every solution (2a) is transformed to the generalised solution, the line $\cases {\td x=k\cr \td p=\hbox {arbitrary number}\cr \td y=f(k)\cr}$, and the special solution (2b), $y=\varphi(x)$ is transformed to the solution, the curve $\cases {\td x= t \cr \td p= f'(t)\cr \td y=f(t)\cr}$,

Our aim to find the one-parametric famiily of contact transformations which include the Legendre transformation and see how look the image of solutions (2a), (2b) under this transformation. Consider the Hamiltonian $H={p^2\over 2}+{x^2\over 2}$ of harmonic oscillator. This Hamiltonian defines the contact vector field $$ \X_H={\p H\over \p p}{\p\over \p x}- {\p H\over \p x}{\p\over \p p}+ \left(p{\p H\over \p p}-H\right){\p\over \p y}\,, $$ and it induces the contact transformation $$ \pmatrix {x\cr p\cr y\cr}\to \pmatrix {\td x_t\cr \td p_t\cr \td y_t\cr}\,, $$ such that $$ \cases { {d {\td x}\over dt}={\p H\over \p {\td p}}=\td p\cr {d {\td p}\over dt}=-{\p H\over \p {\td x}}=-\td x\cr {d {\td y}\over dt}= \left( {{\td p}^2\over 2} -{{\td x}^2\over 2} \right) \cr } $$ Solving this equation and taking in account the boudnary conditions $$ \pmatrix {\td x_t\cr \td p_t\cr \td y_t\cr}\big\vert_{t=0}= \pmatrix { x\cr p\cr y\cr} $$ we come to $$ \cases { x_t=x\cos t+p\sin t\cr p_t=-x\sin t+p\cos t\cr y_t=y+ {1\over 4}p^2\sin 2t -{1\over 4}x^2\sin 2t +{1\over 2}px\left(\cos 2t-1\right)\cr } $$ We see that for $t=0$ this is identity contact transformation, and for $t={\pi\over 2}$ this the Legendre trasnformation $$ \cases { x_{\pi\over 2}=p\cr p_{\pi\over 2}=-x\cr y_{\pi\over 2}=y-px\cr } $$
