\centerline {\bf Upper half plane is not euquivalent to plane}
Consider upeer half-plane: $\bf H\colon \{(x,y), y>0\}$ and all the plane $\bf C$. They are not holomorphically equivalent, i.e. it does not exist analytical bijection $\bf H\leftrightarrow \C$. Indeed $\bf H$ is holomorphically equivalent to disc $D_2\colon \{(x,y), x^2+y^2<1\}$ (Klein map). This implies that disc is eequivalent to $\bf C$. Let $F\colon {\bf C}\leftrightarrow \bf H$. Then $F$ is analytical bounded function on $\bf C$, and due to Lioville Theorem $F$ maps $\bf C$ to the point.
