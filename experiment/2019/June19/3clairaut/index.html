{\bf Clairaut equation}
Solving "naively" Clairaut equation $$ y=xy'+f(y') $$ differentiating we come to equation $$ y^{''}(x+f'(y'))=0 $$
When solving equation $x+f'(y')=0$ we come to solutions defined up to the constant. $$ y'=\varphi(-x)\Rightarrow y=C-\dots, \quad \varphi\circ f'={\bf id} $$ On the other hand we do not need to solve the equation $x+f'(y')=0$. The equation defines the parameteric solution $$ \cases {x=-f'(p)\cr y=xy'+f(y')=f(p)-pf'(p) }\,,\hbox {where $p=y'$} $$:
