An attempt to multiply the vectors

Let $V$ be Euclidean vector space with scalar product $(x,y)$. Consider Clifford algebra over $V$ with quadratic form $Q(\x)=-(\x,\x)$.
Let us try to mimic the multiplication, then we will criticise the construction:
To every vector $\x\in V$ assign rotation $L_\x$, which is rotation around axis directed along $\x$ on the angle $\pi$. To formal product $$ \x_1\otimes \x_2\dots \x_n $$ we assing the element of orthogonal group $$ SO(V)\ni P= L_{\x_1} L_{\x_2}\dots L_{\x_{n-1}} L_{\x_{n}} $$ It seems that we reconstruct in this way the Clifford algebra?
