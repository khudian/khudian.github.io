Spinor group

Let $\Cl (V,Q)$ be Clifford algebra of vector space $V$ equipped with bilinear form $Q$. According the lemma that $$ \Cl \left( \left(Q_1,V_1\right) \right) \oplus \left(Q_2,V_2\right) = \Cl (Q_1,V_1){\hat \otimes} \Cl (Q_2)\,, $$ we come to theorem:
Clifford algebra $\Cl(Q,V)$ is isomorphic to wedge product of $p$ algberas of doube numbers ($a+b\vare$, $\vare^2=1$), $r-p$ algberas of complex numbers ($a+b\vare$, $\vare^2=-1$), and $n-r$ algberas of dual numbers ($a+b\vare$, $\vare^2=0$), where $r$ is the rank of the form $Q$, $(p,r-p)$ is the signature of the form, $n$ is the dimension of vector space $V$, i.e. $p,q,r$ are integers such that quadratic form $Q$ in $V$ can be reduced to the form $$ Q=x_1^2+\dots+x_p^2-x_{p+1}^2-\dots x_r^2 $$ in some linear coordinates

Example $\E^2$ with $Q(\x)=-x_1^2-x_2^2$, then $$ \Cl(\E^2,Q=(-,-))=\C\hat\otimes\C={\bf H}\,\, \hbox {(quaternions)} $$ One can view the algebra $\Cl(V,Q)$ as unital algebra such that vector space $V$ is subpsace in this algebra, and all elements are generated by vectors of $V$ such that $\x^2=Q(\x)\cdot 1$.
