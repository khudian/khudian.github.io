Weyl symbol

Let $$ H(p,q)= \int\varphi (\a,\beta)e^{i\a p+i\beta q}d\a d\beta $$ and operator $$ \hat {H}(p,q)= \int\varphi (\a,\beta) e^{i\a \hat p+i\beta \hat q}d\a d\beta $$ then we say that $H$ is Weyl symbol of $\hat H$. One can see that this is equivalent to the fact that roughly speaking to obtain Weyl we symmetrize the operator. E.g. $$ \hbox{Weyl symbol of }(p^2q)= \hbox{Weyl symbol of } {1\over 3}\left(ppq+pqp+qpp \right)= {1\over 3}\left(\hat p \hat p\hat q+ \hat p\hat q\hat p+\hat q\hat p\hat p\right)\,. $$ Thed informed me (and this sounds very reasonable) that the space of quadratic Hamiltonians is the subalgebra: $$ W() $$
