Composition of morphisms

For Lagrangian $L=L(x,\dot x)$ we know that $$ {\p \SS(x,y,t)\over \p y}=q\, $$ and this implies that $$ {\p \SS(x,y,t)\p x}=-p\, $$ One can see it reversing the time. More carefully this means the following: $$ \SS(t,x,y)=\int_0^td\tau L(x(\tau),\dot x(\tau))d\tau\,, $$ where $x(\tau)$ solution of equations of motion, $x(\tau=0)=x$, $x(\tau=t)=y$. (We suppose that $L$ does not depend on time) We have that $$ \SS(t+\delta t,x+\delta x,y+\delta y)= \int_0^{t+\delta t}d\tau L\left( x\left(\tau\right)+\vare h\left(\tau\right), \dot x\left(\tau\right)+\vare \dot h\left(\tau\right)) \right)d\tau\,, $$ where $$ h(0)=\delta x\,, $$ and $$ x(t+\delta t)+\vare h(t+\delta t)=y(t)+\delta y\,,\rm {i.e.\,}\,\, \dot x(t)+\vare h(t)=\delta y $$ $$ {\p S(q,t)\over \p t}`= H\left({\p S\over \p q},q\right)= H\left({x,\p S\over \p x}\right)\,. $$
