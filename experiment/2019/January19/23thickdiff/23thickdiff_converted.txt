<br>
Recall that generating function $S(x,q)=S(x^i,q_a)$
defines a map
         $$
\Phi_S^* \colon\quad C(N)\to C(M)
         $$
such that
        $$
\forall g(y)\in CM(N)
C(M)\ni f(x)=\Phi^*_S(g)=
   g(y)+S(x,q)-y^aq_a\,,
        $$
where functions $y^a$,
$q_a$ are such that
           $$
y^a={\p S(x,q)\over \p q_a}\big\vert_{q_a=
{\p g\over \p y^a}\,.}\qquad
\Rightarrow
 {\p f\over x^i}=p_i={\p S(x,q)\over \p x^i}
           $$
<br>
NOw consider the case when $M=N$.
<br>
Let $H=H(x,p)$  be Hamiltonian on $T^*M$.
<br>
s Hamiltonian generates infinitesimal
thick morphism
       $$
     \Phi^*_{\vare S}\colon  g(x)\mapsto
      g(x)+\vare H(x,p)\big\vert_{p={\p g\over \p y}}\,,
       $$
This infinitesimal map is nothing but the Hamilton-Jacobi vector field on
$C(M)$
     $$
 \X_S=\int dx H\left(x,{\p g\over \p x}\right)
{\delta\over \delta x}\,.
     $$
<br>
This we see that for finite interval of the time
       $$
\Phi^{(t)*}_S(g)=A(g|x,t)\colon\quad
        \cases
          {
 {\p A\over \p t}=H\left(x,{\p A\over \p x}\right)\cr
   A\big\vert_{t=0}=g\cr 
          }    
       $$
This is up to a sign Hamiton Jacobi equation.
<br>
Thus we see that
the action of 
  inverse image of thick diffeomorphism  generated by
the Hamiltonian $H$ on the arbitrary function
$g(y)$ is the action $A(g|x,t)$, which is equal to $g(x)$
at the moment $t=0$.
<br>
It is difficult to avoid a temptation to claim that the quantum
version of this equation has to be Shrodinger equation.
