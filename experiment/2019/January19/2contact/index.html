Definition Let $\w$ be $1$-form on $M$ which does not vanish. . We say that it is contact form if $$ \hbox{$2$-form $d\w$ is non-degenerate on the plane $\w=0$ in $TM$} $$
Since $d\w$ is not degenerate on $\w=0$ and $\w\not\equiv 0$ them ${\rm dim}\, M=2k+1$.
Theorem Contact form is defined up to a function (Valya had a talk on it!) If $\w$ is contact form and $f\not=0$ then $f\a$ is contact also.

Let $\cal K$ be a distributions of $2n$-dimensional planes in $TM$ such that $\w$ vanishes on these planes.
We say that this distribution is a contact structure
One can say that distribution of hyperplanes defines constant structure if an $1$-form which vanishes this dstribution is non-degenerated on it

Theorem Let $N$ be a submanifold of $M$ which is an integral submanifold (not necessarily maximal) of contact distribution $\cal K$, i.e. for every point on $N$ the tangent vectors belong to this distribution. Then ${\rm dim}\, N\leq n$, where ${\rm dim}\,M \leq 2n+1$
Proof Let $\w$ be an arbitrary non-zero form which vanishes at $\cal K$. Since a form $\w$ vanishes on vectors tangent to the manifold $N$, the form $d\w$ vanishes on these vectors also: $$ \iota\colon N\subset M, \qquad d\left(\iota^* \w\big\vert_N\right)= \iota^* dw\big\vert_N=0\,. $$ Hence two arbitrary vectors are orthogonal to each other with respect to this form. If dimension of tangent plane is bigger than $n$ then there exist at least two vectors which are not orthogonal, since $d\w$ is not degenerate. Now we apply this mathematics to the differential equations.

${\S}2\,\,$ Geometry of first order equation


Let $J^1M$ be a space of first jets of functions on manifold $M$. Cooridnates on $J^1M$ are $(p_i,q^j,u)$, where $q^j$ are coordinates on $M$. Jet of every function $u=u(x)$ has coordinates $\left(p_i={\p u\over \p x q^i},q^i,u\right)$.
Consider $\CC$ , the Cartan distribution of $2n$-dimensional planes in $J^1M$ defined by the form $\w=p_idq^i-du$ $$ \CC_\pb\subset T_\pb J^1M=\{ T_\pb (J^1M)\ni\X\colon\,\,\w(\X)=0 \}\,, $$ Vector field $$ M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\, \hbox{belongs to Cartan distribution $\CC$ if $A=p_iM^i$}\,. $$ $\CC$ is non-integrable distribution. It is a contact structure and the form $\w_C=p_idq^i=du$ is a contact form since $$ d\w\big\vert_{\w=0}=dp_i\wedge dq^i $$ is non-degenerte form. Consider differential equation, $$ \EE\colon F(p,q,u)=0\,. $$ Differential equation is sumbmanifold of codimension $1$ in the space $J^1(M)$.
The Cartan distribution $\CC$ of hyperplanes on $J^1M$ defines distribution $\CC(\EE)$ in $T\EE$: $$ \CC(E)=\CC\cap T\EE\,. $$ $$ \X= M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\in \CC(\EE)\,\, {\rm if}\,\, A=p_iM^i \& \left(M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\right)F(p,q,u)\big\vert_{F=0}=0\,. $$
This distribution is not integrable.
The solution of differential equation (1) is the maximal integral of the distribution.
What is the dimension of $N$?
Let $N$ be an arbitrary solution. Calculate its dimension. Any tangent plane to $N$ belongs to Cartan distribution and is tangent to $\EE$. Consider an arbitrary point $\pb \in N$, and consider the plane $\a=\a_P= T_\pb N$. Calculate $\dim T_\pb N$.
Fact $\dim T_\pb N\leq n$. We proved it above, but we repeat the considerations again. Consider the form $\w_C$ on and its differential the form $d\w_C$ on $TN$. The form $\w_C$ vanishes on $TN$, hence the form $d\w_C$ vanishes on $TN$ also. On the other hand if $\dim N=p>n$, then this is not true, since form $d\w_C$ has rank $2$. In coordinates: in the vicinity of the point $\pb$ one can choose local coordinates $\xi^1,\dots,\xi^{2n+1}$ such that in these coordinates surface $N$ is given by equations $\xi^{p+1}=\dots=\xi^{2n+1}=0$ and $d\w_C=d\xi^1\wedge d\xi^2+\dots + d\xi^{2n-1}\wedge d\xi^{2n}$ (generalised Darboux Theorem). This contradicts to the condition $d\w_C\big\vert_N\equiv 0$ on $N$. (In the case if dimension $N$ is maximal possible it is Lagrangian)
Now we consider some properties of hypersurfces, (recall that hypersurface is differential equation)
DEFINITION Let $\EE$ be an arbitrary hypersurface in $M$: $\dim \EE=2n$). (Hypersurface $\EE$ may define differential equation $F(q,u,p)=0$ ($\EE\colon F=0$)) We say that the hypersurface $\EE$ is non-characteristic hypersurface if at every point $\pb$ the contact hyperplane (hyperplane of distribution $\CC$) and the tangent hyperplane are transversal: $$ \CC_\pb\oplus T_pt M'=T_\pb M\Leftrightarrow \dim (\CC_\pb\cap M'\pb)=2n-1\,. $$

DEFINITION Let $\EE$ be an arbitrary non-characteristic hypersurface in $J^1(M)$.
For an arbitrary point $\pb\in E$ consider the $2n-1$ dimensional subspace $\Pi_\pb$ of tangent vectors which belong to contact space: $$ \Pi_\pb=\CC_\pb\cap T_\pb E\,,\qquad \,,\dim \Pi_\pb=2n-1 $$

Thus we define the distribution of $2n-1$-dimensional planes on non-characteristic differential equation $F=0$ (this equation defines the surface $\EE$: $\EE\colon\,\, F=0$).
Now: VERY IMPORTANT STEP: On every characteristic plane $\Pi_\pb=\CC_\pb\cap T_\pb E$, $d\w_C= \sum_{i=1}^n dp_i\wedge dq^i$, and since the dimension of $\Pi_\pb$ is equal to $2n-1$ then one can define the direction, such that $dw$ vanishes along this direction, and it is unique!
Problem Let $\s in V^*$ be covector in $V^*$ and let $M_\s$, me hyperplane in $V$ which is orthogonal to the covector $\ac$: $$ M_\ac=\{\x\in V\colon\quad \s(\x)=0\} $$
Proposition Subspaces $M_{\s_1}$ and $M_{s_2}$ are transversal if and only if vectors $\s_1,\s_2$ qre linearly independent, (not proportional to each other)

are not proportional
this condition does not depend on choice of function $F$ defining the surface $\EE$
Then
