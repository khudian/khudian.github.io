Chebysghev inequality claims the following: Let $F$ be non-negative function, and $$ M_a=\{x\colon\quad F(x)\geq a\}\,, $$ where $a>0$. Then one can see that $$ \mu(M_a)=\int_{M_a} dx\leq \int_{M_a} {F(x)\over a}dx= {1\over a}\int_{M_a} F(x)dx\leq {1\over a}\int^\infty _{-\infty} F(x)dx\leq $$
(we asssume that all integrals exist.)
This inequality seems to be one almost evidnet and highly effective.
Corollary.
Let random variable $\xi$ has average $\mu$, and dispersion $\sigma$: $$ {A}=\mu\,,\quad \sqrt{<\Delta A^2>}= \sqrt{-^2}=\sigma\,. $$ Then $$ P\left(|\xi-\mu|>a\right)\leq {\sigma^2\over a^2}\,. $$ {\tt Proof}
$$ P\left(|\xi-\mu|>a\right)\leq \int {\left(x-\mu\right)^2\over a^2}\rho(x)dx={\sigma^2\over a^2} \,. $$
