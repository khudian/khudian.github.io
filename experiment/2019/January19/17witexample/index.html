One example of integral



We consider $$ \int_{-\infty}^\infty \exp\left[-{x^4\over 4}+{x\over \nu}\right]dx\,. $$ (this integral is from witten paper....)
We have for the exponent: $$ F(x)=-{x^4\over 4}+{x\over \nu}\,, \,\, F'(x)=-x^3+{1\over \nu}\,,\,\, F^{''}(x)=-3x^2\,,, F^{'''}(x)=-6x\,,, F^{''''}(x)=-6\,,, $$ the stationary point $x_0=\nu^{-1/3}$ and $$ F(x)={3\over 4}\nu^{-4/3}- {3\over 2}\nu^{-2/3}\left(x-\nu^{-1/3}\right)^2- \nu^{-1/3}\left(x-\nu^{-1/3}\right)^3- {1\over 4}\left(x-\nu^{-1/3}\right)^4\,. $$ Thus we see that $$ \int_{-\infty}^\infty \exp\left[-{x^4\over 4}+{x\over \nu}\right]dx = \int_{-\infty}^\infty \exp\left[F(x)\right]dx= $$ $$ \exp\left[{3\over 4}\nu^{-4/3}\right] \int_{-\infty}^\infty\exp\left[- {3\over 2}\nu^{-2/3}\left(x-\nu^{-1/3}\right)^2\right]f_\nu(x)dx\,, $$ where $$ f_\nu(x)=\exp\left[- \nu^{-1/3}\left(x-\nu^{-1/3}\right)^3- {1\over 4}\left(x-\nu^{-1/3}\right)^4 \right]\,. $$ Doing translation we will come to $$ \int_{-\infty}^\infty \exp\left[-{x^4\over 4}+{x\over \nu}\right]dx = \exp\left[{3\over 4}\nu^{-4/3}\right] \int_{-\infty}^\infty\exp\left[- {3\over 2}\nu^{-2/3}x^2\right] \exp\left[- \nu^{-1/3}x^3- {1\over 4}x^4 \right]dx $$
On the other hand we know that $$ \int \exp\left[-{x^2\over h}\right]f(x)dx= C_h\exp\left[h{d^2\over dx^2}\right] f(x)\big\vert_{x=0} $$ prove this formula and calculate $C_h$. Consider $$ I_n(h)=\int_{-\infty}^\infty \exp \left[-{x^2\over h}\right]x^{2n}dx\,. $$ (This integral vanishes if $2n\to 2n+1$.) We have $$ I_n(h)= \int_{-\infty}^\infty \exp \left[-{x^2\over h}\right]x^{2n}dx= (x^2=hz)= 2\int_0^\infty \exp \left[-z\right]h^nz^n d \left( \sqrt h d\sqrt z \right)= $$ $$ h^{n+{1\over 2}}\int_0^\infty \exp \left[-z\right]z^{n-{1\over 2}} dz = h^{n+{1\over 2}}\Gamma\left(n+{1\over 2}\right) = \sqrt h { \left({h\over 4}{d^2\over dx^2}\right)^n \over n! }x^{2n}= \sqrt h\exp\left[{h\over 4}{d^2\over dx^2}\right]x^{2n}\big\vert_{x=0} $$
