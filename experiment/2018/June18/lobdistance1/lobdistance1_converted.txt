\centerline  {Disntance in Lobachevsky geometry}

Let  $z_1=x_1+iy_1$,  $z_2x_2+iy_2$  be two points in
Lobachevsky plane (we consider upper-half plane model).

\m

First consider the case if $z=x+iy$, $w=i$.
  Transformation
      $$
  z\mapsto {z+a\over 1-az}\,,,  a\in \R
      $$ 
preserves the point $w=i$.  Find $a$ such that 
        $
    z'={z+a\over 1-az} 
        $  is also on $OY$ axis, i.e.
         $$
a\colon \,, {\rm Re\,}{z+a\over 1-az}=0\,,
         $$
i.e.
            $$
   {z+a\over 1-az}+
   {\bar z+a\over 1-a z}=
   {(z+a)(1-a\bar z)+
   (\bar z+a)(1-a z)
       \over
   (1-az(1-a\bar z)
       }=0\,.
      $$
We come to the quadratic equation  on $a$
      $$
   (z+a)(1-a\bar z)+
   (\bar z+a)(1-a z)=(z+\bar z)+2a(1-z\bar z)-a^2(z+\bar z)=0
         $$
 which defines Mobius transformation which put two points
on the axis  $0Y$, provided the first point $w=i$.  
Solving this equation we come to
      $$
a={1-z\bar z\pm\sqrt {(1-z\bar z)^2+(z+\bar z)^2}\over z+\bar z}\,
={1-z\bar z\pm\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}\over z+\bar z}\,
      $$
i.e. Mobius   transformation  
       $$
  z'=iy'={z+a\over 1-az}
  ={z+{1-z\bar z+\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}\over z+\bar z}
   \over 1-{1-z\bar z+\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}
       \over z+\bar z}z}=
        $$
           $$
   {1+z^2+\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}
         \over
      \bar z(1+z^2)-z\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}}
=   {1+z^2+\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}
         \over
      \bar z-z\left(\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}-|z|^2\right)}
       $$
transforms an arbitrary point $z=x+iy$  with $y\not =0$
to the point on the axis $OY$.
(we choose the root with $'+'$, the second root is for the points
on $OX$ but we are not interested in these points.)

In other words numerator of the fraction is
proportional to denominator multiplied on imaginary 
  number:
       $$
  \left[1+x^2-y^2+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}:2xy\right]=  
             $$
             $$ 
 \left[
 y\left(1+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}-|z|^2\right)
   :
 x\left(1-\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}+|z|^2\right)\right]\,,  
           $$
i.e.
         $$
{1+x^2-y^2+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}\over 2xy}=
{ y\left(1+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}-|z|^2\right)
   \over
 x\left(1-\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}+|z|^2\right)}
          $$

Proverka: $z=1+i$, i.e. $x=y=1$, then
 $$
{1+x^2-y^2+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}\over 2xy}=
    {1+\sqrt 5\over 2}=
{ y\left(1+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}-|z|^2\right)
   \over
 x\left(1-\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}+|z|^2\right)}=
  {\sqrt 5-1\over 3-\sqrt 5}\,.
          $$

Proverka v obshem sluchae:
We have to check that
     $$
A=\left(1+x^2-y^2+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}\right)\cdot
\left(x\left(1-\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}+|z|^2\right)\right)
    =
      $$
      $$
=B= 2xy\cdot
\left( y\left(1+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}-|z|^2\right)\right)\,,
     $$
i.e.
        $$
A=(1+x^2-y^2+S)x(1+x^2+y^2-S)=2xy^2(1-x^2-y^2+S)=B\,.
        $$
Check it:
      $$
A=\left(1+x^2-y^2+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}\right)\cdot
\left(x\left(1-\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}+|z|^2\right)\right)
    =
$$
      $$
\left(1+x^2-y^2+S\right)
\left(x-xS+x^3+xy^2\right)=  
$$
   $$
( x+ x^3+xy^2-   xS)+
(x^3+x^5+x^3y^2-x^3S)+
(-xy^2-x^3y^2-xy^4+xy^2S)+
(xS+x^3S+xy^2S-xS^2)=
    $$
      $$
  x+2x^3+x^5-xy^4+2xy^2S-x\left(1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2\right)=
        $$
        $$
  x+2x^3+x^5-xy^4+2xy^2S-x\left(1+x^4+2x^2y^2+y^4+2x^2-2y^2\right)=
       $$
     $$
  -2xy^4+2xy^2S-2x^3y^2+2xy^2=2xy^2(-y^2-x^2+1+S)=
     $$
      $$
=2xy\cdot
\left( y\left(1+\sqrt{1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}-|z|^2\right)\right)
 =B
     $$

Thus we checked that the expression is right.

{\it Resum\'e}  

Let $ac=bd$, then
       $$
z'=iy'={a+bi\over c-di}={i(b-ia)\over c-di}=i{b\over c}=i{a\over d}
       $$
According to this identity return to calculations:
             $$
z'=iy'= {1+z^2+\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}
         \over
      \bar z-z\left(\sqrt {1+|z|^4+z^2+\bar z^2}-|z|^2\right)}=
             $$
         $$
          {         
          {\overbrace{(1+x^2-y^2+S)}^a}
         +{\overbrace {2xy}^b}i
       \over
     {\underbrace {x(1+x^2+y^2-S)}_c} 
         -i
     {\underbrace {y(1+S-x^2-y^2)}_d}
         }=
     {1+x^2-y^2+S\over y(1-x^2-y^2+S)}i=
     {2y\over 1+x^2+y^2-S}i\,,
         $$
where
      $$
S=\sqrt {1+x^4+2x^2y^2+y^4+2x^2-2y^2}\,.
     $$

Now play with the last formula:
            $$
y'=
     {1+x^2-y^2+S\over y(1-x^2-y^2+S)}=
     {1+x^2-y^2+\sqrt {(1+x^2-y^2)^2+4x^2y^2}
      \over y\left(
      1-x^2-y^2+\sqrt {(1-x^2-y^2)^2+4x^2}
        \right)}=
            $$
   $$
     {2xy
       \left(
       \left({1+x^2-y^2\over 2xy}\right)+
    \sqrt {\left({1+x^2-y^2\over 2xy}\right)^2+1}
        \right)
      \over 2xy\left(
      \left({1-x^2-y^2\over 2x}\right)+
    \sqrt {\left({1-x^2-y^2\over 2x}\right)^2+1}
        \right)}=
                e^{
    {\rm arcsh\,}{1+x^2-y^2\over 2xy}
              -
    {\rm arcsh\,}{1-x^2-y^2\over 2x}
          }
           $$
Quickly:
        $$
   ch\left(  a
              -
    b
\right)=\sqrt{1+a^2}\sqrt {1+b^2}-ab
         $$
        $$
   ch\left(  {\rm arcsh\,}{1+x^2-y^2\over 2xy}
              -
    {\rm arcsh\,}{1-x^2-y^2\over 2x}
\right)={x^2+y^2+1\over 2y}\,.
         $$

Hence  we come to
        $$
   d(z,w)=d(x_1+iy_1,x_2+iy_2)=
     d\left(x_1-x_2+iy_1, iy_2\right)=
     d\left({x_1-x_2+iy_1\over y_2}, i\right)=
        $$
       $$
  {\rm arcch}\left({x^2+y^2+1\over 2y}\right)
\big\vert_{x\mapsto {x_1-x_2\over y_2}, y\mapsto {y_1\over y_2}}=
{\rm arcch}\left({\left({x_1-x_2\over y_2}\right)^2
    +\left({y_1\over y_2}\right)^2+1\over 
2\left({y_1\over y_2}\right)}\right)=
       $$
     $$
{\rm arcch}\left({\left(x_1-x_2\right)^2
    +y_1^2+y_2^2\over 
2y_1y_2}\right)=
{\rm arcch}\left(1+{\left(x_1-x_2\right)^2
    +(y_1-y_2)^2\over 
2y_1y_2}\right)\,.
       $$
This is what we wnated.
