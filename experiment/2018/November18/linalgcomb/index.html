One combinatorial lemma

Let $M$ be $n\times n$ matrix such that all diagonal entries are $0$ and all other entries are $\pm 1$: $$ M_{ij}=\cases { 0 \,\, {\rm if}\,\, i=j\cr \pm 1 \,\, {\rm if}\,\, i\not j\cr } $$ Then for characteristic polynomial $$ \det (\lambda+M)=\sum_{k=0}^n a_k\lambda^{n-k}=\lambda^n +a_1\lambda^{n-1} +a_2\lambda^{n-2} \dots +a_{n-1}\lambda+a_n\, \tag{2} $$ the following relation holds: $$ a_k= C^k_n S_k=C^k_n(k+1) \,\, (mod 2)\,. $$ Corollary For characteristic polynomial (2) $$ \hbox{$C^k_n$ is odd and $k$ is even}\Rightarrow a_k\not=0\,. $$ Proof of the lemma is straightforward (it is the corollary that is surprisingly effective!) $$ $$
