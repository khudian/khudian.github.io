We have $$ i\hbar {\p \Psi(x,t)\over \p t}=\hat H\Psi\,, \qquad E\Phi(x,E)=\hat H\Phi(x,E)\,, \tag{1} $$ where $$ H={p^2\over 2m}+U(x)\,, $$ $$ \Psi (x,t)=\int \Phi(x, E)e^{-{iEt\over \hbar}}\,. $$ We find first quasiclassical solution in Energy coordinates, then do their Legendre transformation to time picture.
We consider $$ \Psi (x_0,t_0;x,t)= \exp \left[{i\over \hbar}S_\hbar(x_0,t_0;x,t)\right]\,, \qquad \Phi (x_0,x,E)=\exp \left[{i\over \hbar}\SS_\hbar(x_0x,E)\right]\,, $$ where $S_\h, \SS_\h$ are formal polynomials on $\h$ of inifnite degrees: $$ S_\hbar(x_0,t_0;x,t)= S(x_0,t_0;x,t)+ {\hbar\over i} \sigma(x_0,t_0,x,t)+\dots \qquad \SS_\hbar(x,E)= \SS(x,E)+ {\hbar\over i} s(x,E)+\dots $$ Here $S$ ($\SS$ ) is the classical action in time representation ($E$)-representation ($S$ and $\SS$ are reciprocal Legendre transforms): $$ S(x,t)=\SS(x,E)-Et\,, \quad {\rm with}\,\, E\colon t={\p \SS\over \p E}\,. $$ The term $\sigma$ ($s$) is responcible for the amplitude. The dependence on $x_0$ is due to initial conditions; $H$ does not depend on time, it is why $\SS$ deos not depend on $E_0$.
The Shrodinger equation becomes $$ i\h {\p \Psi\over \p t}= i\h {\p\over \p t} e^{ {i\over \hbar}\SS_\hbar (x,t) } = \hat H e^{ {i\over \hbar}\SS_\hbar (x,t) }=\,,\quad \quad E\Phi(x,E)= E e^{ {i\over \hbar}\SS_\hbar (x,E) }= \hat H \Psi (x,E)= $$ $$ \left[ {\hbar\over i}{\p\over \p x^i} \left( {\hbar\over i}{\p\over \p x_i} \right) + U(x) \right] e^{ {i\over \hbar}\SS_\hbar (x,t) }= \quad \left[ {\hbar\over i}{\p\over \p x^i} \left( {\hbar\over i}{\p\over \p x_i} \right) + U(x) \right] e^{ {i\over \hbar}\SS_\hbar (x,E) }= $$ $$ \left[ {\hbar\over i} {1\over 2m}\Delta S_\hbar+{1\over 2m} \left({\rm grad} S_\hbar \right)^2+U(x) \right] e^{{i\over \hbar}\SS_\hbar (x,t)}\,, \qquad \left[ {\hbar\over i} {1\over 2m}\Delta \SS_\hbar+{1\over 2m} \left({\rm grad} \SS_\hbar \right)^2+U(x) \right] e^{{i\over \hbar}\SS_\hbar (x,E)}\,, $$ i.e. $$ \matrix { {\hbar\over i}{1\over 2m}\Delta S_\hbar(x,t)& +{1\over 2m} \left({\rm grad} \SS_\hbar \left(x,t\right)\right)^2\,, \quad {\hbar\over i}{1\over 2m}\Delta \SS_\hbar(x,E)& +{1\over 2m} \left({\rm grad} \SS_\hbar \left(x,E\right)\right)^2\cr &+U=-{\p S\over \p t} \quad +U=E\SS\cr } $$ We have $$ \Delta \SS_\hbar=\Delta S(x,E)+{\hbar\over i} s(x,E)+\dots\,, $$ $$ \left({\rm grad} \SS_\hbar {x,E}\right)^2= \left({\rm grad} \SS \right)^2 +2\left({\hbar \over i}\right) {\rm grad} \SS\cdot {\rm grad} s +\dots $$ The zeroth approximmation is $$ {1\over 2m} \left({\rm grad} \SS \left(x,t\right)\right)^2+U(x)+S_t=0\,, \quad {1\over 2m} \left({\rm grad} \SS \left(x,E\right)\right)^2+U(x)=E\,, $$ this is nothing but Hamilton-Jacobi equation $$ H\left(x,{\p S\over \p x}\right)+{\p S\over \ p t}=0 \,,\qquad H\left(x,{\p S\over \p x}\right)=E $$ in time ($E$)-representation.
