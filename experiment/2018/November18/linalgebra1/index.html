{\bf Lemma 1}
Let $S_k$ be the number of permutations of $k$ numbers such that no number is preserved: $$ S_k= \hbox {number of permutations $[\sigma_1\sigma_2\sigma_3\dots\sigma_k$ such that i\not=\sigma_i} $$
Then $S_k$ is odd if $k$ is even, and $S_k$ is even if $k$ is odd.
In fat $S_k$ can be easily calculated based on the equation $\sum C^k_nS_k=0$ (see the etude ndrunk.tex) but here we need only its oddness.

{\bf Lemma 2} Let $M$ be $2k+1\times 2k+1$ matrix such that $$ M_{ij}=\cases {\pm 1 if i\not=j\cr 0 if i=j\cr} $$ Then for the polynomial $$ P_M(z)=\det (z+M) $$ all coefficients for odd degrees of $z$ are not equal to zero.
