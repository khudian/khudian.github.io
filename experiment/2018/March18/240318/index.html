Consider differential equation $$ \hat P (F)=G $$ where $\hat P=\hat P\left({d\over dx}\right)$ polynomial on derivative, $f,g$ polynomials on $x$.
Lemma: On the space of polynomials $$ \left({d\over dx}+\lambda\right)^{-1}G(x)= G\left(x+{d\over d\lambda}\right){1\over \lambda}\,. $$ It follows from this lemma that the solutions of differential equation $$ \hat P(F)=\prod \left({d\over dx}+\lambda_i\right)F=G $$ is the polynomial $$ F(x)=G\left(x+\sum_i {d\over d\lambda_i}\right) {1\over \lambda_1\lambda_2\dots \lambda_n}\,. $$ Proof of the lemma
We have that the commutator $ \left[{d\over dx}+\lambda,x+{d\over d\lambda}\right]=0$, hence $$ \left({d\over dx}+\lambda\right) G \left( \left({d\over d\lambda}+x\right) \right) {1\over l} = G \left( \left({d\over d\lambda}+x\right) \right) \left({d\over dx}+\lambda\right) {1\over \lambda}= G \left( \left({d\over d\lambda}+x\right) \right) {\bf 1}= G(x)\,. $$
