\centerline {\bf Taylor series expansion formula}
<br>
   We calculate here
      $$
     \Phi^*(t_1 G_1+\dots+t_n G_n)
       $$
<br>
\bigskip
<br>
   Suppose that
        $$
  S(x,l)=S(x)+\varphi(x)l+{1\over 2}A(x)l^2+
{1\over 6}T(x)l^3+\dots
        $$
Recall that for an arbitrary $g(y)$
       $$
\left[\Phi^*(g)\right](x)=g(y(x))+S(x,l(x))-y(x)l(x)\,,
       $$    
where  functions $y(x)=y_g(x), l(x)=l_g(x)$ 
can be defined from equations
       $$
y={\p S(x,l)\over \p l}\,,\quad
l={\p g(y)\over \p y}\,.
      $$
We have that for $g\equiv 0$, $\Phi^*(g)=S(x)$, and
             $$
y_0(x)={\p S(x,l)\over \p l}\big\vert
\big\vert\varphi_{l=0}=\varphi(x)\,.
             $$
<br>
We have also that
       $$
\Phi(g+\vare G)=\vare G(y_g(x))\,.
       $$
<br>
\bigskip
<br>
 {\bf 1}
Let $t\colon\quad t^2=0$. Then
      $$
\Phi(tG(y))=tG\left(y_0(x)\right)=tG(\varphi(x))\,.
    \eqno(1)
      $$
<br>
\medskip
<br>
{\bf 2}
Calculate 
$\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2$ assuming that for parameters $t_1,t_2$ 
         $$
t_1^2=t_2^2=0\,.
         $$
     $$
\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2=
 \Phi^*(t_1G_1)+t_2G_2\left(y_{t_1G_1}(x)\right)\,,
      $$
and
       $$
y(x)=y_{t_1G_1}(x)=\left(
\varphi(x)+A(x)l+{1\over 2}T(x)l^2\right)
\big\vert_{l=t_1G_1(y)}=
\varphi(x)+t_1A(x)G_1'(y)=
          $$
         $$
\varphi(x)+t_1A(x)G_1'(\varphi(x))\,,\quad
\hbox {since $t_1^2=0$}\,.
         $$
 Hence using (1) we come to 
     $$
\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2=
\Phi^*(t_1G_1) +\t_2G_2\left(y_{t_1G_1}(x)\right)=
t_1G_1(\varphi(x))+
t_2G_2\left(\varphi(x)+t_1A(x)G_1'(\varphi(x))\right)=
      $$
        $$
t_1G_1(y)+
t_2G_2(y)+t_2t_1G_2'(y)A(x)G_1'(y)
\big\vert_{y=\varphi(x)}\,.
\eqno (2)
      $$
<br>
\bigskip
<br>
{\bf 3}   Now calculate 
$\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2+t_3 G_3)$ assuming that
          $$
t_1^2=t_2^2=t_3^2=0\,.
      (3a)
          $$
    $$
\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2+t_3 G_3)=
\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2)+
 t_3G_3\left(y_{t_1G_1+t_2G_2}\right)\,.
      $$
We have  using nilpotency conditions (3a) that
         $$
y=y_{t_1 G_1+t_2 G_2}=(\varphi+Al+{1\over 2}Tl^2)
_{l=t_1G_1'+t_2 G_2'}=
         $$
         $$
\varphi+
A\left(t_1G'_1(y)+t_2G_2'(y)\right)+
{1\over 2}T\left(t_1G'_1(y)+t_2G_2'(y)\right)^2=
         $$
         $$
\varphi+
A\left(t_1G'_1(y=\varphi+\dots)+t_2G_2'(y=\varphi+\dots)
\right)+
{1\over 2}T\left(t_1G'_1(\varphi)+t_2G_2'(\varphi)\right)^2
         $$
         $$
y+
A\left(t_1G'_1(y+At_2G_2'(y))+
       t_2G_2'(y+At_1G_1'(y))
\right)+
      T(x)t_1t_2G_1'(y)G_2'(y)\big\vert_{y=\varphi(x)}=
         $$
$$
y+A(x)(t_1G_1'(y)+t_2G_2'(y))+
 t_1t_2A^2(x)\left(G_1'(y)G_2(y)'\right)'+
   T(x)t_1t_2G_1'(y)G_2'(y)
$$
and using (2) we come to
      $$
\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2+t_3 G_3)=
\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2)+
 t_3G_3\left(y_{t_1G_1+t_2G_2}\right)=
       $$
       $$
  t_1G_1(y)+
t_2G_2(y)+t_2t_1G_2'(y)A(x)G_1'(y)
        $$
      $$
t_3G_3\left(y+At_1G_1'(y)+t_2G_2'(y))+
 t_1t_2A^2\left(G_1'(y)G_2(y)'\right)'+
   Tt_1t_2G_1'(y)G_2'(y)
\right)
       $$
      $$
  t_1G_1(y)+
t_2G_2(y)+t_2t_1G_2'(y)A(x)G_1'(y)
\big\vert_{y=\varphi(x)}+t_3G_3(y)+
        $$
       $$
t_3G_3'\left(At_1G_1'(y)+t_2G_2'(y))+
 t_1t_2A^2\left(G_1'(y)G_2(y)'\right)'+
   Tt_1t_2G_1'(y)G_2'(y)\right)+
         $$
        $$
{1\over 2}t_3G_3''
  \left(At_1G_1'(y)+t_2G_2'(y)\right)^2
\big\vert_{y=\varphi(x)}\,.
      $$
Calculating we come to the answer:
<br>
       $$
\Phi^*(t_1G_1+t_2G_2+t_3 G_3)=
  t_1G_1(y)+
t_2G_2(y)+t_3G_3(y)+
       $$
       $$
  A(x)\left(
t_2t_1G_2'(y)G_1'(y)+
t_3t_1G_3'(y)G_1'(y)+
t_3t_2G_3'(y)G_2'(y)
     \right)
       +
        $$
       $$
 t_3t_2t_1A^2(x)\left(
G_1'(y) G_2'(y)G_3'(y)
     \right)'+T(x)t_3t_2t_1G_1'(y)G_2'(y)G_3(y)'
\big\vert_{y=\varphi(x)}
   \eqno (3)
      $$
<br>
 Is it possible to recognize $S(x,l)$ by
expansion of 
 $\Phi^*(t_mG_m)$?
