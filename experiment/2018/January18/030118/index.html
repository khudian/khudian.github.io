\centerline {\bf One approach}
Denote by $g_\k(y)$ a function $g=g_\k=\k\cdot \y=k_ay^a$
If $\Phi=\Phi_S$ is a thick morphism, then $$ \Phi^*(g_\k)(x)=S(x,\k) $$ and $$ y^a_\k(x)={\p S(x,l)\over \p l^a}\big\vert_{\l=\k} $$
Let $L$ be an arbitrary map such that $$ L(g+tH)-L(G)=tH(y_g(x)) $$ Study the relation between symmetricity of second variation and the condition $$ {\p y^a_\k\over \p k_b}- {\p y^b_\k\over \p k_a}=0 \tag{1} $$ If we show that symmetricity implies (1) then we can integrate $y^a_\k$.
