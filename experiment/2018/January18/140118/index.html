\centerline {\bf Killing metric and Killing }
Killing form on algebra $\G$ is $$ K(X,Y)= {\rm Tr} (ad_X ad_Y) \eqno (1) $$ Killing metric on algebra $\G$ is a metric, i.e. symmetric non-degenerate bilinear form on algebra $\G$, such that algebra acts on this form by antisymmetric operators, i.e. a form $(\,,\,)$ such that for an arbitrary vector $Z\in \G$ $$ (ad_Z X,Y)=-(X,ad_Z Y) \eqno (2) $$
Killing form obeys condition (2) and for semisimple Lie algebra a Killing metric is proportional to Killing form.
On Lie groj $G$ left invarint vector fields define canonical flat connection, but it si not symmetric connection. What about Levi-Civita for Killing metric?
Killing metric (arbitrary) on Lie algebra $\G$ define (pseudo)Riemannian metric on group via left-invariant vector fields: $$ =(X,Y) $$
\medskip
How to define connection compatible with this metric?
Answer: connection $\nabla$ such that $$ \nabla_{L_X} L_Y={1\over 2}L_[X,Y] $$ is Levi-Civita connection of Killing metric.
