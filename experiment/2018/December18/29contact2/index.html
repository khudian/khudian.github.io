Geometry of first order equation

Let $J^1M$ be a space of first jets of functions on manifold $M$. Cooridnates on $J^1M$ are $(p_i,q^j,u)$, where $q^j$ are coordinates on $M$. Jet of every function $u=u(x)$ has coordinates $\left(p_i={\p u\over \p x q^i},q^i,u\right)$.
Consider $\CC$ , the Cartan distribution of $2n$-dimensional planes in $J^1M$ defined by the form $\w=p_idq^i-du$ $$ \CC_\pb\subset T_\pb J^1M=\{ T_\pb (J^1M)\ni\X\colon\,\,\w(\X)=0 \}\,, $$ Vector field $$ M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\, \hbox{belongs to Cartan distribution $\CC$ if $A=p_iM^i$}\,. $$ $\CC$ is non-integrable distribution.
Consider differential equation, $$ \EE\colon F(p,q,u)=0\,. $$ Differential equation is sumbmanifold of codimension $1$ in the space $J^1(M)$.
The Cartan distribution $\CC$ of hyperplanes on $J^1M$ defines distribution $\CC(\EE)$ in $T\EE$: $$ \CC(E)=\CC\cap T\EE\,. $$ $$ \X= M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\in \CC(\EE)\,\, {\rm if}\,\, A=p_iM^i \& \left(M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\right)F(p,q,u)\big\vert_{F=0}=0\,. $$ The solution of differential equation (1) is the maximal integral of the distribution $\CC(\EE)$, which is the $n$-dimensional submanifold in $M$.
