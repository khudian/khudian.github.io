Contact vector field

Let $J^1M$ be a space of first jets of functions on manifold $M$. Cooridnates on $J^1M$ are $(p_i,q^j,u)$, where $q^j$ are coordinates on $M$. Jet of every function $u=u(x)$ has coordinates $\left(p_i={\p u\over \p x q^i},q^i,u\right)$.
Consider $\CC$ , the Cartan distribution of $2n$-dimensional planes in $J^1M$ defined by the form $\w=p_idq^i-du$ $$ \CC_\pb\subset T_\pb J^1M=\{ T_\pb (J^1M)\ni\X\colon\,\,\w(\X)=0 \}\,, $$ Vector field $$ M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\, \hbox{belongs to Cartan distribution $\CC$ if $A=p_iM^i$}\,. $$ $\CC$ is non-integrable distribution.
Consider differential equation, $$ \EE\colon F(p,q,u)=0\,. $$ Differential equation is sumbmanifold of codimension $1$.
The Cartan distribution $\CC$ of hyperplanes on $J^1M$ defines distribution $\CC(\EE)$ in $T\EE$: $$ \CC(E)=\CC\cap T\EE\,. $$ $$ \X= M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\in \CC(\EE)\,\, {\rm if}\,\, A=p_iM^i \& \left(M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}\right)F(p,q,u)\big\vert_{F=0}=0\,. $$
Definition 1 The vector field $\K$ in $2n+1$ is is an infinitesiaml symmetry of differential equation $\EE=0$ if it belongs to $\CC(\EE)$: $$ \L_\X \CC(\EE)=0 \tag{2 a} $$ In what follows we consider here mostly an empty differential equation. (We focus the attention on the equation in the next file tomorrow.)
Definition 2 The vector field $\K$ in $2n+1$ is called contact vector field if it is an infinitesimal symmetry of empty differential equation, i.e. if it preserves the Cartan distribution $\CC$ $$ \L_\X \CC=0 \tag{2 b} $$
Theorem There is one-one corrspondence between functions on $M$ and contact vector fields: $$ C^\infty(M)\ni F=F(p_i,q^j,u)\leftrightarrow \X_F $$ such that $$ F=\w(\X_F)\,, \, {\rm and} \quad \X_F= {\p F\over \p p_m}{\p\over \p q^m} - \left( {\p F\over \p q^m}+p_m{\p F\over \p u} \right) {\p\over \p p_m}+ \left( p_m{\p F\over \p p_m}- F \right) {\p\over \p u} $$

The proof of the Theorem follows from the
Lemma If $\X$ is contact vector field and $\w(\X)\equiv 0$ then $\X\equiv 0$.
This lemma implies that for every function $F$ there exists at most one contact vector field $\X_F$ such that $\w(\X_F)=F$.
On the other hand the vecot field (3)
i) is defined for an arbitrary smooth function $F$
ii) it evidently obyes the condition $\w(\X_F)=F$
iii) is contact vector field
Conditions ii) and iii) hold evidently. may be checked by direct calculations: $$ \w \left(\X_F\right)= \left(p_mdq^m-du\right)\left( {\p F\over \p p_m}{\p\over \p q^m} - \left( {\p F\over \p q^m}+p_m{\p F\over \p u} \right) {\p\over \p p_m}+ \left( p_m{\p F\over \p p_m}- F \right) {\p\over \p u} \right)= $$ $$ =p_m{\p F\over \p p_m}- \left( p_m{\p F\over \p p_m}- F \right)=F $$ and $$ \L_{\X_F}= d\left(\w {\rfloor}\X_F\right)+ d\w \left({\rfloor}\X_F\right)= d\left( \w\left(\X_F\right)\right)+ dp_m\wedge dq^m\left({\rfloor}\X_F\right)= $$ $$ =dF -{\p F\over \p p_m}dp_m -\left( {\p F\over \p q^m}+p_m{\p F\over \p u} \right)dq^m= {\p F\over \p u} \left(du-p_mdq^m\right)=F_u\w\,, $$ i.e. $\X_F$ preserves the Cartan distribution $\CC$.
It remians to prove the lemma.
Suppose that the vector field $\X=M^i{\p\over \p q^i}+ N_i{\p\over \p p_i}+A{\p\over \p u}$ is contact vector field $$ \L_\X \w=\l\w\,, \tag{3a} $$ and $$ \w(\X)=p_iM^i-A=0\,. \tag{3b} $$ Condition (3a) means that $$ \L_{\X}= d\left(\w {\rfloor}\X\right)+ d\w \left({\rfloor}\X\right)= d\left( \w\left(\X\right)\right)+ dp_m\wedge dq^m\left({\rfloor}\X\right)= $$ $$ 0-M^mdp_m-N_mdq^m=\l(pdq^m-du)\,. $$ Thus $\l\equiv 0$, and $M^m\equiv 0$, $N_m\equiv 0$ and due to equation (3b), $A\equiv 0$. Hence $\X\equiv 0$ $\finish$.
