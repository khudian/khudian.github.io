Gramm decomposition

Let $A\in GL(n,\R)$. Let $\{\e_i\}$ be a standard basis in $\R^n$ Consider $$ \ac_k=\e_r A_{rk}\,. $$ Now consider $$ \b_1=\ac_1,\quad \f_1={\b_1\over |\b_1|}\,, $$ $$ \b_2=\ac_2-\f_1\langle \f_1,\ac_2\rangle\,,\quad \f_2={\b_2\over |\b_2|}\, $$ and so on: $$ \b_3=\ac_3 -\f_1\langle \f_1,\ac_3\rangle -\f_2\langle \f_2,\ac_3\rangle\,, \quad \f_3={\b_3\over |\b_3|}\,, $$ $$ \b_4=\ac_4 -\f_1\langle \f_1,\ac_4\rangle -\f_2\langle \f_2,\ac_4\rangle -\f_3\langle \f_3,\ac_4\rangle\,, \quad \f_4={\b_4\over |\b_4|}\,, $$ $$ \b_5=\ac_5 -\f_1\langle \f_1,\ac_5\rangle -\f_2\langle \f_2,\ac_5\rangle -\f_3\langle \f_3,\ac_5\rangle -\f_4\langle \f_3,\ac_5\rangle\,, \quad \f_5={\b_5\over |\b_5|}\,, $$ $$ \dots $$ We see that $$ \f_i=\sum_{r\leq i} \ac_r T_{ri}\,, $$ i.e. $T$ is an upper-triangular matrix, and $\{\f_1,\dots,\f_n\}$ is also orthonormal basis: $\langle \f_i,\f_j\rangle=\delta_{ij}$ hence: $$ \f_i=\e_k P_{ki}, $$ where $P$ is orthogonal matrix. Thus we have that $$ \f_i=\ac_r T_{ri}=\e_m A_{mr}T_{ri}=\e_m P_{mi}\,, $$ i.e. $$ A=P\circ T^{-1} $$ We come to decomposition: $$ GL(n,\R)=O(n,\R)\times T(n,\R)\,. $$
