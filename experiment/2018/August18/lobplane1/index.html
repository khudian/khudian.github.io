\centerline {Some amasing formulas}
{\it These days I study Hilbnert Theorem of non-immersion of Lobachevsky plane in $\E^3$. To my surprise it has so many mathphysical applications. Here I just give some very preliminary results. (see the file in Etudes/Geometry/Lobachevsky)}
Consider on $\R^2$ Riemannian metric $$ G=dx^2+2cos\Theta(x,y)dxdy+dy^2 $$ This metric naturally appears in the proof of Hilbert Theorem.
Performing routine calculations we come to $$ \Gamma_{xx}^x=\Theta_x{\rm cotan\,}\Theta\,,\quad \Gamma_{xx}^y=-\Theta_x{1\over \sin \Theta}\,, $$ $$ \Gamma_{yy}^y=\Theta_y{\rm cotan\,}\Theta\,,\quad \Gamma_{yy}^x=-\Theta_y{1\over \sin \Theta}\,, $$ and $$ \Gamma^x_{xy}= \Gamma^x_{yx}= \Gamma^y_{xy} \Gamma^y_{yx}=0 $$
and $$ K={R\over 2}={R_{1212}\det g}={-\Theta_{xy}sin\Theta\over 1-\cos^2\Theta}= {-\Theta_{xy}\over \sin \Theta}\,, $$ and in particular the condition $$ \Theta_{xy}=\sin \Theta $$ means that this manifold is (whole?) Lobachesky plane (this function has to exist)
E.g. function $$ \theta={arctan }Ce^(x+y) $$
