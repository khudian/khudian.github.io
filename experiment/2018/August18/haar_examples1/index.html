\centerline {Examples of Haar measure}
{\bf Examples}
Group of affine transformations of $\R$:
$$ (a,b)| x>=ax+b $$ We have $$ (a,\a)\circ (b,\beta)|x>=(a,\a)|bx+\beta>= |a(bx+\beta)+\a>=(ab,a\beta+\a)|x>\,. $$ Thus $$ (a,\a)(b,\beta)=(ab,a\beta+\a) $$ Haar meausure $$ \mu(db,d\beta)=\omega= \omega(b,\beta)db\wedge d\beta $$
Left-invariance means that for an arbitrary element $(a,\a)$ $$ \omega (b,\beta)db\wedge d\beta= \omega(ab,a\beta+\a)d(ab)\wedge d(a\beta+\a)= \omega(ab,a\beta+\a)a^2db\wedge d\beta $$ Thus left-invariant measure is equal to $$ \omega={db\wedge d\beta\over b^2}\,. $$ and $$ \Phi(f)=\int f(b,\beta){dad\beta\over b^2}\,. $$
